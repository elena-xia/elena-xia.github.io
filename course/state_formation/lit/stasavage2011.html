Dependent variable (DV)

The main dependent variable is access to public credit by European states between 1250 and 1750. Stasavage operationalizes this in two ways. The first is the timing of when a state first established a long-term public debt, the date at which we first observe interest rates on long-term government loans for a given polity (p. 29 of Ch. 2). The second is the cost of borrowing, measured by the interest rate a state paid on its long-term debt (p. 38 of Ch. 2). Which states were able to borrow earlier, and which states borrowed at lower cost? argues that a city-state that had not already created a long-term debt would be expected to have a 43% chance of doing so in a given half-century, compared to only a 5% chance for a territorial state; and that a city-state would be estimated to pay an interest rate roughly two percentage points lower than a territorial state (p. 17 of Ch. 1).

主要因变量是 1250 年至 1750 年间欧洲各国获取公共信贷的能力。Stasavage 以两种方式将其操作化。第一是一国首次建立长期公债的时间，即我们首次观察到某一政体长期政府贷款利率的日期（第二章 p. 29）。第二是借款成本，以一国为其长期债务支付的利率来衡量（第二章 p. 38）。哪些国家能够更早借款，哪些国家能够以更低的成本借款？（Stasavage）论证：一个尚未建立长期债务的城市国家，在某一半个世纪内建立长期债务的概率预计为 43%，而领土国家仅为 5%；并且城市国家所支付的利率估计比领土国家低大约两个百分点（第一章 p. 17）。

Independent variable (IV)

The main independent variable is the type of polity, specifically the distinction between city-states and territorial states. Stasavage argues that the city-state versus territorial-state distinction is ultimately grounded in two deeper underlying conditions: (1) geographic scale, and (2) the composition of elite wealth (that is, whether the political elite was dominated by merchants holding liquid wealth or by landowners holding illiquid wealth) (p. 1 of Ch. 1). These two underlying factors jointly determined the form of political representation a polity could sustain, which in turn determined its access to credit. City-states were small, so the costs of regularly convening and monitoring a representative assembly were low; city-states were also dominated by merchants who themselves purchased public debt and who had direct control over city councils (pp. 1–2 of Ch. 1). Territorial states were large, making it costly to sustain active representative assemblies, and their political elites were dominated by landowners who had less interest in public credit (pp. 2, 8 of Ch. 1).

主要自变量是政体类型，具体而言是城市国家与领土国家之间的区分。Stasavage 论证，城市国家与领土国家的区分最终植根于两个更深层的基础条件：（1）地理规模，以及（2）精英财富的构成（即政治精英是由持有流动财富的商人主导，还是由持有非流动财富的地主主导）（第一章 p. 1）。这两个基础因素共同决定了一个政体能够维持的政治代表形式，而后者又决定了其获取信贷的能力。城市国家规模小，因此定期召集和监督代表议会的成本较低；城市国家还由商人主导，这些商人本身购买公债，并对市议会拥有直接控制权（第一章 pp. 1–2）。领土国家规模大，使得维持活跃的代表议会代价高昂，且其政治精英由对公共信贷兴趣较小的地主所主导（第一章 pp. 2, 8）。

Stateness

Stasavage treats the state as a political entity that must finance itself and whose survival depends on its ability to raise funds through taxation and borrowing (pp. 25–28 of Ch. 2). The key dimension of “stateness” is the capacity to borrow at low cost, which Stasavage treats as dependent on the credibility of repayment commitments, in turn depending on the structure of political institutions. A state with a representative assembly that can monitor and modify spending and debt is, in this framework, more “credible” as a borrower and thus more successful as a state in the fiscal-military competition of premodern Europe (p. 7 of Ch. 1).

Stasavage defines a “city-state” as a city that exerted a substantial degree of control over its economic and judicial affairs while also playing a role in organizing its own defense, with political rights restricted to inhabitants of the core city (p. 6 of Ch. 1). He includes fully autonomous cities like Venice and Florence as well as cities with more limited autonomy like Ghent /ɡɛnt/ and Bruges /bruːʒ/ to avoid selection bias. He notes that dropping the less autonomous cities from the sample actually strengthens his results (pp. 6, 33 of Ch. 1 and Ch. 2).

Stasavage 将国家视为一个必须为自身筹资、其存续取决于通过征税和借款筹集资金的能力的政治实体（第二章 pp. 25–28）。“国家性”的关键维度是以低成本借款的能力，Stasavage 认为这取决于偿还承诺的可信度，而后者又取决于政治制度的结构。在这一框架中，拥有能够监督和修改支出与债务的代表议会的国家，作为借款者更“可信”，因而在前现代欧洲的财政，军事竞争中作为国家更为成功（第一章 p. 7）。

Stasavage 将“城市国家”定义为：一座对其经济和司法事务施加相当程度控制、同时在组织自身防务方面发挥作用、且政治权利仅限于核心城市居民的城市（第一章 p. 6）。为避免选择偏差，他不仅纳入了像威尼斯和佛罗伦萨这样完全自治的城市，也纳入了像 Ghent（根特）和 Bruges（布鲁日）这样自治程度更有限的城市。他指出，将自治程度较低的城市从样本中剔除实际上会强化他的结果（第一章和第二章 pp. 6, 33）。

Core argument

Stasavage’s core argument: First, access to public credit was critically important for the survival and success of European states in an era of incessant warfare, because military expenditures often arose suddenly and could not be met by immediate increases in taxation alone (pp. 25–28 of Ch. 2). Second, in order for individuals to lend to a government, they needed assurance that the government would take policy actions consistent with eventual debt repayment. For example, that the executive would not divert resources to personal consumption or overly expensive military ventures (p. 7 of Ch. 1). Third, because it was infeasible for lenders to specify in advance a complete contract covering all contingencies, one solution was for the executive to concede a degree of control rights over future policy to lenders, via a representative assembly that could monitor and modify public expenditures.

The cost of exercising such a control right depended critically on geographic scale: in large territorial states, the costs of sending representatives to a distant assembly, of having the assembly meet frequently enough to monitor spending, and of constituents monitoring their own representatives, were all prohibitively high (p. 8 of Ch. 1). In small city-states, assemblies could be convened by something as simple as the ringing of a town bell (p. 14 of Ch. 1). The effectiveness of representation also depended on the composition of the political elite: Merchants held liquid wealth that could be quickly converted into government annuities; landowners held illiquid wealth. Merchants were therefore the natural purchasers of public debt. In city-states, merchants dominated the political elite and could use their control of city councils to ensure that taxes would be levied to service debt. In territorial states, landowners dominated assemblies and had less stake in ensuring debt repayment (pp. 15–16 of Ch. 1).

Even within the group of city-states, those with more oligarchical institutions where merchant elites formally reserved seats on city councils and representatives were selected by cooptation rather than election tended to have better access to credit than those with more open, democratic institutions (pp. 2, 13–14 of Ch. 1). This is because when popular pressures led to the inclusion of craft guild members on city councils, the new representatives were less likely to own public debt and more likely to bear the tax burden of debt servicing, creating incentives to default or reduce interest payments (pp. 2, 13, 18 of Ch. 1). Stasavage frames this as a trade-off: more oligarchical institutions could reinforce the credibility of debt repayment, but at the cost of broader political participation.

Stasavage 的核心论点：第一，在一个战争不断的时代，获取公共信贷对欧洲各国的存续与成功至关重要，因为军事支出往往突然产生，且无法仅靠即时增加税收来满足（第二章 pp. 25–28）。第二，为使个人愿意向政府放贷，他们需要确信政府将采取与最终偿债相一致的政策行动。例如，行政当局不会将资源挪用于个人消费或过于昂贵的军事冒险（第一章 p. 7）。第三，由于贷款人事先拟定一份涵盖所有偶发情况的完整契约是不可行的，一种解决办法是行政当局向贷款人让渡对未来政策的一定程度的控制权，途径是设立一个能够监督和修改公共支出的代表议会。

行使这种控制权的成本关键取决于地理规模：在大型领土国家中，派遣代表前往遥远议会的成本、让议会频繁开会以监督支出的成本，以及选民监督自己代表的成本，都高得令人望而却步（第一章 p. 8）。在小型城市国家中，仅凭敲响城镇钟声这样简单的方式就能召集议会（第一章 p. 14）。代表制的有效性还取决于政治精英的构成：商人持有可迅速转换为政府年金的流动财富；地主持有非流动财富。因此商人是公债的天然购买者。在城市国家中，商人主导政治精英，能够利用其对市议会的控制来确保征税以偿付债务。在领土国家中，地主主导议会，对确保偿债的利害关系较小（第一章 pp. 15–16）。

即便在城市国家群体内部，那些制度更为寡头化、商人精英正式保留市议会席位、代表通过增补选任（cooptation）而非选举产生的城市，往往比那些制度更开放、更民主的城市拥有更好的信贷获取能力（第一章 pp. 2, 13–14）。这是因为当民众压力导致行会手工业者被纳入市议会时，新代表拥有公债的可能性更小，而承担偿债税负的可能性更大，从而产生了违约或削减利息支付的激励（第一章 pp. 2, 13, 18）。Stasavage 将此表述为一种权衡：更寡头化的制度能够强化偿债承诺的可信度，但以更广泛的政治参与为代价。

Data & methods

Data

Stasavage constructs an original dataset covering 31 European states (19 city-states and 12 territorial states) over five centuries from 1250 to 1750 (p. 16 of Ch. 1). The unit of analysis is the state-half-century (i.e., each state is observed across successive 50-year periods).

On the dependent variable (public credit): The data on the timing of the first long-term debt come from a systematic review of secondary sources cited by Stephan Epstein /ˈstɛfən ˈɛpstaɪn/ (2000), supplemented by numerous additional sources. Stasavage substantially augmented Epstein’s dataset by adding new polities, extending the time coverage, and refining measurement (pp. 29–30 of Ch. 2). Interest rate data are based on nominal rates at issue when available, and on a fiscal proxy (the ratio of annual debt service to total debt stock) when they are not. He notes that across the sample, interest rates measured by the fiscal proxy method were not significantly different from nominal rates (p. 38 of Ch. 2). He also distinguishes between types of debt instruments: life annuities versus perpetual annuities, forced loans versus voluntary loans (p. 16 of Ch. 1).

On the independent variables (political representation): For each of the 31 states and each 50-year period, Stasavage codes four binary indicators from secondary historical sources: (1) whether a representative assembly existed; (2) whether the assembly had veto power over taxes; (3) whether the assembly played a direct role in administering tax collection; and (4) whether the assembly directly controlled spending and debt decisions (pp. 55–57 of Ch. 3). He also codes the frequency with which assemblies met (on a scale from 0 to 1, where 1 means at least annually), and the degree of merchant representation in the assembly (pp. 62, 65–66 of Ch. 3). Geographic scale is measured by the natural log of a polity’s area in square kilometers, and alternatively by a “polity scale” measure that captures the log distance the average urban inhabitant would need to travel to reach the capital (pp. 66–67 of Ch. 3). Sources for the representation data are drawn from an extensive set of secondary works, including the multi-volume studies published by the Société Jean Bodin /soʊsjeˈteɪ ʒɑːn boʊˈdæ̃/ and the International Commission for the History of Representative and Parliamentary Institutions, as listed in Table 3.1 (pp. 54–56 of Ch. 3).

Controls and additional variables: Stasavage uses urbanization rates as a proxy for economic and commercial development. He also employs data on returns to land rents across five European regions (England, France, Flanders /ˈflændərz/, Germany, Italy) from Clark (1988) to construct a measure of the “risk premium” paid by governments, namely the difference between observed government borrowing rates and prevailing private land-rent returns (pp. 40–41 of Ch. 2). Revenue volatility data are also compiled for a smaller subset of states to evaluate the possibility that city-states simply had more stable revenues (pp. 45–46 of Ch. 2).

Stasavage 构建了一个原创数据集，涵盖 1250 年至 1750 年五个世纪间的 31 个欧洲国家（19 个城市国家和 12 个领土国家）（第一章 p. 16）。分析单位是国家，半世纪（即每个国家在连续的 50 年时段内被观察）。

关于因变量（公共信贷）：首次长期债务时间的数据来自对 Stephan Epstein（2000）所引二手来源的系统梳理，并辅以大量额外来源。Stasavage 大幅扩充了 Epstein 的数据集，增添了新的政体、延长了时间覆盖范围并改进了测量（第二章 pp. 29–30）。利率数据在有条件时以发行时的名义利率为基础，在没有条件时则以一个财政代理指标（年度偿债额与债务总存量之比）为基础。他指出，在整个样本中，以财政代理法测得的利率与名义利率没有显著差异（第二章 p. 38）。他还区分了债务工具的类型：终身年金与永久年金、强制贷款与自愿贷款（第一章 p. 16）。

关于自变量（政治代表制）：对 31 个国家中的每一个及每一个 50 年时段，Stasavage 从二手历史来源编码四个二元指标：（1）是否存在代表议会；（2）议会是否对税收拥有否决权；（3）议会是否在管理征税方面发挥直接作用；以及（4）议会是否直接控制支出和债务决策（第三章 pp. 55–57）。他还编码了议会开会的频率（以 0 到 1 的量表衡量，1 表示至少每年一次），以及议会中商人代表的程度（第三章 pp. 62, 65–66）。地理规模以政体面积（平方公里）的自然对数来衡量，或者以一种“政体规模”度量来衡量，后者捕捉的是普通城市居民到达首都所需行进距离的对数（第三章 pp. 66–67）。代表制数据的来源取自大量二手著作，包括 Société Jean Bodin 和国际代表制与议会制度史委员会出版的多卷本研究，如表 3.1 所列（第三章 pp. 54–56）。

控制变量与其他变量：Stasavage 使用城市化率作为经济和商业发展的代理指标。他还利用 Clark（1988）关于五个欧洲地区（英格兰、法国、Flanders（佛兰德斯）、德国、意大利）土地租金回报的数据，构建了一个政府所支付“风险溢价”的度量，即观察到的政府借款利率与当时私人土地租金回报之间的差额（第二章 pp. 40–41）。他还为一个较小的国家子集编制了收入波动数据，以评估城市国家是否只是拥有更稳定收入的可能性（第二章 pp. 45–46）。

Methods

In Chapters 1–3, Stasavage primarily presents descriptive evidence and preliminary regression analysis.

Descriptive comparisons: Stasavage uses histograms, time-series plots, and simple summary statistics to establish the basic empirical patterns. Figure 2.1 shows that city-states created long-term debts much earlier than territorial states. Figure 2.2 plots all interest rate observations over time, revealing both a secular decline and substantial variation, with city-states consistently borrowing at lower rates. Figure 2.4 plots the difference between government borrowing rates and private land-rent returns to show that city-states paid lower risk premiums (pp. 31–32, 39–41 of Ch. 2).

OLS regressions on assembly meeting frequency: In Chapter 3, Table 3.2, Stasavage reports OLS regressions of assembly meeting frequency on geographic scale measures, with period dummies included. He estimates this both in levels and in first differences. The first-difference specification is important because it controls for all time-invariant unobserved factors specific to each polity. Using the “polity scale” measure, geographic scale is statistically significant both in levels and in first differences (pp. 67–68 of Ch. 3). Standard errors are clustered to allow for within-polity correlation.

Key assumptions for validity: (1) The coding of institutional characteristics from secondary sources must be reasonably accurate and not systematically biased in favor of the hypothesis. Stasavage acknowledges possible errors but argues the patterns are too large to be artifacts of coding choices (p. 68 of Ch. 3). (2) The interest rate data must be comparable across states and time periods despite differences in measurement methods, and he controls for instrument type and measurement method in Chapter 4’s regressions (p. 16 of Ch. 1). (3) For the first-difference regressions to identify a causal effect of geographic scale on meeting frequency, changes in geographic scale must not be driven by the same factors that also independently cause changes in meeting frequency. Because geographic scale is highly persistent (R² = 0.93 in a regression on polity dummies), first differencing is a very demanding test and the log-area variable loses significance, but the polity-scale variable remains significant (p. 68 of Ch. 3). (4) For the broader causal claim that city-states had better credit access because of their representative institutions, one must rule out the possibility that city-states simply emerged in more economically developed regions, a concern Stasavage addresses with instrumental variables in Chapter 5 using distance from the Carolingian /ˌkærəˈlɪndʒɪən/ partition line at the Treaty of Meersen /ˈmɪərsən/ in 870 (pp. 19–20 of Ch. 1).

在第一至三章中，Stasavage 主要呈现描述性证据和初步回归分析。

描述性比较：Stasavage 使用直方图、时间序列图和简单的汇总统计来确立基本的经验模式。图 2.1 显示城市国家远早于领土国家建立长期债务。图 2.2 绘制了随时间变化的所有利率观测值，揭示出既有长期下降趋势又有相当大的差异，其中城市国家始终以较低利率借款。图 2.4 绘制了政府借款利率与私人土地租金回报之间的差额，以表明城市国家支付较低的风险溢价（第二章 pp. 31–32, 39–41）。

关于议会开会频率的 OLS 回归：在第三章表 3.2 中，Stasavage 报告了议会开会频率对地理规模度量的 OLS 回归，并纳入了时段虚拟变量。他既以水平值也以一阶差分进行估计。一阶差分设定很重要，因为它控制了每个政体特有的所有不随时间变化的未观测因素。使用“政体规模”度量时，地理规模在水平值和一阶差分中都具有统计显著性（第三章 pp. 67–68）。标准误经过聚类处理，以允许政体内部的相关性。

有效性的关键假设：（1）从二手来源对制度特征的编码必须相当准确，且不存在系统性地偏向该假设的偏差。Stasavage 承认可能存在错误，但论证这些模式过于显著，不可能是编码选择的人为产物（第三章 p. 68）。（2）利率数据尽管测量方法有别，仍必须在跨国家和跨时段之间可比，他在第四章的回归中控制了工具类型和测量方法（第一章 p. 16）。（3）为使一阶差分回归能够识别地理规模对开会频率的因果效应，地理规模的变化必须不是由那些同时独立地导致开会频率变化的相同因素所驱动。由于地理规模高度持久（对政体虚拟变量回归的 R² = 0.93），一阶差分是一个非常严苛的检验，对数面积变量失去了显著性，但政体规模变量仍然显著（第三章 p. 68）。（4）对于城市国家因其代表制度而拥有更好信贷获取能力这一更宽泛的因果论断，必须排除城市国家只是恰好出现在经济更发达地区的可能性，Stasavage 在第五章以工具变量来处理这一顾虑，使用的是到公元 870 年 Meersen 条约（Treaty of Meersen）所划加洛林（Carolingian）分界线的距离（第一章 pp. 19–20）。

Class Notes

Week 9 lecture: discussion of this reading

Stasavage engages explicitly with North & Weingast and makes several important advances:

Advance 1, the centrality of debt: Stasavage emphasizes the government’s ability to convert future revenue into present resources through debt. This matters for war: a ruler needs money now, not a slowly growing revenue stream. It also gives Tilly’s “war makes states” hypothesis a concrete mechanism that goes beyond “wealth”: city-states, despite their small size, performed well militarily through their capacity to access credit.

Advance 2, endogenizing P: Stasavage’s core theoretical contribution is to make the exogenous institutional penalty parameter

\(P\) in the North & Weingast model endogenous (endogenizes \(P\)). In his theory:

\[ P=f(A,M). \]

\(A\) is alignment: whether the interests of the elites in the assembly align with those of the creditors (lenders). When a merchant oligarchy controls the assembly, \(A\) is high; when a group whose interests are close to the ruler’s controls the assembly, \(A\) is low. \(M\) is monitoring: the elites’ actual capacity to carry out the penalty, which is heavily influenced by geographic scale.

Stasavage draws on an earlier empirical article of his own to illustrate the importance of alignment. The instructor described the key finding: after the Glorious Revolution, when the Whigs, who represented merchant interests, controlled Parliament, government bond interest rates kept falling, just as the commitment problem model predicts. But in the decades after the Whigs lost control of Parliament, rates rose again.

The instructor’s reading: this shows that P was not permanently fixed by the Glorious Revolution. P needs a credible political coalition to sustain it. When the Whigs were in power, if the king tried to expropriate merchants’ wealth, the threat of beheading was credible; but when the Whigs did not control Parliament, the threat of relying on a minority of Whigs to raise an army and overthrow the king was much less credible.

Core logic: in small city-states, the cost of assembling and monitoring is extremely low; Stasavage describes in his case studies a mechanism “like a neighborhood alarm system”: ring the bell, the king is about to default, and everyone shows up to the meeting with pitchforks. In large territorial states, by contrast, representatives must travel long distances, the journey is dangerous, and the information environment is already completely different by the time they set out versus when they reach the capital. Student contribution (Speaker 6): asked whether it is scale alone that affects the capacity to coordinate, or more the homogeneity and cohesion of interest among the merchant class.

Instructor’s response: conceded that the monitoring dimension can go beyond pure geographic scale; for example, a small but rugged country may be harder to coordinate than a large one with navigable rivers. He also noted that other organizational factors can facilitate coordination, such as guilds that make people meet regularly. Stasavage chose geographic scale as the core variable partly because “geography is kind of exogenous,” which helps his causal argument.

Stasavage provides evidence on several dimensions:

1. Scale and the existence of assemblies: polities with smaller jurisdictions are more likely to have assemblies, and those assemblies have greater powers. Even when large states have assemblies, their function is usually limited to approving or vetoing new taxes.

2. Alignment and the cost of credit: cities that have an assembly controlled by a merchant oligarchy obtain the cheapest credit, compared with cities that have no assembly or whose assembly is controlled by non-merchant groups.

Both the instructor and students stressed a key point: this is not an argument about democracy. It is about credible bargaining between taxpayers and the ruler. In fact, Stasavage’s empirical findings run counter to the intuition that “democracy and access to credit must be positively correlated”: assemblies controlled by oligarchs rather than by the broad population are more effective at obtaining cheap credit. Student contribution (Speaker 2): noted that Stasavage remedies an important shortcoming of North & Weingast: they do not distinguish “having an institution” from “having an effective institution.” Stasavage’s argument is that institutions need substance as well as form. This connects directly to later work on state capacity and helps explain why simply transplanting institutional forms in the Global South often does not work.

Instructor’s assessment: warmly praised this connection and noted it can be traced back to Levi’s concept of “relative bargaining power”; if we understand it (with a slight stretch) as being about what institutions substantively do rather than formal institutions, then Levi’s and Stasavage’s arguments come very close. Stasavage is a model of carving a more precise argument out of the “raw material” Levi provided.

Stasavage 明确地与 North & Weingast 对话，做出了几个重要推进：

推进1，债务的核心地位：Stasavage 强调的不仅仅是增加收入，而是政府将未来收入转化为当下资源的能力（convert future revenue into present resources via debt）。这对战争至关重要，你需要的是今天的钱来生存，而不是缓慢增长的收入流。这也为 Tilly 的“战争制造国家”假说提供了一个超越“财富”的具体机制，城市国家尽管规模小，却通过信贷获取能力在军事上表现出色。

推进2，内生化 P：Stasavage 的核心理论贡献是将 North & Weingast 模型中外生的制度惩罚参数

\(P\) 内生化（endogenizes \(P\)）。在他的理论中：

\[ P=f(A,M). \]

\(A\) 是利益一致性（Alignment）：议会中的精英与债权人（出借人）的利益是否一致。当商人寡头控制议会时，\(A\) 高；当与统治者利益接近的群体控制议会时，\(A\) 低。\(M\) 是监控能力（Monitoring）：精英实际执行惩罚的能力，很大程度上受到地理规模的影响。

Stasavage 利用了他自己提到的一篇更早的实证文章来说明利益一致性的重要性。老师描述了关键发现：在光荣革命后，当代表商人利益的辉格党（Whigs）控制议会时，政府债券利率持续下降，正如承诺问题模型所预测。但当辉格党失去议会控制权后几十年，利率重新上升。

老师的解读：这证明了 P 并非因光荣革命而永久固定。P 需要一个可信的政治联盟（credible political coalition）来支撑。当辉格党在位时，如果国王试图征用商人的财富，砍头的威胁是可信的；但当辉格党不控制议会时，依靠少数派辉格党来组织军队推翻国王的威胁就不那么可信了。

核心逻辑：在小型城市国家中，集会和监控成本极低，Stasavage 在案例研究中描述了一种“类似邻里报警系统”的机制：敲响钟声，国王要赖账了，大家带上干草叉来开会。而在大型领土国家中，代表需要长途跋涉，旅途危险，信息环境在出发时和到达首都时已经完全不同。学生贡献（Speaker 6）：质问是否仅仅是规模影响了协调能力，还是更在于商人阶层的同质性和利益凝聚力。

老师回应：承认监控维度可以超越纯粹的地理规模，例如，一个小但地形崎岖的国家可能比一个大但拥有可通航河流的国家更难协调。他还指出可以有其他组织性因素促进协调，比如行业协会让人们定期聚会。Stasavage 选择地理规模作为核心变量，部分原因在于“地理是外生的”（geography is kind of exogenous），这有助于其因果论证。

Stasavage 提供了多层面的证据：

1. 规模与议会存在：管辖范围越小的政体越可能拥有议会，且议会权力越大。大国即使有议会，其功能通常仅限于批准或否决新税。

2. 利益一致性与信贷成本：拥有议会且由商人寡头控制的城市获得最便宜的信贷，相较于无议会的城市或议会由非商人群体控制的城市。

老师和学生都强调了一个关键点：这不是关于民主的论述。这是关于纳税人与统治者之间的可信议价。事实上，Stasavage 的实证发现与“民主与信贷获取必然正相关”的直觉相悖，由寡头而非广大人口控制的议会在获取便宜信贷方面更有效。学生贡献（Speaker 2）：指出 Stasavage 弥补了 North & Weingast 的一个重要不足，即后者不区分“拥有制度”与“拥有有效制度”。Stasavage 的整个论点是制度需要有实质内容（substance），而非仅有形式（form），这与后来关于“国家能力”（state capacity）的文献直接相关，也解释了为何在全球南方简单移植制度形式往往无效。

老师评价：高度赞赏这一联系，并指出它可以追溯到 Levi 的“相对议价能力”概念，如果我们（稍作延伸地）将其理解为关乎制度实质上做了什么（what institutions substantively do），而非形式制度，那么 Levi 和 Stasavage 的论述就非常接近。Stasavage 是从 Levi 提供的“原材料”中雕刻出更精确论证的典范。

Shared week context: course framing & four-reading synthesis

This week compares four classic or synthetic accounts of limited government, fiscal contracts, and tax compliance. The shared premise is that a ruler wants revenue, but sustainable revenue requires solving constraints that pure coercion cannot cheaply solve.

\[ \max R=(\text{tax revenue})-(\text{collection costs}) \quad\text{s.t.}\quad \{\text{bargaining power},\text{ transaction costs},\text{ discount factor}\}. \]

Levi (1988)

Starts with the ruler as revenue maximizer, then adds quasi-voluntary compliance. Compliance is more than coercion; it combines perceived fairness, coordination among taxpayers, and the ruler's credibility.

North & Weingast (1989)

Turns the problem into a commitment game. Institutions impose a penalty \(P\) on exploitation, making provision credible when \(P>G\). Levi's bargaining power can be read as one source of \(P\).

Stasavage (2011)

Endogenizes the penalty parameter: \(P=f(A,M)\), where \(A\) is alignment between creditors and representatives and \(M\) is monitoring capacity. The effect is strongest in small, creditor-controlled polities.

Gehlbach (2008)

Flips the fiscal-contract logic. The government may support taxable sectors without voluntary compliance: it feeds sectors that are easy to tax. Taxability works like a transaction-cost/legibility condition.

The theoretical map is therefore not a simple sequence from coercion to legitimacy. It is a set of alternative ways to make revenue extraction sustainable: quasi-voluntary compliance, institutional penalties, endogenous representative constraints, or coercive support for legible taxable sectors.

\[ \frac{T-G}{1-\beta}>T\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \beta>\frac{G}{T}. \]

The repeated-game result shows where Levi and Olson connect to North and Weingast: patient rulers may sustain cooperation even without formal institutions, but when time horizons shorten, institutional penalties become more important.

本周四篇文献共同讨论有限政府、财政契约与税收合规。共同前提是：统治者想要收入，但可持续收入不能只靠昂贵的强制，还必须解决若干约束。

\[ \max R=(\text{税收收入})-(\text{征税成本}) \quad\text{s.t.}\quad \{\text{议价能力},\text{ 交易成本},\text{ 贴现因子}\}. \]

Levi (1988)

以“统治者最大化收入”为起点，随后加入准自愿合规。合规不只是强制，而是由公平感、纳税人之间的协调、以及统治者可信承诺共同支撑。

North & Weingast (1989)

将问题转写为承诺博弈。制度对掠夺施加惩罚 \(P\)，当 \(P>G\) 时，提供公共品才可信。Levi 的议价能力可以理解为 \(P\) 的来源之一。

Stasavage (2011)

将惩罚参数内生化：\(P=f(A,M)\)，其中 \(A\) 是债权人与代表者的利益一致性，\(M\) 是监控能力。小规模、债权人控制议会的政体中效果最强。

Gehlbach (2008)

翻转财政契约逻辑。政府可以在没有自愿合规的情况下扶持可税部门：它“喂养”容易征税的部门。可税性相当于一种交易成本/可读性条件。

因此，这张理论图谱不是从强制走向合法性的单线进步史，而是展示几种让汲取可持续的替代路径：准自愿合规、制度性惩罚、内生代表约束，或对可读可税部门的强制性扶持。

\[ \frac{T-G}{1-\beta}>T\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \beta>\frac{G}{T}. \]

重复博弈结果说明 Levi/Olson 与 North-Weingast 的连接点：有耐心的统治者即使没有正式制度，也可能维持合作；但当时间视野变短，制度性惩罚就变得更关键。
