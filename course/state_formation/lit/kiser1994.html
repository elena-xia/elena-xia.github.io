Edgar Kiser (1994), “Markets and Hierarchies in Early Modern Tax Systems: A Principal-Agent Analysis.”凯泽（1994）《早期现代税收体系中的市场与科层：一种委托-代理分析》。

Deductive theoryComparative history

Dependent variable (DV)

The main dependent variable is the structure of tax collection systems in early modern states, specifically, the form of the agency relation between rulers and their tax collectors. This variable takes three main values: (1) tax farming (a market form), in which independent entrepreneurs purchase the right to collect a particular tax in a given area by paying the ruler a fixed sum determined by auction, retaining any surplus collected above that price as profit (p. 284–285); (2) state administration (a hierarchical form), in which rulers employ permanent, salaried state officials to collect taxes under direct monitoring and sanctioning (p. 285); and (3) intermediate or proportional payment forms, in which agents receive a proportion of the revenue they collect, thereby splitting residual claimancy between principal and agent (p. 290). Kiser examines variation in this dependent variable along three dimensions: across different types of taxes (direct versus indirect), across different countries (England /ˈɪŋɡlənd/, France /fræns/, Spain /speɪn/, Prussia /ˈprʌʃə/, Sweden /ˈswiːdən/, China /ˈtʃaɪnə/, Russia /ˈrʌʃə/, the Ottoman Empire /ˈɒtəmən ˈɛmpaɪər/, and the Netherlands /ˈnɛðərləndz/), and over time within the early modern period (roughly the 13th through 18th centuries) (p. 285, 295).

主要因变量是早期现代国家中的税收征集制度结构，具体来说，是统治者与税收征集者之间代理关系的形式。该变量有三种主要取值：（1）包税制（市场形式），即独立企业家通过拍卖方式向统治者支付一笔固定金额，购买在特定区域征收特定税种的权利，征收金额超出购买价格的部分作为利润归包税人所有（第284–285页）；（2）国家行政征收（科层形式），即统治者雇佣固定薪资的永久官员来征收税款，并对其进行直接监督和惩罚（第285页）；（3）中间形式或比例支付形式，即代理人获得其征收收入的一定比例作为报酬，从而在委托人和代理人之间分割剩余索取权（第290页）。凯泽沿三个维度考察该因变量的变异：不同税种之间（直接税与间接税）、不同国家之间（英格兰 /ˈɪŋɡlənd/、法兰西 /fræns/、西班牙 /speɪn/、普鲁士 /ˈprʌʃə/、瑞典 /ˈswiːdən/、中国 /ˈtʃaɪnə/、俄罗斯 /ˈrʌʃə/、奥斯曼帝国 /ˈɒtəmən ˈɛmpaɪər/ 和尼德兰 /ˈnɛðərləndz/），以及早期现代时期（大约13至18世纪）的时间变化（第285、295页）。

Independent variable (IV)

The main independent variable is the control capacity of rulers, that is, the structural ability of rulers (as principals) to monitor and sanction their tax-collecting agents. This control capacity is itself shaped by several structural factors: (1) the level of development of technologies of control, including communications, transportation, and record-keeping systems, which determine how effectively rulers can oversee agents operating at a distance (p. 290, 293–294); (2) the size of the country, since larger territories and geographical obstacles reduce rulers’ ability to monitor agents (p. 290); (3) the nature of the tax being collected, whether the taxable asset is mobile, variable over time, or difficult to measure, which determines how hard it is for rulers to detect corruption and evasion by agents and taxpayers (p. 291–292); and (4) the autonomy of the ruler, meaning the extent to which rulers are constrained by political institutions such as parliaments, which affects both the transaction costs of creating tax farming contracts and the type of corruption that is most damaging (p. 291). When control capacities are low, rulers will prefer market forms (tax farming); when they are high, rulers will prefer hierarchical forms (state administration) (p. 290).

主要自变量是统治者的控制能力，即统治者作为委托人监督和惩罚其税收征集代理人的结构性能力。该控制能力本身受到若干结构性因素的塑造：（1）控制技术的发展水平，包括通讯、交通运输和档案记录体系，它们决定了统治者能多有效地监控在远方运作的代理人（第290、293–294页）；（2）国家的规模，因为更大的领土和地理障碍降低了统治者监控代理人的能力（第290页）；（3）所征税种的性质，应税资产是否具有流动性、是否随时间波动、是否难以度量，这决定了统治者发现代理人和纳税人腐败与逃税行为的难度（第291–292页）；（4）统治者的自主性，即统治者在多大程度上受到议会等政治制度的约束，这既影响创建包税合同的交易成本，也影响何种类型的腐败最具危害性（第291页）。当控制能力低时，统治者倾向于选择市场形式（包税制）；当控制能力高时，统治者倾向于选择科层形式（国家行政征收）（第290页）。

Stateness

Kiser conceptualizes the state primarily as a revenue-extracting organization headed by a ruler who seeks to maximize net tax revenue (defined as total tax collected minus the costs of collection, including corruption) to maintain power and maximize personal wealth (p. 285, 290). State building in this framework is understood as the gradual transition from market-based (tax farming) to hierarchical (bureaucratic state administration) forms of tax collection, a transition driven by the development of control technologies that make direct bureaucratic management increasingly efficient relative to contracting with independent entrepreneurs (p. 293–294). The state is not measured through a single index but rather through the observable organizational form of its fiscal apparatus: the proportion of total revenue collected via tax farms versus state officials, the degree of consolidation of tax farms, and the level of bureaucratic development in tax collection (salaries, monitoring procedures, merit-based recruitment) (p. 295, 298, 303). Kiser does not adopt a Weberian ideal-type of the state as a starting point, but rather treats the actual, imperfect hierarchical structures available to rulers as the relevant comparison to market alternatives. The key insight is that state building in the fiscal domain was not a linear march toward bureaucratization; instead, rulers frequently switched back and forth between tax farming and state administration as structural conditions changed (p. 298).

凯泽主要将国家概念化为一个以统治者为首的资源汲取组织，统治者寻求最大化净税收收入（定义为税收总额减去征收成本，包括腐败损失），以维持权力和最大化个人财富（第285、290页）。在这一框架中，国家建设被理解为税收征集形式从市场化（包税制）向科层化（官僚国家行政征收）的逐步转型，这一转型是由控制技术的发展所驱动的，这些技术使直接的官僚管理相对于与独立企业家签约变得越来越高效（第293–294页）。国家不是通过单一指标来测量的，而是通过其财政机构的可观察组织形式来衡量的：包税收入占总收入的比例、包税场的整合程度，以及税收征集中的官僚化发展水平（薪资、监督程序、基于绩效的招聘等）（第295、298、303页）。凯泽没有采用韦伯式的国家理想类型作为起点，而是将统治者实际可用的、不完美的科层结构作为与市场替代方案进行比较的参照。其关键洞见是，财政领域的国家建设并非线性地走向官僚化；相反，随着结构条件的变化，统治者频繁地在包税制和国家行政征收之间来回切换（第298页）。

Core argument

Kiser’s core argument is that the structure of early modern tax systems was determined by efficiency considerations rooted in the control capacities of rulers. Rulers were rational wealth-maximizers who attempted to choose the form of tax collection, tax farming, state administration, or an intermediate proportional-payment form, that would produce the highest net revenue (p. 285). The choice between these forms was fundamentally a principal-agent problem: since tax collectors (agents) had interests divergent from those of rulers (principals), rulers had to find ways to control agent behavior (p. 290). When rulers could effectively monitor and sanction their agents, that is, when control capacities were high, they chose state administration (hierarchy), because paying agents fixed salaries and directly overseeing their work minimized the share of revenue lost to agents’ profits, corruption, or shirking. When rulers could not effectively control their agents, that is, when control capacities were low, they chose tax farming (market relations), because making the agent the residual claimant gave the agent a strong self-interest in maximizing tax collection, thereby reducing the need for direct monitoring (p. 290). This argument challenges the prevailing scholarly consensus that tax farming was inherently inefficient and was used only because rulers needed loans or wanted predictable revenue. Kiser contends instead that tax farming was often the most efficient system available given the structural constraints rulers faced, and that tax farming organizations actually employed better monitoring and control techniques than the state bureaucracies of the same period (p. 294, 302–303).

凯泽的核心论点是，早期现代税收体系的结构是由根植于统治者控制能力的效率考量所决定的。统治者是理性的财富最大化者，他们试图选择能产生最高净收入的税收征集形式，包税制、国家行政征收或中间性的比例支付形式（第285页）。这些形式之间的选择本质上是一个委托-代理问题：由于税收征集者（代理人）的利益与统治者（委托人）的利益存在分歧，统治者必须找到控制代理人行为的方式（第290页）。当统治者能有效监督和惩罚其代理人，即控制能力高时，他们选择国家行政征收（科层制），因为向代理人支付固定薪资并直接监督其工作，可以最小化因代理人利润、腐败或偷懒而造成的收入损失。当统治者不能有效控制其代理人，即控制能力低时，他们选择包税制（市场关系），因为让代理人成为剩余索取者，使其产生了最大化税收征集的强烈自利动机，从而减少了对直接监督的需求（第290页）。这一论点挑战了学界流行的共识，即包税制天然低效、仅因统治者需要贷款或希望获得可预测收入才被采用。凯泽则认为，鉴于统治者面临的结构性约束，包税制往往是可用的最高效体系，而且包税组织实际上采用了比同时期国家官僚机构更好的监控和控制技术（第294、302–303页）。

Mechanisms

Kiser proposes several interconnected mechanisms through which control capacity shapes the choice of tax collection system:

(1) The incentive-alignment mechanism. In state administration, agents on fixed salaries have weak incentives to maximize revenue collection; their income does not depend on how much they collect. This creates classic shirking and corruption problems: officials may accept bribes from taxpayers in exchange for underassessing taxable assets, or they may embezzle collected funds. Since the principal (ruler) bears the residual risk, the ruler must invest heavily in monitoring. In tax farming, by contrast, the agent is the residual claimant, everything collected above the fixed purchase price is the farmer’s profit. This creates a powerful self-interest in maximizing collection and, critically, in controlling corruption among the farmer’s own subordinate agents, because corruption directly reduces the farmer’s income (p. 290, 294).

(2) The monitoring-difficulty mechanism. The nature of the taxable asset determines how difficult it is for rulers to monitor both their agents and taxpayers. When taxable assets are mobile (e.g., traded commodities subject to customs duties), variable over time (e.g., fluctuating trade volumes), and difficult to measure (e.g., the value of goods in transit), it is extremely hard for rulers to determine whether low revenue reflects honest collection of a genuinely low tax base, agent shirking, agent corruption (taking bribes to underassess), or taxpayer evasion. Under these conditions of high information asymmetry, making the agent the residual claimant through tax farming is the most efficient solution, because it shifts the measurement problem to the agent who has the strongest incentive to solve it. Conversely, when assets are immobile, stable, and easily measured (e.g., land), state administration becomes feasible because rulers can verify agent performance through relatively simple cross-checks such as repeating land surveys (p. 291–293).

(3) The corruption-type mechanism. The form of the agency relation determines both the type and the total level of corruption. Tax farmers with short-term contracts tend toward overtaxation (using surcharges to extract maximum revenue before the contract expires), which harms the tax base in the long run. Salaried state officials tend toward embezzlement and bribery (taking bribes for underassessment), which reduces revenue flowing to the ruler but may be less damaging to the tax base. Since the type of corruption associated with tax farming (overtaxation) hurts taxpayers more directly and visibly than the type associated with state administration, tax farming should be more common when rulers have high autonomy and are less constrained by taxpayer resistance (p. 291).

(4) The technology-of-control mechanism. Improvements in communications, transportation, and record-keeping directly increase rulers’ ability to monitor distant agents, thereby increasing the relative efficiency of state administration. In the early modern period, basic transportation and communication technologies remained quite primitive until the late 18th century (for example, in the 16th century, it took three weeks to travel from Paris /pəˈriː/ to Lyon /liˈɒ̃/, and even in 1765 it took fourteen days from Paris to Toulouse /tuːˈluːz/), which meant that rulers’ control capacities remained structurally low for most of the period (p. 298). The gradual improvement of these technologies over time explains the long-run shift from tax farming to state administration.

(5) The consolidation-and-learning mechanism. Over time, tax farms themselves consolidated into larger organizations, and in the process developed sophisticated administrative techniques, improved accounting procedures, rigorous inspections and audits, merit-based recruitment, higher salaries for agents, that were eventually adopted by state administrations when they took over tax collection. Thus, ironically, tax farming paved the way for its own replacement by creating the bureaucratic innovations that made state administration efficient enough to collect indirect taxes (p. 299–300, 303).

凯泽提出了若干相互关联的机制，解释控制能力如何塑造税收征集制度的选择：

（1）激励对齐机制。 在国家行政征收中，领取固定薪资的代理人缺乏最大化税收征收的激励；其收入不取决于征收多少。这产生了经典的偷懒和腐败问题：官员可能接受纳税人贿赂以换取低估应税资产，或挪用已征收的资金。由于委托人（统治者）承担剩余风险，统治者必须在监督上投入大量资源。相比之下，在包税制中，代理人是剩余索取者，超出固定购买价格的所有征收额都是包税人的利润。这创造了最大化征收的强大自利动机，而且关键的是，也激励包税人控制其下属代理人的腐败，因为腐败直接减少包税人的收入（第290、294页）。

（2）监督困难机制。 应税资产的性质决定了统治者监督其代理人和纳税人的难度。当应税资产具有流动性（如受关税约束的贸易商品）、随时间变动（如波动的贸易量）、且难以度量（如运输中货物的价值）时，统治者极难判断低收入究竟反映的是诚实征收下真正低的税基、代理人偷懒、代理人腐败（受贿低估）还是纳税人逃税。在这种高度信息不对称的条件下，通过包税制使代理人成为剩余索取者是最有效的解决方案，因为它将度量问题转移给了最有激励去解决它的代理人。反之，当资产不可移动、稳定且容易度量（如土地）时，国家行政征收变得可行，因为统治者可以通过相对简单的交叉核查（如重复土地调查）来验证代理人的表现（第291–293页）。

（3）腐败类型机制。 代理关系的形式不仅决定腐败的总体水平，还决定占主导地位的腐败类型。持有短期合同的包税人倾向于过度征税（使用附加费在合同到期前榨取最大收入），这从长远来看损害税基。领取固定薪资的国家官员则倾向于挪用公款和受贿（为低估资产而收受贿赂），这减少了流向统治者的收入，但对税基的损害可能较小。由于与包税制相关的腐败类型（过度征税）比与国家行政征收相关的腐败类型更直接、更明显地伤害纳税人，因此包税制应在统治者自主性高、较少受纳税人抵抗约束时更为普遍（第291页）。

（4）控制技术机制。 通讯、交通和档案记录的改进直接提高了统治者监控远方代理人的能力，从而提高了国家行政征收的相对效率。在早期现代时期，基本的交通和通讯技术直到18世纪晚期仍然相当原始（例如，16世纪从巴黎 /pəˈriː/ 到里昂 /liˈɒ̃/ 需要三周，甚至到1765年从巴黎到图卢兹 /tuːˈluːz/ 仍需十四天），这意味着统治者的控制能力在该时期的大部分时间内结构性地保持在低水平（第298页）。这些技术的逐渐改进解释了从包税制到国家行政征收的长期转变。

（5）整合与学习机制。 随着时间推移，包税场本身整合为更大的组织，并在此过程中发展出精密的行政技术，改进的会计程序、严格的检查和审计、基于绩效的招聘、更高的代理人薪资，这些技术最终在国家行政部门接管税收征集时被采用。因此，颇具讽刺意味的是，包税制通过创造使国家行政征收足以高效征收间接税的官僚创新，为自身的被取代铺平了道路（第299–300、303页）。

Data & methods

Data

Kiser uses qualitative, comparative-historical data drawn from secondary historical sources covering the fiscal systems of multiple early modern states. The geographic coverage includes England /ˈɪŋɡlənd/, France /fræns/, Spain /speɪn/, Prussia /ˈprʌʃə/, Sweden /ˈswiːdən/, China /ˈtʃaɪnə/, Russia /ˈrʌʃə/, the Ottoman Empire /ˈɒtəmən ˈɛmpaɪər/, and the Netherlands /ˈnɛðərləndz/ (p. 295). The time period spans roughly from the 13th century to the late 18th century, with the most detailed coverage of England (1275–1688) and France (13th century to the Revolution of 1789) (p. 295–299). The unit of analysis varies: it is sometimes a country-period (e.g., Elizabethan /ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən/ England), sometimes a specific tax (e.g., the English customs, the French gabelle /ɡəˈbɛl/), and sometimes a specific tax farming organization (e.g., the French General Farms). The dependent variable, the form of tax collection, is measured qualitatively as whether a given tax in a given country-period was collected by tax farmers, state officials, lump-sum negotiated payments, or some combination thereof (p. 295–296). Key independent variables are measured as follows: the nature of the tax (direct versus indirect) is coded based on whether the tax falls on immobile, easily measured assets like land or on mobile, variable assets like traded commodities (p. 291–292); the development of control technologies is assessed through historical evidence on transportation times, communications infrastructure, and record-keeping practices (p. 298); country size is assessed in broad comparative terms; ruler autonomy is assessed through the presence or absence of absolutist versus parliamentary regimes (p. 291, 298–299). The data are drawn from a wide range of secondary historical works, including Matthews /ˈmæθjuːz/ on the French Royal General Farms, Ashton /ˈæʃtən/ on English revenue farming, Dietz /diːts/ on English public finance, Wolfe /wʊlf/ on Renaissance France, and many others cited in the extensive endnotes (p. 306–315). Kiser explicitly acknowledges that the data are preliminary and not sufficient for systematic hypothesis testing, but argues they can provide useful preliminary assessments of the theoretical approaches (p. 295).

凯泽使用的是从二手历史文献中提取的定性比较历史数据，涵盖多个早期现代国家的财政体系。地理覆盖范围包括英格兰 /ˈɪŋɡlənd/、法兰西 /fræns/、西班牙 /speɪn/、普鲁士 /ˈprʌʃə/、瑞典 /ˈswiːdən/、中国 /ˈtʃaɪnə/、俄罗斯 /ˈrʌʃə/、奥斯曼帝国 /ˈɒtəmən ˈɛmpaɪər/ 和尼德兰 /ˈnɛðərləndz/（第295页）。时间跨度大约从13世纪到18世纪晚期，其中对英格兰（1275–1688年）和法兰西（13世纪至1789年大革命）的覆盖最为详细（第295–299页）。分析单位各有不同：有时是国家-时段（如伊丽莎白时代 /ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən/ 的英格兰），有时是特定税种（如英国关税、法国盐税 /ɡəˈbɛl/），有时是特定的包税组织（如法国总包税场）。因变量，税收征集形式，通过定性方式测量，即特定国家-时段的特定税种是由包税人、国家官员、一次性协商支付还是某种组合方式征收（第295–296页）。主要自变量的测量方式如下：税种性质（直接税与间接税）根据税收是否落在土地等不动产、易于度量的资产上还是贸易商品等流动性、可变资产上来编码（第291–292页）；控制技术的发展通过关于交通运输时间、通讯基础设施和档案记录实践的历史证据来评估（第298页）；国家规模以宽泛的比较方式评估；统治者自主性通过绝对主义政体与议会制政体的存在与否来评估（第291、298–299页）。数据来源于大量二手历史著作，包括马修斯 /ˈmæθjuːz/ 关于法国皇家总包税场的研究、阿什顿 /ˈæʃtən/ 关于英国收入包税的研究、迪茨 /diːts/ 关于英国公共财政的研究、沃尔夫 /wʊlf/ 关于文艺复兴时期法兰西的研究，以及大量尾注中引用的其他文献（第306–315页）。凯泽明确承认这些数据是初步性的，不足以进行系统性的假设检验，但认为它们可以为理论方法提供有用的初步评估（第295页）。

Methods

Kiser employs comparative-historical analysis guided by rational choice theory, specifically principal-agent (agency) theory. The method involves deriving a set of testable propositions from the theoretical model and then assessing their plausibility against the historical record of multiple early modern states. Seven formal propositions are stated (p. 294–295): (1) the greater the difficulty of controlling agents, the greater the use of tax farming; (2) tax farming will be more common for indirect than direct taxes; (3) larger countries will use more tax farming; (4) higher ruler autonomy leads to more tax farming; (5) better control technologies lead to less tax farming; (6) switching between systems will be common; and (7) tax farms will have higher salaries, better monitoring, and faster innovation than state administrations. Each proposition is then assessed against the available historical evidence from the cases examined. The assessment is conducted through structured qualitative comparison: Kiser compares patterns across types of taxes within countries, across countries at similar time periods, and within countries over time. He also uses process tracing to examine the causal mechanisms at work in specific historical episodes, such as the consolidation of English and French tax farms and the timing of the shift from tax farming to state administration relative to political revolutions (p. 295–300). For the key methodological assumptions to hold, several conditions must be met: rulers must be reasonably approximated as rational revenue-maximizers; the structural factors identified (control technologies, country size, tax type, ruler autonomy) must be the primary determinants of control capacity; and the secondary historical sources must provide reasonably accurate accounts of the fiscal institutions described. Kiser acknowledges that the data are not adequate for systematic testing and that the results should be treated as preliminary (p. 295, 303).

凯泽采用了以理性选择理论，特别是委托-代理（代理）理论为指导的比较历史分析方法。其方法是从理论模型中推导出一组可检验的命题，然后对照多个早期现代国家的历史记录评估其合理性。文章陈述了七个正式命题（第294–295页）：（1）控制代理人的难度越大，使用包税制的程度越高；（2）包税制用于间接税比直接税更为普遍；（3）较大的国家更多使用包税制；（4）统治者自主性越高，包税制越多；（5）控制技术越发达，包税制越少；（6）在不同体系之间切换将是常见的；（7）包税场的薪资将更高、监督将更好、控制技术的创新将更快。然后，每个命题都对照所考察案例的可用历史证据进行评估。评估通过结构化的定性比较进行：凯泽比较了同一国家内不同税种之间的模式、相似时期不同国家之间的模式，以及同一国家内随时间变化的模式。他还使用过程追踪来考察特定历史事件中运作的因果机制，如英法包税场的整合以及从包税制向国家行政征收转变的时机与政治革命之间的关系（第295–300页）。为使关键方法论假设成立，需要满足若干条件：统治者必须可以合理地近似为理性的收入最大化者；所识别的结构性因素（控制技术、国家规模、税种、统治者自主性）必须是控制能力的主要决定因素；二手历史文献必须提供对所描述财政制度的合理准确的记述。凯泽承认数据不足以进行系统性检验，结果应被视为初步性的（第295、303页）。

Class Notes

Week 8 lecture: discussion of this reading

Locating the text: the professor notes that Kiser is a sociologist who applies the principal-agent framework from new institutional economics to the problem of early modern tax systems. This is an interdisciplinary attempt: from contract theory (economics) to historical sociology.

The core question: what Kiser tries to explain is not tax revenue itself, but the institutional form that tax collection takes, namely state administration/hierarchy versus tax farming/market. This is a question about institutional choice. An analogy using two ideal-typical contracts:

The professor uses agricultural land contracts from development economics as an analogy to aid understanding: tax system, state administration (hierarchy), tax farming, sharecropping (an intermediate form).

Agricultural contract analogy: hiring agricultural laborers (paying a fixed wage), renting land to tenant farmers (collecting a fixed rent), sharecropping.

Core feature: the agent has no marginal incentive and needs monitoring; the agent is a residual claimant, self-incentivizing; shares proceeds proportionally.

A student also proposed the analogy of mercenaries vs. standing armies. The professor thought this was an interesting principal-agent relationship worth examining further in terms of specific contract forms, for example, “pay only if you win” vs. “pay first, then fight” would produce very different incentives.

The core factors determining institutional choice: students summarized the list of structural factors Kiser proposes (the nature of the tax type, state size, ruler autonomy, and the development of control technology). The professor pointed out that the common thread linking these factors is monitoring capacity. When the ruler can effectively monitor the agent, state administration tends to be chosen; when monitoring is difficult, tax farming tends to be chosen. Key criticisms and responses in class discussion: Criticism one (student Speaker 3): the model assumes the agent cannot pocket the difference, that is, the agent cannot report a low income to the ruler after collecting a high one. In reality, the agent is the first to see the actual amount collected, so why would he report it honestly?

The professor’s response: agreed that this criticism is fair to the model itself, but it does not deal a fatal blow to Kiser’s argument. Kiser’s solution is that precisely because the ruler cannot observe the actual amount collected, rulers adopt an auction mechanism: a large number of potential agents bid, and the winning bidder pays a fixed sum in advance and then collects taxes on his own. This is pure tax farming, and the ruler does not need to observe the collection process at all. The professor also used the example of the alcabala tax (similar to a sales/turnover tax) in colonial Mexico to vividly illustrate the difficulty of monitoring: tax officials inspected caravans at the city gate but could not tell whether a merchant was lying (whether he was really just passing through rather than selling locally), could not verify transaction receipts, and auditors coming from elsewhere had no knowledge of local conditions. Criticism two (student Speaker 5/Tanvi): the model lacks enforcement. Monitoring is not important, but enforcement is very important: if you sign a contract stating that the other party must give you a certain amount, you must have the ability to enforce that contract. Moreover, enforcement itself also depends on monitoring: you must be able to monitor whether the tax farmer has violated the contract. It is hard to imagine a state that has perfect enforcement capacity but no monitoring capacity at all.

The professor’s response: fully agreed that this is a key scope condition. If a tax farmer could simply take armed men to the market to collect taxes without paying the crown anything, the whole model would collapse. But the professor used the example of Spanish colonial Mexico to show that, in practice, it was relatively easy for the crown to enforce tax-farming contracts: when someone impersonated a tax official to collect taxes, local residents would complain to the crown, and the crown could send in garrison troops to suppress it. The monitoring threshold required for this kind of enforcement activity (someone falsely posing as a tax collector) is far lower than the monitoring threshold required to audit tax records. Therefore, enforcement is a necessary background condition, but it is not equivalent to monitoring capacity. Criticism three (student Speaker 2): wouldn’t a tax official on a fixed salary just collect more tax and pocket the difference?

The professor’s response: this is precisely why monitoring is needed. The ruler knows that tax bureaucrats will be tempted to pocket the difference, and this is exactly the core problem that must be solved when choosing hierarchical administration. On the assessment of methodology:

The professor thinks Kiser’s method of analysis falls between qualitative comparative historical analysis and semi-quantitative analysis: there is no formal statistical test, but it implicitly carries the logic of “in the cases I have examined, most fit this argument.” The professor criticized the article for lacking a systematic summary table showing which cases fit and which do not. The article was published in 1994, and its main contribution lies in interdisciplinary theoretical arbitrage: bringing contract theory from economics into historical sociology. The professor also pointed out that Kiser’s treatment of alternative theories such as Weber, Williamson, and North is somewhat of a “straw man” style of criticism, especially in the discussion of Weber; but the criticism of “predictability” as an alternative explanation is forceful, because that is indeed the practitioner’s perspective. On the discussion of “hybrid institutions”: a student (Speaker 6) asked whether the “hybrid institutions” Kiser refers to can combine both mechanisms within the same agent. The professor clarified: the hybrid institutions Kiser refers to mean that different tax types use different institutions: indirect taxes (such as a sales/turnover tax) adopt tax farming because q is low, while direct taxes (such as land tax) adopt state administration because q is higher. These two systems operate simultaneously but independently. On the “experimentation” argument: Kiser proposes that rulers experiment back and forth between the two institutions. The professor thinks this is more of an independent argument, not directly related to the core of the principal-agent framework, and has more to do with the ruler’s learning process when uncertain which institution is more efficient. On the historical evolution of indirect and direct taxes: a student (Speaker 5/Tanvi) pointed out that the proposition “indirect taxes suit tax farming” is strongly time-bound: contemporary developed states’ fiscal capacity in fact relies mainly on indirect taxes (such as VAT). The professor responded that this is entirely correct, and the key lies in technological change.

The professor used the mechanism design of VAT to illustrate this: the cleverness of VAT lies in placing the burden of proof on the seller: the seller needs to collect input invoices to deduct tax, which aligns the incentives of every participant in the transaction in the right direction, essentially a self-enforcing mechanism design. This kind of technology was not available in the early modern period.

文献定位：老师指出 Kiser 是一位社会学家，将新制度经济学中的委托-代理框架应用于早期现代税收制度的问题。这是跨学科的尝试：从合约理论（经济学）到历史社会学。

核心问题：Kiser 试图解释的不是税收收入本身，而是税收征集采取的制度形式，即国家行政征收（state administration/hierarchy）还是包税制（tax farming/market）。这是关于制度选择的问题。两种理想型合约的类比：

老师用发展经济学中的农业土地合约来做类比，帮助理解：税收制度国家行政征收（科层制）包税制分成制（中间形态）

农业合约类比雇佣农业劳动者（付固定工资）出租土地给佃农（收固定租金）佃耕制（sharecropping）

核心特征代理人无边际激励，需要监控代理人是剩余索取者，自我激励按比例分享收益

一位学生还提出了雇佣兵 vs 常备军的类比，老师认为这是一个有趣的委托-代理关系，值得进一步考察具体的合约形式，例如”打赢了才付钱”vs”先付钱再打仗”会产生截然不同的激励。

决定制度选择的核心因素：学生归纳了 Kiser 提出的结构性因素清单（税种性质、国家规模、统治者自主性、控制技术的发展）。老师指出，这些因素的共同主线就是监控能力（monitoring）。当统治者能有效监控代理人时，倾向于选择国家行政征收；当监控困难时，倾向于包税制。课堂讨论中的关键批评与回应：批评一（学生 Speaker 3）：模型假设代理人不能私吞差额，即代理人不能在征收到高收入后只向统治者报告低收入。在现实中，第一个看到实际征收额的是代理人自己，他为什么要如实上交？

老师的回应：承认这个批评对模型本身是公平的，但对 Kiser 的论证并不构成致命打击。Kiser 的解决方案是：正因为统治者无法观察实际征收额，所以他们采用拍卖机制：让大量潜在代理人竞标，中标者预先支付固定金额，然后自行征税。这就是纯粹的包税制，统治者根本不需要观察征收过程。老师还用殖民地墨西哥的 alcabala 税（类似于商品流转税）的例子生动说明了监控的困难：税务官在城市入口检查商队，但无法判断商人是否说谎（是否真的只是路过而非在本地销售），无法核实交易凭证，审计员从外地来也无法了解本地情况。批评二（学生 Speaker 5/Tanvi）：模型中缺少执行力（enforcement）。监控不重要但执行力很重要：如果你签了合约说对方必须给你这么多，你必须有能力执行这个合约。而且执行力本身也依赖于监控：你必须能够监控到包税人是否违反了合约。很难想象一个国家拥有完美的执行力却完全没有监控能力。

老师的回应：完全同意这是一个关键的范围条件（scope condition）。如果包税人可以直接带着武装人员去市场上征税而不向王室缴纳任何费用，整个模型就崩塌了。但老师通过西班牙殖民地墨西哥的例子说明，在实践中，王室执行包税合约相对容易：当有人冒充税务人员征税时，当地居民会向王室投诉，王室可以派驻军镇压。这种执法活动所需的监控门槛（有人冒名征税）远低于审计税收报表所需的监控门槛。因此，执行力是一个必要的背景条件，但不等同于监控能力。批评三（学生 Speaker 2）：拿固定薪水的税务官员难道不会多征税然后私吞差额吗？

老师的回应：这正是为什么需要监控的原因。统治者知道税务官僚会被诱惑私吞差额，这正是他们在选择科层行政时需要解决的核心问题。关于方法论的评价：

老师认为 Kiser 的分析方法属于定性比较历史分析与半定量分析之间：没有正式的统计检验，但隐含了”在我考察的案例中，多数符合这个论点”的逻辑。老师批评说，文章缺少一个系统性的汇总表来展示哪些案例符合、哪些不符合。文章发表于 1994 年，主要贡献在于跨学科的理论套利：将经济学中的合约理论引入历史社会学。老师还指出，Kiser 对 Weber、Williamson、North 等替代理论的处理有些”稻草人”（straw man）式的批评，尤其是对 Weber 的讨论；但关于”可预测性”（predictability）作为替代解释的批评是有力的，因为这确实是实践者的视角。关于”混合制度”的讨论：一位学生（Speaker 6）问 Kiser 所说的”混合制度”是否可以在同一代理人身上结合两种机制。老师澄清：Kiser 所说的混合制度是指不同税种使用不同制度：间接税（如商品流转税）由于 q 很低而采用包税制，直接税（如土地税）由于 q 较高而采用国家行政征收。这两个系统同时运行但各自独立。关于”实验”论点：Kiser 提出统治者会在两种制度之间来回实验。老师认为这更多是一个独立的论点，与委托-代理框架的核心并不直接相关，更多关乎统治者在不确定哪种制度更高效时的学习过程。关于间接税与直接税的历史演变：一位学生（Speaker 5/Tanvi）指出，”间接税适合包税制”这个命题有很强的时间局限性：当代发达国家的财政能力恰恰主要依赖间接税（如增值税/VAT）。老师回应说这完全正确，关键在于技术变革。

老师用增值税的机制设计来说明：VAT 的巧妙之处在于它将举证责任放在卖方身上：卖方需要收集进项发票以抵扣税款，这使得每个交易参与者的激励都指向正确的方向，本质上是一种自动执行的机制设计。这种技术在早期现代是不可用的。

Shared week context: the blackboard principal-agent model & cross-reading synthesis

Lecture framing: the Feb. 26 class used a principal-agent toy model to organize the week around legibility, monitoring, and centralization. The ruler chooses an institutional form, indirect rule/tax farming (\(I\)) or direct rule/state administration (\(D\)); the agent chooses effort \(e\in\{0,1\}\); and effort affects the probability of high tax revenue \(R\in\{L,H\}\).

\[ \Pr(R=H\mid e=1)=p\in(0,1),\qquad \Pr(R=H\mid e=0)=0. \]

The key monitoring parameter is:

\[ q=\Pr(e=0\mid R=L),\qquad q\in\left[\frac{1}{2},1\right]. \]

Indirect rule/tax farming works through a share contract. The agent's incentive compatibility constraint and the ruler's optimal share are:

\[ pSH\ge k,\qquad S_I^*=\frac{k}{pH}. \]

The ruler's profit under indirect rule is independent of monitoring quality:

\[ \pi_I=p(1-S_I^*)H=pH-k. \]

Direct rule/state administration adds the threat of dismissal. If the agent is retained, the position has continuation value \(V>0\). The incentive constraint, optimal share, and profit are:

\[ pSH+qV\ge k,\qquad S_D^*=\max\left\{0,\frac{k-qV}{pH}\right\}. \]
\[ \pi_D=\begin{cases} pH, & qV>k,\\ pH-k+qV, & qV\le k. \end{cases} \]

The institutional switch from \(I\) to \(D\) requires paying a transition cost \(C\). Direct rule is chosen when:

\[ \pi_D-C\ge\pi_I\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad qV\ge C\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad q^*=\frac{C}{V}. \]

The core takeaway is that tax farming is the fallback institution when monitoring is weak, while direct administration becomes attractive once monitoring and dismissal can discipline agents enough to justify the transition cost.
