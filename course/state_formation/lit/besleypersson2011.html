Dependent variable (DV)

If one asks this question at the level of the whole book’s opening problem, the broad dependent variable is the pattern the authors call “development clusters”: the systematic co-movement, within the same country, of state capacity, low political violence, and high income. The key point in Chapter 1 is that “clusters” do not mean geographic clustering across neighboring countries. They mean that different development outcomes inside the same country tend to move together. Countries with strong state capacity also tend to have higher income and less internal violence, while weak states tend to be poor and violent. (pp. 6–10)

But if one asks the question strictly for Chapters 1–2, then the answer must be narrower and more precise. Chapter 2 does not yet model the entire development cluster. Its direct endogenous outcome is fiscal capacity, understood both as a stock of state capability and as an object of forward-looking investment. In that chapter, the main thing to be explained is why governments do or do not invest in the administrative and institutional structures that allow broad-based taxation in the future. So, for Chapters 1–2 specifically, the most accurate answer is: the broad explanandum is development clusters, but the directly modeled dependent variable is fiscal-capacity investment and the resulting level of fiscal capacity. (pp. 13–16, 40–42, 45–47)

如果从整本书最初提出的问题来看，广义上的因变量是作者所谓的“发展集群”现象：也就是在同一个国家内部，国家能力、较低的政治暴力和较高的收入会系统性地一起出现。第一章特别强调，这里的“集群”不是地理学意义上相邻国家之间的空间聚集，而是同一个国家内部不同发展结果变量之间的共变。国家能力强的国家往往也更富裕、内部暴力更少；而弱国家则往往同时贫困且暴力频发。 （第6–10页）

但如果严格限定在第1–2章之内，这个问题就必须回答得更窄、更精确。第二章还没有把整个“发展集群”都正式放进模型里。它直接内生化、正式建模的结果变量是财政能力，既把它理解为一种国家能力存量，也把它理解为一种前瞻性的投资对象。第二章真正要解释的是：政府为什么会、或者不会，去投资那些使未来广泛征税成为可能的行政与制度结构。因此，对第1–2章最准确的说法应当是：广义的解释对象是发展集群，但第二章直接建模的因变量是财政能力投资及其形成的财政能力水平。 （第13–16页，第40–42页，第45–47页）

Independent variable (IV)

There is no single master independent variable in these chapters. That is one reason why forcing the book into a simple “one DV, one IV” template can be misleading. The theoretical framework instead identifies a set of political and economic determinants that jointly shape state building. In Chapter 2, the most important determinants are the strength of common interests relative to redistributive interests, the cohesiveness of political institutions, political stability, income, and access to nontax revenue such as natural-resource rents or foreign aid. Chapter 1 is explicit that many of these are treated as given only temporarily, and later chapters progressively endogenize more of them. (pp. 11–16, 40–42, 45–47)

In empirical terms, the authors use observable proxies for those theoretical determinants. The most prominent are past external war prevalence as a proxy for demand for common-interest public goods, average executive constraints as a proxy for institutional cohesiveness, indicators of nonopen executive recruitment as a proxy for political stability, ethnic homogeneity as a proxy for lower heterogeneity in public-goods valuations, income per capita, and inequality in some specifications. But the authors are careful not to claim that these proxies are perfect mappings from data to theory. (pp. 16–21, 91–98)

这两章并不存在一个单独支配全局的“主要自变量”。这也是为什么把这本书硬套进“一因变量、一个主自变量”的模板会有误导性。作者的理论框架实际上识别的是一组共同塑造国家建设的政治与经济决定因素。在第二章里，最重要的决定因素包括：社会中共同利益相对于再分配利益的强弱、政治制度的凝聚性、政治稳定性、收入水平，以及来自自然资源租金或外国援助等非税收入的可得性。第一章还明确说明，这些因素中很多只是暂时被当作给定参数，后面章节会逐步把更多因素内生化。 （第11–16页，第40–42页，第45–47页）

在经验分析层面，作者用一组可观测变量去代理这些理论决定因素。最重要的包括：过去外部战争的发生率，用来代理共同利益公共品的需求；平均 executive constraints，用来代理制度凝聚性；非开放式的行政领导人选拔指标，用来代理政治稳定性；族群同质性，用来代理公共品偏好异质性的较低程度；以及人均收入和部分设定中的不平等程度。但作者也很谨慎，并没有声称这些代理变量能够和理论参数一一精确对应。 （第16–21页，第91–98页）

Stateness

The authors conceptualize the state in these chapters primarily through state capacity, meaning the institutional capability of government to carry out policy. This is narrower and more operational than a broad philosophical or Weberian definition of the state. The authors are not trying to define the state in the abstract. They are trying to explain the creation of effective policy-supporting institutions. In Chapter 1, they distinguish two broad dimensions of state capacity: fiscal capacity and legal capacity. Fiscal capacity refers to the extractive side of the state, the administrative, monitoring, enforcement, and compliance structures that allow revenue to be raised from broad tax bases such as income and consumption. Legal capacity refers to the productive or market-supporting side of the state, the courts, judges, registers, and legal infrastructures that help enforce contracts, protect property rights, and support markets. (pp. 6–8)

In measurement terms, Chapter 1 uses illustrative proxies. Fiscal capacity is first illustrated with total tax revenue as a share of GDP in 1999. Legal capacity is illustrated with the International Country Risk Guide property-rights or antidiversion index in 1997, normalized between 0 and 1. But the authors immediately stress that these are approximations. Tax revenue mixes capacity and willingness. Legal-capacity proxies are imperfect as well. This caution matters because one of the book’s deeper methodological claims is that good theory is needed precisely to distinguish symptoms from causes and to interpret imperfect indicators. (pp. 2–7, 8–10)

Most importantly, state building is conceptualized not as a one-shot historical event but as a forward-looking investment process. Chapter 2 makes this explicit. Fiscal capacity is treated as a form of capital: it is inherited from the past, it depreciates, it constrains current policy, and it can be increased only through costly investment by current incumbents. This is one of the book’s central moves. It shifts attention away from treating institutions as fixed background conditions and toward treating them as strategic objects of political choice under uncertainty. (pp. 12–16, 40–48)

在这两章里，作者主要是通过“国家能力”来概念化国家，也就是政府执行政策的制度性能力。这种处理比抽象的哲学定义或广义的韦伯式国家定义更窄，也更可操作。作者并不是想在抽象层面回答“国家是什么”，而是想解释有效的政策支持性制度是怎样形成的。第一章把国家能力区分为两个维度：财政能力和法律能力。财政能力指国家的汲取性一面，也就是使国家能够从所得税、消费税等广泛税基中征税的行政、监测、执法和合规结构。法律能力则是国家的生产性、市场支持性一面，也就是法院、法官、登记制度和法律基础设施，它们帮助执行合同、保护产权并支持市场运行。 （第6–8页）

在度量上，第一章先用一些示意性的代理变量来说明问题。财政能力首先用1999年的税收占GDP比重来近似，法律能力则用1997年的 International Country Risk Guide 的产权保护或 anti-diversion 指数来近似，并把它标准化到0到1之间。但作者立刻就提醒读者，这些都只是近似量。税收占比混合了征税能力与征税意愿，法律能力的代理指标也同样不完美。这个提醒很重要，因为本书一个更深的方法论主张正是：只有先有理论框架，才能区分症状与原因，也才能对这些并不完美的指标作出解释。 （第2–7页，第8–10页）

更重要的是，作者把国家建设概念化为一种前瞻性的投资过程，而不是一次性的历史事件。第二章把这一点明确写进了模型。财政能力被当作一种资本：它从过去继承而来，会折旧，会约束当期政策，而且只能通过当期执政者付出代价去投资才能提高。这是本书最核心的理论动作之一。它把注意力从“把制度当成固定背景条件”转向“把制度当成在不确定条件下被政治主体战略性选择和积累的对象”。 （第12–16页，第40–48页）

Core argument

The core argument in Chapter 1 is that the observed clustering of high state capacity, low internal political violence, and high income is not accidental. These outcomes tend to come together because they are shaped by overlapping political and economic determinants, and because there are complementarities and feedbacks among them. However, this broad claim is only introduced in Chapter 1. It is not yet fully proven there. Chapter 1 is a programmatic chapter: it states the problem, introduces the broad logic, shows salient correlations, and explains how the later chapters will build the full framework step by step. (pp. 5–13, 37–38)

The core argument in Chapter 2 is narrower and more formal. Fiscal capacity is built when incumbents expect the future return to a broader tax base to be high enough relative to its present cost. Whether that expected return is high depends on politics. If future public revenue is likely to be used in the common interest, or if institutions are cohesive enough that future governments cannot easily turn state capacity into one-sided redistribution, then incumbents are willing to invest. If institutions are noncohesive and political turnover is unstable, incumbents fear that stronger fiscal capacity will later be used against them, and then they underinvest or do not invest at all. In that sense, political institutions and political stability shape the incentives to build the state as well as the policies governments can choose. (pp. 40–47, 45–47, 52–63)

The broader social-scientific significance of this argument is that it translates a classic historical-sociological insight into a formal political-economy model. Historians such as Otto Hintze /ˈɒtoʊ ˈhɪntsə/ and Charles Tilly /tʃɑːrlz ˈtɪli/ argued that the demands of war pushed rulers to build states. Besley and Persson do not simply repeat that claim. They reinterpret it in incentive terms. External war matters because it raises the expected value of common-interest public goods, which raises the expected payoff from fiscal investment. But they also show that war pressure alone is not enough. Whether war promotes state building depends on the political-institutional environment in which those revenues will later be used. (pp. 12–16, 16–20, 44–47)

第一章的核心论点是：高国家能力、低内部政治暴力和高收入之所以经常聚集在一起，并不是偶然的。这些结果之所以会一起出现，是因为它们受到一组相互重叠的政治与经济决定因素的共同塑造，而且它们之间还存在互补性和反馈效应。但这个广义主张在第一章里还只是被提出，并没有被完全证明。第一章本质上是一个 programmatic chapter：它提出问题，介绍总体逻辑，展示一些显著相关关系，并说明后面章节将如何一步步把这个框架正式搭起来。 （第5–13页，第37–38页）

第二章的核心论点则更窄，也更形式化。财政能力之所以会被建设，是因为执政者认为：更广税基在未来带来的收益，足以抵过当期投资的成本。而这种预期收益是否足够高，关键取决于政治。如果未来财政收入很可能被用于共同利益，或者制度足够有凝聚力，以至于未来政府不能轻易把国家能力变成单边再分配工具，那么当前执政者就愿意投资。反过来，如果制度缺乏凝聚性、政治更替又不稳定，执政者就会担心：今天建设出来的财政能力，未来反而会被对手拿来对付自己，于是就会投资不足，甚至根本不投资。也就是说，政治制度和政治稳定性不只是约束政策，它们还塑造了国家建设本身的激励结构。 （第40–47页，第52–63页）

这一论点在更广的社会科学意义上，在于它把经典的历史社会学洞见转译成了一个形式化的政治经济学模型。像 Otto Hintze /ˈɒtoʊ ˈhɪntsə/ 和 Charles Tilly /tʃɑːrlz ˈtɪli/ 这样的历史学家和历史社会学家早就提出，战争需求推动了国家建设。Besley 和 Persson 并不是简单重复这个命题，而是把它重写成激励逻辑：外部战争之所以重要，是因为它提高了共同利益公共品的预期价值，从而提高了财政投资的预期回报。但他们同时也说明，仅有战争压力并不足够。战争是否真的推动国家建设，还取决于这些收入日后将置于何种政治制度环境中使用。 （第12–16页，第16–20页，第44–47页）

Mechanisms

The first mechanism is the common-interest mechanism. When future public funds are expected to finance genuinely common-interest goods, especially defense against external threats, the incumbent values future revenue even if political power changes hands. In the model, a higher probability of the high-value public-goods state raises the expected marginal value of future public funds, which makes fiscal-capacity investment more attractive. This is the formal channel through which the book reworks the classic “war made the state” claim. (pp. 15–20, 52–58)

The second mechanism is the institutional-cohesiveness mechanism. Even when common interests are not overwhelming, cohesive political institutions can make state building possible because they constrain the winner from using the future state primarily against the loser. In the model, greater cohesiveness lowers the attractiveness of pure redistribution and increases the range of situations in which incumbents prefer public-goods provision. This makes investments in fiscal capacity safer from the incumbent’s point of view. In simple terms, institutions matter because they make future policy use more predictable and less threatening. (pp. 18–21, 48–57)

The third mechanism is the political-stability mechanism. If institutions are not cohesive, then the key question becomes whether today’s incumbent expects to remain in office. When turnover is highly likely, the incumbent fears that any fiscal investment will later be captured by the opposition and turned into redistribution against the incumbent’s own group. Under those conditions, incentives to invest collapse. This mechanism is what generates the weak-state equilibrium in Chapter 2. (pp. 19–21, 56–63)

The fourth mechanism is the economic-structure or rentier mechanism. When governments have access to large nontax revenues from resource rents or aid, they depend less on broad market incomes as a fiscal base. That weakens incentives to build the institutions needed for broad-based taxation. In the larger logic of the book, this also tends to weaken incentives to build market-supporting institutions, because lower market income means a lower tax base. This is how the authors incorporate a political-economy version of the rentier-state argument into their framework. (pp. 20–21, 40–42)

The fifth mechanism, developed through extensions already foreshadowed in Chapter 2, concerns polarization and inequality. Greater heterogeneity reduces the degree to which public goods are truly common-interest goods across groups. Greater inequality can raise the tax price of public spending for the incumbent if the incumbent’s group is richer than average. In both cases, the perceived return to state building falls. These extensions are important because they show that the same framework can move beyond a purely symmetric two-group world and connect to broader theories of polarization, ethnic politics, and distributive conflict in comparative politics. (pp. 47, 52–63; Chapter 2 plan at pp. 45–47)

第一个机制是共同利益机制。如果未来的公共资金预期会被用于真正的共同利益公共品，尤其是防御外部威胁，那么现任执政者即使担心未来失去权力，也仍然会重视未来财政收入。在模型里，高价值公共品状态出现的概率越高，未来公共资金的预期边际价值就越高，财政能力投资也就越有吸引力。这就是本书把“战争塑造国家”这个经典命题形式化的核心渠道。 （第15–20页，第52–58页）

第二个机制是制度凝聚性机制。即便共同利益并不压倒一切，只要政治制度足够有凝聚力，国家建设仍然可能发生，因为这种制度会约束胜者，使其不能在未来主要把国家机器用来对付败者。在模型中，更高的制度凝聚性降低了纯再分配的吸引力，并扩大了执政者选择公共品供给而非单边转移的情形范围。这样一来，从现任者的角度看，投资财政能力就不那么危险了。简单说，制度之所以重要，是因为它让未来的政策用途更可预期，也更不具威胁性。 （第18–21页，第48–57页）

第三个机制是政治稳定性机制。如果制度本身并不凝聚，那么关键问题就变成：今天的执政者是否预期自己还会继续执政。当政治更替概率很高时，执政者会担心：今天投资的财政能力，未来会被反对派接管，并转化成针对本群体的不利再分配。在这种条件下，投资激励就会塌陷。第二章中的弱国家均衡，正是由这一机制产生的。 （第19–21页，第56–63页）

第四个机制是经济结构机制，也可以说是食利机制。当政府可以从资源租金或援助中获得大量非税收入时，它对广泛市场收入作为财政基础的依赖就会下降。这会削弱它建设广泛征税制度的激励。在本书更大的逻辑里，这还会进一步削弱国家建设市场支持性制度的动力，因为市场收入越低，税基也越小。这就是作者如何把“食利国家”论点转写进自己的政治经济学框架之中。 （第20–21页，第40–42页）

第五个机制是通过第二章的拓展部分所展开的极化与不平等机制。更大的异质性会降低公共品在不同群体之间作为“共同利益”的程度。更高的不平等则会在执政群体比平均收入更高时，提高其为公共支出承担的“税价格”。在这两种情况下，国家建设的预期回报都会下降。这个拓展很重要，因为它说明：同一个框架并不只适用于一个完全对称的两群体世界，它还能连接到比较政治中更广泛的极化、族群政治和分配冲突理论。 （第45–47页，第52–63页）

Data & methods

Data

Chapter 1 first uses descriptive cross-country indicators to show the empirical pattern the book wants to explain. Fiscal capacity is illustrated with total tax revenue as a share of GDP in 1999 from IMF sources. Legal capacity is illustrated with the International Country Risk Guide antidiversion or property-rights index in 1997. Political violence is illustrated using at least one year of civil war during 1950–2000 from the Armed Conflict Dataset, and fragility is shown using the Polity IV State Fragility Index. The unit of analysis in these descriptive figures is the country. The point of these figures is not causal identification. It is to establish that fiscal capacity, legal capacity, income, fragility, and violence are clustered in the way the theory aims to explain. (pp. 2–10)

The dedicated empirical analysis in Chapter 2 uses a cross-section of roughly one hundred countries, with sample size varying by data availability. The dependent-side measures are five proxies for fiscal capacity. These are total tax revenue as a share of GDP, the income-tax share of tax revenue, the nontrade-tax share of tax revenue, the bias toward income taxation relative to trade taxation, and the formal-sector share of the economy. The authors show that these proxies are strongly correlated with one another, with pairwise correlations roughly between 0.55 and 0.95. They standardize the fiscal-capacity variables by their sample standard deviation so that coefficients are more comparable across regressions. (pp. 91–93)

On the explanatory side, the main variables are constructed to proxy the model’s theoretical parameters. External war prevalence is measured as the share of years a country spent in interstate or extrastate war from 1816 or independence to 2000, using the Correlates of War data. Institutional cohesiveness is proxied by average executive constraints from Polity IV over the same long historical period, normalized to lie between 0 and 1. Political stability is proxied by nonopen, noncompetitive executive recruitment, again from Polity IV. Ethnic homogeneity is derived from ethnic fractionalization measures. Some specifications also include log GDP per capita in 2000 from Penn World Tables and a low-inequality indicator based on the Gini coefficient. The unit of analysis is again the country. (pp. 16–21, 91–98)

The data have a limited role. They show whether broad cross-country patterns line up with the model’s comparative statics. They do not estimate a clean causal effect of one determinant on fiscal capacity. The authors themselves are explicit about this limitation, both in Chapter 1 and in Chapter 2. (pp. 11–12, 91, 96–97)

第一章首先用一组描述性的跨国指标来展示本书想解释的经验事实。财政能力用1999年的税收占GDP比重来示意，数据来自 IMF。法律能力用1997年的 International Country Risk Guide 的 anti-diversion 或产权保护指数来示意。政治暴力则用 Armed Conflict Dataset 中1950，2000年间是否至少有一年发生内战来示意，脆弱性则用 Polity IV 的 State Fragility Index 来展示。这些描述性图表的分析单位都是国家。它们的目的不是做因果识别，而是先确立：财政能力、法律能力、收入、脆弱性和暴力确实以本书想要解释的方式聚集在一起。 （第2–10页）

第二章真正专门展开的经验分析，使用的是大约一百个国家左右的横截面样本，具体样本数会随变量可得性而变化。因变量一侧，作者使用了五个财政能力代理指标：税收占GDP比重、所得税占总税收份额、非贸易税占总税收份额、相对贸易税而言的所得税偏向，以及经济中正规部门所占比重。作者还展示了这五个财政能力代理量之间高度相关，成对相关系数大约在0.55到0.95之间。为了使不同回归中的系数更具有可比性，他们把这些财政能力变量都按样本标准差做了标准化处理。 （第91–93页）

在解释变量一侧，作者构造的主要变量都旨在代理模型中的理论参数。外部战争发生率被定义为：从1816年或独立年份到2000年，一个国家处于 interstate 或 extrastate war 状态的年份占比，数据来自 Correlates of War。制度凝聚性则用同一长时段内 Polity IV 的平均 executive constraints 来代理，并把它标准化到0到1之间。政治稳定性用非开放、非竞争性的行政领导人选拔来代理，也来自 Polity IV。族群同质性则是从族群分裂化指标推导出来的。一些设定里还加入了 2000 年的人均 GDP 对数（来自 Penn World Tables）以及基于基尼系数构造的低不平等指标。这里的分析单位同样是国家。 （第16–21页，第91–98页）

这里必须很清楚地区分数据的用途和限度。作者使用这些数据，是为了考察广义的跨国模式是否与模型的比较静态方向相一致，而不是为了估计某个单一决定因素对财政能力的“干净因果效应”。无论在第一章还是第二章，作者都明确承认了这一点。 （第11–12页，第91页，第96–97页）

Methods

The book uses two distinct kinds of method in Chapters 1–2. The first is formal theory. Chapter 2 develops a dynamic two-period political-economy model in which an incumbent government chooses whether to invest in fiscal capacity for the future while facing political constraints and uncertainty about future turnover. This is the core analytical method of the chapter. The second is empirical illustration through cross-country descriptive plots and OLS cross-sectional regressions. The regressions examine whether the signs and broad patterns in the data are consistent with the model’s predictions. (pp. 11–13, 40–47, 91–98)

The main empirical specification regresses each fiscal-capacity proxy on the core explanatory proxies together: war prevalence, executive constraints, political stability, and ethnic homogeneity, with robust standard errors. Additional specifications introduce interaction terms motivated by the model and then add income and low inequality. The model predicts some global comparative statics quite robustly, but the interaction evidence is weaker and does not strongly confirm all of the finer-grained local comparative-static predictions. This is something the authors themselves admit. (pp. 95–98)

For these empirical methods to have a strong causal interpretation, demanding assumptions would have to hold. The proxies for war, institutions, stability, and social structure would need to be exogenous with respect to omitted determinants of fiscal capacity. There would need to be no serious reverse causality, no major omitted historical confounders, and no large mapping error between the theoretical parameters and the observable proxies. The authors do not claim that these assumptions are satisfied. On the contrary, they say that because most relevant variables are jointly determined, isolating causal relations in cross-country data is very difficult. That is why the empirical material in these chapters should be read as pattern matching or theory-consistency checking, not as decisive causal adjudication. (pp. 11–12, 91, 96–97)

第1–2章实际上同时使用了两类方法。第一类是形式理论。第二章建立了一个两期动态政治经济学模型，其中现任政府在面临政治约束和未来权力更替不确定性的情况下，决定是否为未来投资财政能力。这是本章最核心的分析方法。第二类则是经验说明，包括跨国描述性图表和 OLS 横截面回归。回归的目标是考察数据中的符号方向和总体模式是否与模型预测相一致。 （第11–13页，第40–47页，第91–98页）

主要经验设定是把每一个财政能力代理变量，同时对战争发生率、行政约束、政治稳定性和族群同质性这些核心解释变量做代理回归，并使用稳健标准误。进一步的设定又加入了由模型所启发的交互项，之后再加入收入和低不平等变量。模型对一些总体性的比较静态方向预测得比较稳，但更细粒度的局部比较静态在交互项上支持得较弱，并没有被很强地证实。这一点作者自己也承认了。 （第95–98页）

如果要把这些经验方法解释成强因果识别，那么必须满足很苛刻的条件。战争、制度、稳定性和社会结构这些代理变量必须相对于财政能力的遗漏决定因素是外生的；不能存在严重的反向因果；不能有重大的历史性遗漏混杂因素；理论参数和可观测代理变量之间也不能有太大的映射误差。作者并没有声称这些条件成立。相反，他们明确说，因为大多数相关变量本来就是共同决定的，所以想在跨国数据中分离出因果关系极其困难。因此，这两章的经验材料更适合被理解为 pattern matching

或 theory-consistency checking，而不是决定性地裁判 competing causal explanations。 （第11–12

页，第91页，第96–97页）
