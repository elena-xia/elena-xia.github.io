Week 3 · Material–Technological lane · Pristine states (S1)

Robert C. Allen (1997), “Agriculture and the Origins of the State in Ancient Egypt.”艾伦（1997）《农业与古埃及国家的起源》。

Comparative historyDeductive theory

Dependent variable (DV)

The main dependent variable is the speed, stability, and territorial scope of state formation.

Specifically, Allen focuses on three core questions: First, why did Egypt form a unified state only about 1000 years after adopting agriculture, while other parts of the Near East experienced a lag of perhaps 5000 years (p. 138)? Second, why was the Egyptian state able to govern a broad territory from the First or Second Cataract /ˈkætərækt/ to the Delta /ˈdeltə/ for 3000 years, while other ancient empires were relatively short-lived and unstable (p. 138)? Third, why did Egypt form a territorial state rather than a city-state system (pp. 137-138)?

主要因变量: 国家形成的速度、稳定性与地域范围

具体而言，Allen关注三个核心问题：第一，为何埃及在采纳农业后仅约1000年就形成了统一国家，而近东其他地区则经历了约5000年的滞后期（第138页）；第二，为何埃及国家能够统治从第一或第二瀑布到三角洲的广大领土长达3000年，而其他古代帝国则相对短命不稳定（第138页）；第三，为何埃及形成了一个领土型国家而非城邦体系（第137-138页）。

Independent variable (IV)

The main independent variable is geographical circumscription and the characteristics of agricultural production.

Allen emphasizes that the desert terrain on both sides of the Nile /naɪl/ is the key to understanding Egyptian state formation. This geographical environment limited the habitation range of the population, making it easier for rulers to control labor mobility (p. 137). At the same time, the storability of agriculture, particularly grain agriculture, made taxation and exploitation possible, a characteristic that the foraging economy did not possess (p. 140).

主要自变量: 地理环境的“封闭性”（geographical circumscription）与农业生产的特性

Allen强调，尼罗河两岸的沙漠地形是理解埃及国家形成的关键。这种地理环境限制了人口的居住范围，使得统治者更容易控制劳动力流动（第137页）。同时，农业，特别是谷物农业，的可储存性（storability）使得税收和剥削成为可能，这是游牧采集经济所不具备的特征（第140页）。

Stateness

Allen conceptualizes the “state” as an exploitation mechanism, i.e., an institutional arrangement through which a non-productive elite extracts surplus from agricultural producers. He explicitly states that the main function of the Pharaonic /feɪˈrɒnɪk/ regime was “to redistribute a considerable fraction of the income produced by Egyptian farmers to a non-productive aristocracy” (p. 139).

In terms of measurement, Allen primarily measures the existence and capacity of the state through the following indicators: First, the timing of establishment and duration of unified rule; second, the scope of territorial control; third, labor conscription capacity (such as the corvée /kɔːrˈveɪ/ mobilization required for pyramid construction) (p. 148); fourth, the coverage of the taxation system, particularly the biennial cattle census and the nationwide network of taxation (p. 148).

Allen将“国家”概念化为一种剥削机制，即一个非生产性的精英阶层从农业生产者手中榨取剩余的制度安排。他明确指出，法老政权的主要功能是“将埃及农民生产的相当部分收入重新分配给非生产性的贵族阶层”（第139页）。

在测量方面，Allen主要通过以下指标来衡量国家的存在和能力：第一，统一政权的建立时间和持续时间；第二，领土控制范围；第三，劳动力征用能力（如金字塔建造所需的劳役动员）（第148页）；第四，税收体系的覆盖程度，特别是两年一度的牲畜普查和全国性的税收网络（第148页）。

Core argument

Allen’s core argument is that the combination of geographical circumscription and the storability of agricultural production enabled Egypt to form a unified state earlier and more stably than other regions of the Near East.

The logical chain of the argument is as follows: First, agricultural production, especially grain production, created a “production surplus” and a “labor surplus,” which are prerequisites for any stratified society (p. 139). However, the existence of surplus alone was not sufficient to lead to state formation, because in a “frontier economy” with abundant land and sparse population, farmers could escape exploitation by migrating (p. 145).

Egypt’s uniqueness lay in its geographical circumscription. The deserts on both sides of the Nile limited habitation to a narrow strip along the river valley, which meant that farmers wishing to escape their rulers had virtually nowhere else to go (pp. 137, 147). This geographical condition greatly reduced the cost for rulers to control labor, enabling exploitation systems to be established and maintained. Allen draws on Carneiro’s /kɑːrˈneɪroʊ/ “circumscription theory” but makes an important modification: the key was not that population pressure led to warfare, but that geographical circumscription reduced the cost of labor control (p. 137).

Allen的核心论点是：地理封闭性与农业生产的可储存性相结合，使得埃及比近东其他地区更早、更稳定地形成了统一国家。

论点的逻辑链条如下：首先，农业生产，尤其是谷物生产，创造了“生产剩余”（production surplus）和“劳动剩余”（labor surplus），这是任何分层社会的前提条件（第139页）。然而，仅有剩余并不足以导致国家形成，因为在土地丰裕、人口稀少的“边疆经济”（frontier economy）中，农民可以通过迁移来逃避剥削（第145页）。

埃及的独特之处在于其地理封闭性。尼罗河两岸的沙漠将居住区限制在河谷狭长地带，这意味着想要逃避统治者的农民几乎没有其他可去之处（第137页、第147页）。这种地理条件大大降低了统治者控制劳动力的成本，使得剥削制度得以建立和维持。Allen引用Carneiro的“封闭理论”（circumscription theory），但对其进行了重要修正：关键不在于人口压力导致战争，而在于地理封闭性降低了劳动力控制的成本（第137页）。

Mechanisms

Allen proposes several interrelated causal mechanisms:

Mechanism One: The storability of agriculture makes exploitation possible

In a foraging economy, food mainly consisted of perishable items such as fish and tubers /ˈtjuːbəz/; even if tax collectors could collect this food, they could not preserve or transport it for long periods, so “taxing them was pointless” (p. 140). Agriculture, particularly grain agriculture, changed this situation. Grain could be stored for years, meaning it could be transported to the capital to support the court, supply the army, and sustain large construction projects (p. 142). Allen notes that this storability meant that “when Egyptian foragers became farmers, they became targets for tax and rent collectors” (p. 142).

Mechanism Two: The seasonality of labor creates a mobilizable labor surplus

Egyptian agriculture was highly seasonal due to the Nile flood. From summer to autumn, farmland was inundated, and farmers had little to do except tend to their animals. This period, the flood season, was when the Pharaoh mobilized labor to build pyramids and other large projects (p. 143). In contrast, although foragers might have had free time each day, they had to remain near their resource locations throughout the year and could not be mobilized to distant places for construction work (p. 140).

Mechanism Three: Geographical circumscription limits labor mobility

Allen elaborates on the difficulty of exploitation in a “frontier economy.” When land was abundant, farmers could escape unsatisfactory circumstances by migrating to empty land, making it difficult for landlords to collect high rents (p. 145). The experience of the American and Canadian West during the frontier period demonstrated this: because there was abundant cheap or free land, the region was dominated by owner-occupying farmers rather than rent-paying tenants (p. 146).

However, Egypt’s desert terrain changed this logic. Between 15,000 and 3,000 B.C., the desert allowed at most some pastoral activities, and a drier trend beginning ca. 3400 B.C. further eliminated these opportunities (p. 147). If farmers wanted to escape from rulers in Upper Egypt /ˈʌpər ˈiːdʒɪpt/, they could only migrate downstream along the river, eventually into the Delta. This meant that emerging states had to expand their territory downstream to prevent population loss, and the ultimate result was Upper Egypt’s conquest of Lower Egypt /ˈloʊər ˈiːdʒɪpt/ to form a unified state (p. 148).

Mechanism Four: A unified state is the most effective tool for exploitation

Allen argues that in a sparsely populated frontier economy, only a unified state could effectively exploit farmers. If there were multiple competing landlords or states, farmers could lower rents by threatening to migrate; competing landowners would offer favorable terms to attract settlers (pp. 148-149). The experience of medieval Eastern Europe showed that when empty land existed, lords had to attract peasants with favorable terms (p. 149). Only a unified state could implement a uniform taxation system that prevented farmers from escaping taxation through migration (p. 148).

Allen提出了多个相互关联的因果机制：

机制一：农业的可储存性使剥削成为可能

在采集经济中，食物主要是鱼类和块茎等易腐烂物品，税收官员即使征收到这些食物也无法长期保存和运输，因此“征税毫无意义”（第140页）。农业，特别是谷物农业，改变了这一状况。谷物可以储存数年，这意味着它可以被运送到首都供养宫廷、供给军队、支持大型建设项目（第142页）。Allen指出，这种可储存性使得“当埃及采集者变成农民时，他们就成为了税吏和地租征收者的目标”（第142页）。

机制二：劳动的季节性创造了可动员的劳动剩余

埃及农业因尼罗河洪水而具有高度季节性。从夏季到秋季，农田被洪水淹没，农民除了照看牲畜外几乎无事可做。这段时间，洪水季节，正是法老动员劳动力修建金字塔和其他大型工程的时机（第143页）。相比之下，采集者虽然每天可能有空闲时间，但他们必须整年留在资源所在地附近，无法被调动到遥远的地方从事建设工作（第140页）。

机制三：地理封闭性限制了劳动力流动

Allen详细论述了“边疆经济”中剥削的困难。当土地丰裕时，农民可以通过迁移到空地来逃避不满意的境况，这使得地主难以收取高额地租（第145页）。美洲和加拿大西部在边疆时期的经验证明了这一点：由于存在大量廉价或免费的土地，那里主要是自耕农而非租佃农（第146页）。

然而，埃及的沙漠地形改变了这一逻辑。公元前15000年至3000年间，沙漠最多只允许一些牧业活动，而约公元前3400年开始的更干旱趋势进一步消除了这些机会（第147页）。农民若想逃离上埃及的统治者，只能沿河向下游，最终进入三角洲，迁移。这意味着新兴国家必须向下游扩张领土以阻止人口流失，最终结果就是上埃及征服下埃及，形成统一国家（第148页）。

机制四：统一国家是最有效的剥削工具

Allen论证，在人口稀少的边疆经济中，只有统一国家才能有效地剥削农民。如果存在多个相互竞争的地主或国家，农民可以通过威胁迁移来压低地租；竞争的土地所有者会为吸引定居者而提供优惠条件（第148-149页）。中世纪欧洲东部的经验表明，当存在空地时，领主不得不以优厚条件吸引农民（第149页）。只有统一的国家能够实施统一的税收制度，使农民无法通过迁移来逃避税负（第148页）。

Data & methods

Data

Allen’s argument relies primarily on secondary literature from archaeology, anthropology, and history, rather than on original datasets.

Geographic Coverage: The study focuses primarily on the Nile River valley in Egypt, while conducting comparative analysis with Sumer, the Levant, Syria, Anatolia, northern Mesopotamia, and the Zagros Mountains region (p. 150).

Time Period: From ca. 6000 B.C. (introduction of agriculture to Egypt) to ca. 150 B.C. (population peak in the Ptolemaic /ˌtɒləˈmeɪɪk/ era), with a focus on the Predynastic period to the Old Kingdom.

Main Data Sources:

Butzer’s /ˈbʊtsər/ (1976) research on population estimates: approximately 350,000 around 4000 B.C., about 1 million by 1800 B.C., and approximately 5 million in the Ptolemaic era (p. 145)

Wetterstrom’s /ˈwetərstrɒm/ (1993) research on the foraging economy and early agriculture in the Nile basin

Bard’s /bɑːrd/ (1994a, 1994b) analysis of mortuary evidence and social stratification in the Predynastic period (p. 146)

Estimate of harvestable land area along the Nile: approximately 16,100 square kilometers (p. 145)

Unit of Analysis: Primarily comparative analysis at the level of civilizations or regions, while also involving analysis of modes of production (foraging vs. agriculture) and forms of political organization (village society vs. state).

Allen的论证主要依赖于考古学、人类学和历史学的二手文献资料，而非原始数据集。

地理覆盖范围: 研究主要集中于埃及尼罗河流域，同时与苏美尔（Sumer /ˈsuːmər/）、黎凡特（Levant /ləˈvænt/）、叙利亚（Syria /ˈsɪriə/）、安纳托利亚（Anatolia /ˌænəˈtoʊliə/）、美索不达米亚北部（northern Mesopotamia /ˌmesəpəˈteɪmiə/）以及扎格罗斯山脉（Zagros /ˈzæɡrɒs/ Mountains）地区进行比较分析（第150页）。

时间跨度: 从公元前约6000年（农业引入埃及）到公元前约150年（托勒密时代人口达峰值），重点关注前王朝时期（Predynastic period /ˌpriːdaɪˈnæstɪk/）至古王国时期（Old Kingdom）。

主要数据来源:

Butzer（1976）关于人口估计的研究：公元前4000年约35万人，到公元前1800年约100万人，托勒密时代约500万人（第145页）

Wetterstrom（1993）关于尼罗河流域采集经济和早期农业的研究

Bard（1994a, 1994b）关于前王朝时期墓葬证据和社会分层的分析（第146页）

尼罗河可收获土地面积估计：约16,100平方公里（第145页）

分析单位: 主要以文明或地区为单位进行比较，同时也涉及生产方式（采集vs.农业）和政治组织形式（村落社会vs.国家）的分析。

Methods

Allen employs comparative historical analysis combined with deductive reasoning.

Methodological Characteristics: The author first critically reviews existing theories of state origins, including Childe’s /tʃaɪld/ “surplus theory,” Wittfogel’s /ˈwɪtfəʊɡəl/ “hydraulic hypothesis,” population pressure theory, trade theory, ecosystem theory, and Carneiro’s “circumscription theory”, and then synthesizes insights from these theories to propose his own explanatory framework (pp. 135-137).

Comparative Cases: The contrast between Egypt and Sumer, as well as the Levant-Syria-Anatolia region, constitutes the main comparative framework. Allen argues that Sumer’s situation differed from Egypt because population expansion there was achieved through immigration, and geographical circumscription was less significant than in Egypt (p. 150). In regions such as the Levant, states did not form for thousands of years after the advent of agriculture, demonstrating that agriculture itself does not automatically lead to social complexity (p. 150).

Key Assumptions:

Rational behavior assumption: Both farmers and rulers are assumed to be rational actors who make decisions based on cost-benefit calculations (such as migration decisions)

Technological conditions assumption: Ancient rulers did not have the surveillance and coercive technologies of modern states, so the impact of geographical environment on control capacity was more critical

Necessity of economic surplus assumption: Any stratified society requires some form of surplus to maintain a non-productive class

Allen采用的是历史比较分析方法（comparative historical analysis）结合演绎推理。

方法特征: 作者首先批判性地回顾了现有的国家起源理论，包括Childe的“剩余论”、Wittfogel的“水利假说”、人口压力论、贸易论、生态系统论以及Carneiro的“封闭论”，然后综合各理论的洞见，提出自己的解释框架（第135-137页）。

比较案例: 埃及与苏美尔、黎凡特-叙利亚-安纳托利亚地区的对比构成了主要的比较框架。Allen论证，苏美尔的情况不同于埃及，因为那里的人口扩张是通过移民实现的，地理封闭性的作用不如埃及显著（第150页）。而黎凡特等地区在农业出现后数千年都未形成国家，这表明农业本身不会自动导致社会复杂化（第150页）。

关键假设:

理性行为假设：农民和统治者都被假定为理性行为者，会根据成本-收益计算做出决策（如迁移决策）

技术条件假设：古代统治者没有现代国家的监控和强制技术，因此地理环境对控制能力的影响更为关键

经济剩余的必要性假设：任何分层社会都需要某种形式的剩余才能维持非生产性阶层
