Dependent variable (DV)

The paper has multiple interconnected dependent variables, all revolving around state fiscal capacity and its decline. The most important is the Qing /tʃɪŋ/ state’s tax revenue over time, measured in both nominal silver taels, real terms (converted to rice equivalents), and per capita terms. The author shows that the Qing state’s real tax revenue peaked early in the eighteenth century and then contracted steadily even as the economy continued to grow (p. 109). Beyond aggregate revenue, the paper also examines the spatial distribution of land tax per capita across prefectures and the density of administrative units (number of counties per 10,000 km²), both measured circa 1820. Finally, the paper treats the decline in public goods provision, military strength and local administrative capacity, as downstream consequences of fiscal contraction (pp. 119–122). In sum, the dependent variable is fundamentally about why a large, stable, and outwardly powerful autocratic state was fiscally weak and grew progressively weaker over time, and what this fiscal weakness meant for China’s ability to respond to the crises of the nineteenth century.

本文有多个相互关联的因变量，全部围绕国家财政能力及其衰退展开。最核心的因变量是清朝（Qing /tʃɪŋ/）国家的税收收入随时间的变化，以名义白银两、实际值（折算为大米等价物）和人均值三种方式衡量。作者表明，清朝的实际税收收入在十八世纪初达到峰值，此后即使经济持续增长，税收却稳步萎缩（第109页）。除总收入外，本文还考察了1820年前后各府的人均土地税的空间分布以及行政单位密度（每万平方公里的县数量）。最后，本文将公共物品供给的下降，军事力量与地方行政能力，视为财政萎缩的下游后果（第119至122页）。总之，因变量从根本上关注的是：为何一个庞大、稳定、外表强大的专制国家在财政上是虚弱的，并且随时间日益衰弱，以及这种财政虚弱对中国应对十九世纪危机的能力意味着什么。

Independent variable (IV)

The main independent variable is the size of the Chinese empire, understood in two dimensions: geographic size and economic size. Geographic size refers to the physical extent of the territory that the emperor must govern from the capital Beijing /beɪˈdʒɪŋ/. It determines the severity of information asymmetry between the ruler (the principal) and his agents (local officials) scattered across the empire. The author operationalizes geographic size as the distance from each prefecture to Beijing, measured along imperial postal relay routes (p. 114). Economic size refers to the aggregate output of the economy, \(Y=AN\), where \(A\) is agricultural output per capita and \(N\) is population. As the economy grows through population expansion, the Chinese population more than doubled from less than 150 million to over 400 million between 1650 and 1850 (p. 109, footnote 6), the economic stakes at every administrative node increase, intensifying agents’ incentives for corruption. Hence, the independent variable is more than static “bigness.” It is a dynamic interaction between territorial extent and economic expansion that progressively worsened the principal-agent problem over time.

主要自变量是中华帝国的规模，从两个维度理解：地理规模和经济规模。地理规模指的是皇帝必须从首都北京（Beijing /beɪˈdʒɪŋ/）治理的疆域物理范围。它决定了统治者（委托人）与散布帝国各地的代理人（地方官员）之间信息不对称的严重程度。作者将地理规模操作化为各府到北京的距离，沿帝国驿道路线度量（第114页）。经济规模指的是经济的总产出，\(Y=AN\)，其中 \(A\) 为人均农业产出，\(N\) 为人口数量。随着经济通过人口扩张而增长，中国人口从1650年不足1.5亿增长到1850年超过4亿（第109页，注释6），每个行政节点的经济利益增加，加剧了代理人的腐败动机。因此，自变量不仅仅是静态意义上的“庞大”，而是领土幅员与经济扩张之间的动态交互，随时间推移不断恶化委托，代理问题。

Stateness

Sng conceptualizes the Chinese state as a large, stable dictatorship governed through a centralized, hierarchical bureaucracy, not as the kind of despotic Leviathan that older scholarship (e.g., Wittfogel /ˈvɪtfəʊɡəl/, 1957; Balazs /ˈbɑːlɑːʒ/, 1964) described. Building on Mancur Olson /ˈmæŋkər ˈoʊlsən/’s (1993) theory of the stationary bandit, Sng treats the emperor as a rational ruler with an encompassing interest in the long-term prosperity of his domain, he understands that excessive exaction reduces future revenues and provokes rebellion. The problem is not that the ruler is predatory, but that his agents are. The state is therefore conceptualized primarily through the lens of the principal-agent problem: the emperor is the principal who sets tax rates and attempts to monitor his bureaucracy; the magistrates, clerks, runners, and local gentry are the agents who collect taxes on his behalf but have private incentives to extort taxpayers for personal gain (pp. 107–108, 111).

Sng measures “stateness” or state capacity in several concrete ways. First, fiscal capacity is measured as total tax revenue, reconstructed from official Qing documents including the Collected Statutes of the Qing Dynasty, the Veritable Records, and archival records from the National Library of China (p. 117). Second, administrative capacity is measured as the density of county-level administrative units (counties per 10,000 km²) and the ratio of officials to population, around 20,000 ranked officials for a population exceeding 300 million in 1800, or roughly one official per 15,000 people (p. 113). Third, coercive capacity is proxied by troop deployment data, specifically the statutory number of Green Standard Army soldiers in the provinces (p. 122, Table 6). In all three dimensions, Sng shows that the Chinese state was remarkably thin. This picture stands in sharp contrast to the traditional “Oriental Despotism” image and aligns instead with a growing consensus among Chinese historians that the Qing state was a “night watchman state” that governed minimally and administered sparingly (p. 120).

This conceptualization is closely connected to the broader state formation literature. Unlike the European experience analyzed by Charles Tilly /ˈtʃɑːrlz ˈtɪli/, where interstate warfare drove state-building and fiscal extraction (Tilly, 1990), Sng’s China faced a peaceful external environment during the eighteenth century, which reduced the pressure to build fiscal capacity (p. 119). And unlike the European absolutist states analyzed by Dincecco /dɪnˈtʃɛkoʊ/ (2011) and Hoffman /ˈhɒfmən/ and Rosenthal /ˈroʊzənθɔːl/ (1997), where institutional fragmentation limited the extractive power of kings, China had overcome jurisdictional fragmentation quite early through a nonhereditary bureaucracy selected by civil service examinations (p. 108, footnote 4). The puzzle, then, is why a politically unified empire with no institutional fragmentation remained fiscally weak, and the answer, Sng argues, lies in the principal-agent problem that size imposed.

孙端辉将中国国家概念化为一个大型、稳定的独裁政权，通过中央集权的层级官僚体系进行治理，而非旧有学术文献（如魏特夫 Wittfogel /ˈvɪtfəʊɡəl/，1957；白乐日 Balazs /ˈbɑːlɑːʒ/，1964）所描述的那种专制利维坦。在曼瑟·奥尔森（Mancur Olson /ˈmæŋkər ˈoʊlsən/，1993）“驻足匪帮”（stationary bandit）理论基础上，孙端辉将皇帝视为一个理性统治者，对其领地的长期繁荣拥有涵盖性利益，他明白过度榨取会减少未来收入并激发叛乱。问题不在于统治者是掠夺性的，而在于他的代理人是。因此，国家主要通过委托，代理问题的视角被概念化：皇帝是设定税率并试图监督官僚的委托人；知县、胥吏、衙役和地方绅士则是代他征税但有私人动机向纳税人勒索敛财的代理人（第107至108页，第111页）。

孙端辉以几种具体方式度量“国家性”或国家能力。第一，财政能力以总税收收入衡量，根据清朝官方文献重建，包括《大清会典》、《清实录》以及中国国家图书馆的档案记录（第117页）。第二，行政能力以县级行政单位密度（每万平方公里的县数量）和官民比衡量，1800年约有20,000名有品级官员治理超过3亿人口，约每15,000人对应一名官员（第113页）。第三，强制能力以军队部署数据代理，具体为各省绿营兵的编制人数（第122页，表6）。在所有三个维度上，孙端辉都表明中国国家极为单薄。这幅图景与传统的“东方专制主义”形象形成鲜明对比，而与中国历史学家日益形成的共识一致：清朝是一个治理极简、行政俭省的“守夜人国家”（第120页）。

这一概念化与更广泛的国家形成文献密切相关。与查尔斯·蒂利（Charles Tilly /ˈtʃɑːrlz ˈtɪli/，1990）分析的欧洲经验不同，在欧洲，国际间战争推动了国家建设和财政汲取，孙端辉笔下的中国在十八世纪面临和平的外部环境，这降低了建设财政能力的压力（第119页）。与丁切科（Dincecco /dɪnˈtʃɛkoʊ/，2011）以及霍夫曼（Hoffman /ˈhɒfmən/）和罗森塔尔（Rosenthal /ˈroʊzənθɔːl/，1997）分析的欧洲绝对君主制国家也不同，在那些国家，制度碎片化限制了国王的榨取权力，中国很早就通过科举选拔的非世袭官僚制克服了管辖碎片化（第108页，注释4）。那么谜题就是：为何一个政治统一、没有制度碎片化的帝国仍然财政虚弱，答案，孙端辉论证道，在于规模所施加的委托，代理问题。

Core argument

The core argument is that China’s vast territorial size created a severe principal-agent problem that constrained the state’s fiscal capacity, and economic expansion during the prosperous eighteenth century paradoxically worsened the problem, causing fiscal decline that sowed the seeds of the nineteenth-century crises.

The argument proceeds in several steps. First, Sng challenges the traditional view that China’s economic stagnation was caused by an all-powerful, interventionist autocratic state that suppressed private enterprise (Wittfogel, 1957; Balazs, 1964). He points out that this view is theoretically problematic because the Chinese emperor, as a stable dictator with long time horizons, had rational incentives to restrain his exaction to preserve future revenue and maintain political stability, as Olson (1993) demonstrated (p. 107). The view is also empirically inconsistent with recent scholarship showing that the eighteenth-century Chinese economy was far more commercialized, market-integrated, and developed than previously assumed, as argued by Pomeranz /ˈpɒmərænts/ (2000), Shiue /ʃjuː/ and Keller /ˈkɛlər/ (2007), and others (p. 108).

Second, if the eighteenth-century economy was prosperous, why did China’s fortunes reverse in the nineteenth century? Sng proposes that the answer lies not in the ruler’s behavior but in the behavior of his agents. Reprising the earlier work of Kiser /ˈkaɪzər/ and Tong /tɒŋ/ (1992), Sng argues that in a large dictatorship, excessive exploitation comes not from the ruler, who has encompassing interests, but from his agents, who have shorter decision horizons and less encompassing interests. The agents have private incentives to extort the taxpayers for personal enrichment (p. 108).

Third, size exacerbates this problem through two channels. Geographically, the vast territory makes monitoring costly. Information must travel enormous distances, in 1853, news of Wuchang /ˈwuːtʃɑːŋ/’s fall to the Taiping /ˈtaɪˌpɪŋ/ rebels took eight days to reach Beijing, 1200 km away (p. 108). Regional diversity further complicates monitoring because local agents require flexibility to adapt central government decrees to local conditions, but that same flexibility provides cover for self-interested deviations (p. 108). Economically, as the economy grows, the rent-seeking incentives of agents increase because there is more surplus to extract. This puts pressure on the ruler to lower the tax rate to prevent the total effective burden (official tax plus unofficial extortion) from exceeding the rebellion threshold (p. 108).

Fourth, and most critically, Sng argues that if the principal-agent problem is sufficiently severe, economic expansion can actually hurt the ruler’s fiscal position. While economic growth expands the tax base (positive effect), it also increases corruption which forces the ruler to lower the tax rate (negative effect). Under severe agency costs, the negative effect eventually dominates the positive effect, producing a hump-shaped pattern: tax revenue first rises and then falls as the economy continues to grow. This is the paper’s most striking theoretical prediction, the “paradox of development” in which a regime that successfully fosters growth may become a victim of its own success (pp. 108, 113, 119).

核心论点是：中国辽阔的疆域造成了严重的委托，代理问题，制约了国家的财政能力，而十八世纪繁荣时期的经济扩张反而使问题悖论性地恶化，导致了财政衰退，为十九世纪的危机埋下了种子。

这一论点分几步推进。首先，孙端辉挑战了传统观点，即中国的经济停滞是由一个全能的、干预主义的专制国家压制私人企业所导致（魏特夫，1957；白乐日，1964）。他指出这一观点在理论上有问题，因为中国皇帝作为一个稳定的独裁者，有着长远的时间视野，有理性动机约束自己的榨取以保全未来收入和维持政治稳定，正如奥尔森（1993）所论证的（第107页）。这一观点在经验上也与近年学术研究不一致，这些研究表明，十八世纪的中国经济远比此前认为的更加商业化、市场整合和发达，如彭慕兰（Pomeranz /ˈpɒmərænts/，2000）、史志宏（Shiue /ʃjuː/）和凯勒（Keller /ˈkɛlər/，2007）等人所论证（第108页）。

其次，如果十八世纪经济繁荣，为何中国的命运在十九世纪逆转？孙端辉提出答案不在于统治者的行为，而在于其代理人的行为。延续基瑟（Kiser /ˈkaɪzər/）和佟（Tong /tɒŋ/，1992）的早期研究，孙端辉论证在大型独裁政权中，过度榨取不是来自统治者，统治者有涵盖性利益，而是来自他的代理人，代理人有更短的决策视野和更不具涵盖性的利益。代理人有私人动机向纳税人勒索以自肥（第108页）。

第三，规模通过两条渠道加剧了这一问题。在地理上，辽阔疆域使监督成本高昂。信息必须跨越巨大距离传递，1853年太平军（Taiping /ˈtaɪˌpɪŋ/）攻占武昌（Wuchang /ˈwuːtʃɑːŋ/）的消息花了八天才传到1200公里外的北京（第108页）。地域多样性进一步增加了监督难度，因为地方代理人需要灵活变通中央政府法令以适应当地情况，但这种灵活性同时也为自利偏离提供了掩护（第108页）。在经济上，随着经济增长，代理人的寻租激励加强，因为有更多的剩余可供榨取。这迫使统治者降低税率，以防止总有效负担（官方税加非官方勒索）超过叛乱门槛（第108页）。

第四，也是最关键的，孙端辉论证如果委托，代理问题足够严重，经济扩张实际上可能损害统治者的财政状况。虽然经济增长扩大了税基（正效应），但它同时增加了腐败，迫使统治者降低税率（负效应）。在严重的代理成本下，负效应最终压倒正效应，产生驼峰形模式：税收收入先升后降，即使经济继续增长。这是本文最引人注目的理论预测，“发展悖论”，即一个成功促进增长的政权可能成为自身成功的牺牲品（第108、113、119页）。

Mechanisms

The paper proposes a clear causal chain with several linked mechanisms:

Mechanism 1: Information asymmetry from geographic distance. The ruler resides in Beijing and must rely on agents stationed across a vast empire to collect taxes and administer justice. Distance increases the cost and time of transmitting information. Regional diversity compounds the problem because local agents must exercise discretion, and the ruler cannot easily distinguish between an agent who deviates for legitimate local reasons and one who deviates for personal gain (p. 108). The formal model captures this through a monitoring parameter \(m\), where the probability of detecting extortion \(\epsilon\) is \(m\epsilon^2\). A larger territory implies a smaller \(m\) for distant regions, meaning the ruler’s audit is less effective (p. 112).

Mechanism 2: Agent extortion (“tyranny at the bottom”). Given weak monitoring, the agent’s optimal extortion rate is:

\[ \epsilon^*=\frac{Y}{2mX}. \]

Here \(Y\) is aggregate output and \(X\) is the punishment for getting caught (p. 112, Eq. 1). This means extortion increases with the size of the economy and decreases with monitoring capacity. The representative agent in the model embodies the magistrate, county clerks, runners, and local gentry, all of whom had historical incentives and opportunities to exploit taxpayers, for example, by manipulating tax documents, imposing unauthorized surcharges, or engaging in tax arbitrage (pp. 110–111).

Mechanism 3: The ruler’s constrained response, low taxes. The ruler understands that the total effective tax burden on the peasants is \(\tau+\epsilon\) (official tax rate plus extortion rate). If this exceeds a rebellion threshold \(r\), the peasants revolt. Because the ruler wants to avoid revolt at all costs, he sets:

\[ \tau^*=r-\frac{Y}{2mX}. \]

When monitoring is weak (\(m\) is small), equilibrium extortion is high (\(\epsilon^*\) is large), so the ruler must set the official tax rate low. This is Proposition 1: “a severe principal-agent problem leads to a (fiscally) weak state” (p. 112). It explains the paradox that official taxes in Qing China were low while the effective burden on peasants was relatively heavy.

Mechanism 4: Small bureaucracy. When the ruler extends the model to choose the number of counties \((\beta)\), the equilibrium condition

\[ \tau Y=C'(\beta) \]

means that a low equilibrium tax rate implies a smaller bureaucracy, because the marginal benefit of adding another county is too small to justify the cost (p. 113, Eq. 3). This is Proposition 2: “a severe principal-agent problem leads to a small bureaucracy” (p. 113). Historically, there were only about 20,000 ranked officials governing over 300 million people (p. 113), and this extraordinary thinness of the administrative apparatus is predicted by the model.

Mechanism 5: The dynamic trap, economic growth worsening fiscal capacity. As the population \(N\) grows, extortion \(\epsilon^*\) rises \((d\epsilon^*/dN>0)\) and the tax rate \(\tau^*\) falls \((d\tau^*/dN<0)\). When \(m\) is small enough, the negative effect (falling tax rate) eventually overwhelms the positive effect (growing tax base), and total tax revenue

\[ V_R=\tau^*\beta Y \]

follows a hump-shaped pattern (p. 113). This is Proposition 3: “if the principal-agent problem is severe, economic expansion will initially increase the tax revenue but eventually depress it” (p. 113). This mechanism explains why the Qing state’s real tax revenue peaked early in the eighteenth century and then declined steadily through the prosperous High Qing period, long before the military and social upheavals of the nineteenth century.

Mechanism 6: Fiscal decline leading to reduced public goods and vulnerability. The sustained revenue contraction led to cuts in military spending (troop numbers declined in southern provinces before the Opium War) and administrative underinvestment (the number of counties barely increased despite a population that more than doubled). Understaffed and underfunded magistracies could not maintain social order, especially in fast-growing frontier areas where communal violence was common (p. 122). The White Lotus Rebellion (1796–1804), the Taiping Rebellion (1850–64), and China’s defeat in the Opium War (1839–42) were all consequences, not causes, of this long-term fiscal and administrative decline (pp. 119–123).

本文提出了一条由多个环节链接的清晰因果链：

机制1：地理距离导致的信息不对称。 统治者驻扎在北京，必须依靠部署在广袤帝国各地的代理人征税和司法。距离增加了信息传递的成本和时间。地域多样性使问题更加复杂，因为地方代理人必须行使裁量权，而统治者难以区分一个因合理的地方原因而偏离的代理人与一个为私利而偏离的代理人（第108页）。正式模型通过监督参数 \(m\) 捕捉这一点，发现勒索 \(\epsilon\) 的概率为 \(m\epsilon^2\)。更大的领土意味着对偏远地区而言 \(m\) 更小，即统治者的审计效力更弱（第112页）。

机制2：代理人勒索（“底层暴政”）。在监督薄弱的情况下，代理人的最优勒索率为：

\[ \epsilon^*=\frac{Y}{2mX}. \]

其中 \(Y\) 是总产出，\(X\) 是被抓获的惩罚（第112页，方程1）。这意味着勒索随经济规模增大而增加，随监督能力增强而减少。模型中的代表性代理人体现了知县、胥吏、衙役和地方绅士，他们在历史上都有剥削纳税人的动机和机会，例如，操纵税务文件、征收未经授权的附加费、或从事税收套利（第110至111页）。

机制3：统治者的受限回应，低税率。统治者明白农民承受的总有效税负是 \(\tau+\epsilon\)（官方税率加勒索率）。如果超过叛乱门槛 \(r\)，农民就会造反。因为统治者不惜一切代价避免叛乱，他设定：

\[ \tau^*=r-\frac{Y}{2mX}. \]

当监督薄弱（\(m\) 小）时，均衡勒索率高（\(\epsilon^*\) 大），所以统治者必须设定低官方税率。这就是命题1：“严重的委托，代理问题导致（财政上）虚弱的国家”（第112页）。它解释了清代中国官方税收低而农民实际负担却相对沉重的悖论。

机制4：小官僚机构。当统治者将模型扩展到选择县的数量（\(\beta\)）时，均衡条件

\[ \tau Y=C'(\beta) \]

意味着低均衡税率对应更小的官僚机构，因为增设一个县的边际收益太小，不足以抵偿成本（第113页，方程3）。这就是命题2：“严重的委托，代理问题导致小官僚机构”（第113页）。历史上，仅约20,000名有品级官员治理超过3亿人口（第113页），这种行政机器的非凡单薄正是模型所预测的。

Methods

The paper employs a combination of formal modeling, cross-sectional regression analysis, time-series construction, and historical narrative.

Formal model. The core theoretical contribution is a simple game-theoretic model involving three players: the ruler (principal), a representative tax agent, and a peasant population. The ruler sets the tax rate \(\tau\), the agent chooses an extortion level \(\varepsilon\), and the peasants revolt if the combined burden \(\tau+\varepsilon\) exceeds a threshold \(r\). The ruler audits the agent after tax collection; the probability of catching the agent is \(m\varepsilon^2\). The model is solved by backward induction and yields closed-form solutions for the equilibrium tax rate, extortion rate, and bureaucratic size. The model generates three propositions: (1) a severe principal-agent problem leads to low taxes and a weak state; (2) it leads to a small bureaucracy; (3) under severe agency costs, economic expansion eventually depresses tax revenue (pp. 111–113).

Cross-sectional regressions. To test Propositions 1 and 2, the author estimates log-linear regressions (Eq. 4, p. 114) where the dependent variable is either log(tax per capita) or log(counties per 10,000 km²), and the key regressor is distance from Beijing (in 1000 km). Standard errors are clustered by province to address potential heteroskedasticity. The baseline OLS results show that every 1000 km increase in distance from Beijing is associated with a statistically significant decrease in both land tax per capita and administrative density (Table 3, p. 117). The author addresses endogeneity concerns (that postal routes might have been routed through heavily taxed regions) in two ways: first, by showing via GIS analysis that the hypothetical slope-dependent least-cost road network closely resembles the historical imperial road system, suggesting the road system was determined mainly by physical geography rather than human choices (p. 116); second, by using the straight-line distance between each prefecture and Beijing as an instrument for road distance in IV regressions. The IV results are stable: every 1000 km increase in distance is associated with a 25.9% decrease in tax per capita and a 25.7% decrease in county density (Table 4, p. 118). Multiple robustness checks are performed, including restricting the sample to the “developed area” in the east, dropping outlier prefectures with extremely low taxes (which had experienced recent rebellions), restricting the sample to prefectures within 1500 km of Beijing, adding dummy variables for Yellow River proximity and southern provinces, using linear instead of log-linear specifications, and using alternative measures of distance, tax, and administrative density (Tables 4 and A1, pp. 118, 124–125).

The key assumptions for these methods to be valid are: (a) that the instrument (straight-line distance) satisfies the exclusion restriction, that it affects tax per capita and county density only through its effect on road distance (and hence monitoring difficulty), not through other channels. This is plausible but not beyond challenge, as straight-line distance from the capital could correlate with other factors such as economic geography, proximity to foreign threats, or historical patterns of settlement. (b) That the distance-based measure adequately captures monitoring difficulty, which conflates geographic distance with other correlated factors. (c) That the cross-sectional data from 1820 reflects long-run equilibrium patterns rather than short-run shocks. (d) That the relationship is not driven by reverse causation (e.g., the state taxing more in areas where it already had more administrative presence, rather than the other way around).

Time-series reconstruction. For Proposition 3, the author reconstructed Qing tax revenue year by year from 1650 to 1850. The reconstruction draws on both national-level aggregates from the Veritable Records and more reliable provincial-level breakdowns from other official sources. The real revenue series is obtained by deflating with rice prices; per capita revenue is computed using population estimates. The resulting figure (Fig. 3, p. 120) shows a clear hump-shaped pattern: nominal revenue rose steadily but real revenue peaked around the early 1700s and then declined, while per capita revenue fell more precipitously, from enough to feed and clothe 9.6% of the population in 1685 to only 2.3% in 1848. This is descriptive rather than causal, but the pattern is striking and directly consistent with Proposition 3.

Historical narrative. The final empirical component uses qualitative historical evidence to trace the consequences of fiscal decline. This includes analysis of troop deployment records, imperial edicts revealing the emperors’ awareness of military weaknesses (Emperor Qianlong /tʃjɛnˈlʊŋ/’s 1793 directive after the Macartney /məˈkɑːrtni/ Mission, Emperor Daoguang /daʊˈɡwɑːŋ/’s 1834 lament), the stagnation of the county system, and the geographical origins of major rebellions in undergovernment frontier areas (pp. 119–123). The historical narrative is not formally tested, but it works as process-tracing evidence linking fiscal decline to concrete policy outcomes and eventual state failure.

形式化模型。 核心理论贡献是一个涉及三个参与者的简单博弈论模型：统治者（委托人）、一个代表性税务代理人和农民群体。统治者设定税率 \(\tau\)，代理人选择勒索水平 \(\varepsilon\)，如果合计负担 \(\tau+\varepsilon\) 超过门槛 \(r\)，农民就造反。统治者在税收征收后审计代理人；抓获代理人的概率为 \(m\varepsilon^2\)。模型通过逆向归纳法求解，得出均衡税率、勒索率和官僚规模的闭式解。模型产生三个命题：(1) 严重的委托，代理问题导致低税收和弱国家；(2) 导致小官僚机构；(3) 在严重的代理成本下，经济扩张最终压低税收（第111至113页）。

横截面回归。 为检验命题1和2，作者估计了对数线性回归（方程4，第114页），因变量为log(人均税收)或log(每万平方公里县数)，关键回归变量为到北京的距离（以千公里计）。标准误按省聚类以应对潜在异方差性。基线OLS结果表明，距北京每增加1000公里，人均土地税和行政密度均显著下降（表3，第117页）。作者通过两种方式解决内生性疑虑（驿道可能经过高税区）：首先，通过GIS分析表明假设的基于坡度的最低成本路网与历史帝国驿道系统高度相似，暗示驿道系统主要由自然地理而非人为选择决定（第116页）；其次，使用各府到北京的直线距离作为路线距离的工具变量进行IV回归。IV结果稳定：距离每增加1000公里，人均税收减少25.9%，县密度减少25.7%（表4，第118页）。进行了多项稳健性检验，包括将样本限制在东部“发达地区”、剔除税收极低的异常值府（这些府近期经历过叛乱）、将样本限制在距北京1500公里以内的府、为是否临近黄河和南方省份添加虚拟变量、使用线性而非对数线性模型、以及使用距离、税收和行政密度的替代度量（表4和表A1，第118、124至125页）。

这些方法有效性所依赖的关键假设是：（a）工具变量（直线距离）满足排除性限制，它仅通过影响道路距离（从而影响监督难度）来影响人均税收和县密度，不通过其他渠道。这是合理的但并非不可质疑，因为到首都的直线距离可能与其他因素相关，如经济地理、与外部威胁的距离、或历史上的定居模式。（b）基于距离的度量充分捕捉了监督难度，实际上这与其他相关因素混合在一起。（c）1820年的横截面数据反映的是长期均衡模式而非短期冲击。（d）关系不是由反向因果驱动的（例如，国家在已有更多行政存在的地方征收更多税收，而非反过来）。

时间序列重建。 对于命题3，作者逐年重建了1650至1850年的清朝税收。重建工作既利用了《清实录》的全国性汇总数据，也利用了其他官方来源中更可靠的省级分项数据。实际收入序列通过米价平减获得；人均收入使用人口估计值计算。得到的图表（图3，第120页）呈现明显的驼峰形模式：名义收入稳步上升，但实际收入约在1700年代初达到峰值后下降，而人均收入下降更为剧烈，从1685年足以养活9.6%人口降至1848年仅2.3%。这是描述性而非因果性的，但这一模式引人注目且与命题3直接一致。

历史叙事。 最后的经验部分使用定性历史证据追踪财政衰退的后果。包括分析军队部署记录、皇帝诏书中显示皇帝对军事弱点的认识（乾隆帝 Qianlong /tʃjɛnˈlʊŋ/ 1793年马戛尔尼 Macartney /məˈkɑːrtni/ 使团后的指令、道光帝 Daoguang /daʊˈɡwɑːŋ/ 1834年的感叹）、县制的停滞、以及主要叛乱发生在治理不足的边疆地区的地理起源（第119至123页）。历史叙事未经正式检验，但作为过程追踪证据，将财政衰退与具体政策后果和最终国家失败联系起来。

Core question: why did China experience economic and state decline in the nineteenth century after relative prosperity in the eighteenth century? During this period, state institutions appeared very stable; the only thing that changed was the growth of population and economic scale. Difference from Kiser: this article is not about the question of contract choice (not a choice between tax farming and bureaucracy), but about the ruler’s choice of tax rate and number of administrative jurisdictions given a bureaucratic system, and how the expansion of scale led to state decline by weakening monitoring capacity.

The article lacks clear, theory-derived “non-obvious observable implications” to distinguish it from alternative explanations. 1. Internal consistency check: a model forces you to make your assumptions explicit and avoid logical contradictions. Although philosophers and legal scholars can also maintain the internal consistency of an argument through other means, a model is an effective linguistic tool.

2. 3. 惩罚参数 x 是外生的：x 是国家对违规官僚的惩罚力度。但这应该是国家的选择变量：国家可以直接将 x 调到很高来解决问题。一旦允许 x 内生化，整个模型就会崩塌。归纳 vs 演绎：老师认为这篇文章虽然读起来像演绎研究（先有理论，再检验），但实际上更接近归纳：作者先知道了中国19世纪衰落的历史模式，然后构建模型来”合理化”这一模式。文章并没有明确的、从理论中衍生出的”非显而易见的可观察含义”来与替代解释区分。学生关于模型必要性的质疑（Speaker 2）：为什么要写一个模型？为什么不直接展示数据说”中国就是这样的”？

老师的系统性回应：正式模型的两种用途：

1. 内部一致性检查：模型迫使你明确假设、避免逻辑矛盾。虽然哲学家和法学家也能通过其他方式保持论证的内部一致性，但模型是一种有效的语言工具。

3. 学科社会学：老师还坦率地承认，有些文章使用正式模型来”掩饰”（obfuscate）经验分析的不足。在学科现实中，同时包含正式模型和经验分析的文章会同时吸引理论审稿人和经验审稿人，两者的期望往往相互矛盾，导致模型最终被放到附录中甚至删除。但即便如此，老师建议学生在研究初期尝试形式化自己的想法，即使不打算在文章中使用模型，哪怕只是用一句话写下你的论点，都是有价值的。来自中国史专家学生的批评（Speaker 7 和 Speaker 4）：Speaker 7：模型难以解释晚清面临英法入侵等巨大外部威胁时的行为。模型假设统治者主要关注内部叛乱威胁，但历史上北京已经被英法联军占领，统治者为何仍然不提高税率应对外部威胁？

Speaker 7 进一步补充：清朝低税率是中国史学界的重大争论。除了能力约束（capacity constraint），还有意识形态约束：儒家思想认为不应高税率。面对外部威胁仍坚持低税率，可能更好地用意识形态来解释。Speaker 4 提供了更深入的历史细节：历史学家秦晖指出，传统中国政治思想中没有”税收作为社会契约”的概念：税收是单向的从民到官的转移，提高税率不会带来更好的公共服务。清代经济已经从农业转向商业，但税基仍然停留在土地上。要改变税制结构需要重新丈量土地、分类，而朝廷缺乏资源和意愿做这件事。鸦片战争后清朝税收急剧增长，恰恰是因为外国人（赫德/Robert Hart）建立的海关系统：一个制度创新突破了传统土地税制的天花板。这说明关键不在于规模，而在于制度设计。将问题简化为”统治者是委托人、官员是代理人”过于简单：实际上存在多层次的委托-代理关系（书吏和衙役才是直接面对民众的”代理人”，而被朝廷派遣的官员反而是中间层的”委托人”）。

老师的总结评价：文章确实抓住了一个核心的委托-代理关系，但留下了大量未回答的问题：这是否是最有效的税种？这是否是最重要的代理关系？外部威胁为何被忽略？而且整个讨论中，监控参数 q 都是从天上掉下来的，这就引出了下两篇文献的主题。

  1. Point 1: The Spatial Gradient of State Capacity: SUPPLEMENTARY

    Sng’s argument: China’s geographic size created severe information asymmetries between the emperor in Beijing and his agents across the empire. Monitoring capacity declined with distance from the capital. Empirically, both land tax per capita and county density declined significantly with distance from Beijing, even after controlling for population density, elevation, and other factors. Every 1,000 km from Beijing was associated with a 25.9% lower land tax per capita and 25.7% fewer counties per 10,000 km² (pp. 114–118, Tables 3–4).

    Supplementary observation: Qin Hui’s analysis of China’s “big community standard” (大共同体本位) provides the institutional foundation for why this spatial gradient was so steep. From the Qin dynasty onward, the Legalist tradition systematically dissolved small communities (clans, village associations, guilds) that could have served as alternative channels of local governance and mutual monitoring. The state extended power directly to individual subjects (“编户齐民”), with no intermediate self-governing bodies to buffer or supplement the formal bureaucracy (Qin Hui, pp. 263, 269–271). In Western Europe, lords, parishes, and guilds partially performed governance functions at the local level, reducing the monarch’s reliance on a single chain of bureaucratic agents. In China, because all small communities had been suppressed, the emperor had no choice but to rely entirely on the formal bureaucratic chain. This made the monitoring problem that Sng identifies far worse than it would have been in a society with stronger local self-organization.

    Further evidence: Qin Hui shows that in regions where small communities survived, the southeastern provinces of Guangdong, Zhejiang, and Jiangsu, where clan organizations were most active, resistance to collectivization in the 1950s was notably stronger than in the North China Plain, where atomized smallholders lacked any organizational basis for collective action (pp. 266–267). This confirms that the presence or absence of intermediate social organizations directly affected the state’s ability to penetrate and control local society, a factor that Sng’s model, focused solely on geographic distance, does not capture.

    Sng的观点：中国的地理规模在北京的皇帝与帝国各地代理人之间制造了严重的信息不对称。监督能力随距首都的距离递减。经验上，即使控制了人口密度、海拔等因素，人均土地税和县密度都随距北京的距离增加而显著下降。每距北京1000公里，人均土地税降低25.9%，每万平方公里的县数量减少25.7%（第114至118页，表3至4）。

    补充性观察：秦晖对中国“大共同体本位”的分析，为这一空间梯度为何如此陡峭提供了制度基础。从秦朝开始，法家传统系统性地消解了小共同体（宗族、村社、行会），而这些组织本可以作为地方治理和互相监督的替代渠道。国家权力直接延伸到个体臣民（“编户齐民”），没有中间自治组织来缓冲或补充正式官僚体系（秦晖，第263、269至271页）。在西欧，领主、教区和行会在地方层面部分承担了治理功能，减少了君主对单一官僚代理人链条的依赖。在中国，由于所有小共同体被压制，皇帝别无选择只能完全依赖正式官僚链条。这使得Sng所识别的监督问题，比一个拥有更强地方自组织的社会要严重得多。

    进一步证据：秦晖表明，在小共同体存活的地区，即宗族组织最活跃的东南沿海省份广东、浙江和江苏，1950年代对集体化的抵制明显强于华北平原，后者的原子化小农缺乏任何集体行动的组织基础（第266至267页）。这证实了中间社会组织的存在与否直接影响国家渗透和控制地方社会的能力，这是Sng的模型仅关注地理距离所未能捕捉的因素。

  2. Point 2: “Tyranny at the Bottom” and the Sources of Peasant Suffering: SUPPLEMENTARY

    Sng’s argument: Local agents exploited their informational advantage to extort taxpayers. The equilibrium extortion rate \(\varepsilon^*=Y/(2mX)\) increases with the economy’s size and decreases with monitoring capacity. The ruler keeps the official tax rate \(\tau\) low because the total effective burden \(\tau+\varepsilon\) must not exceed the rebellion threshold \(r\). Hence “high corruption and light taxation were causally linked” (p. 112).

    Supplementary observation: Qin Hui’s description of the “state broker” (国家经纪) role of village cadres provides a near-perfect empirical illustration of Sng’s representative agent, extending the mechanism into the modern period. Post-reform administrative villages occupy an awkward position: “serving the state without being supported by the state, supported by villagers without benefiting villagers” (p. 29). Their function, in peasants’ words, is to “demand grain, demand money, demand lives” on behalf of higher authorities. When such agents are “neither officials nor commoners nor clan elders, yet manage land, people, and imperial grain” (p. 302), they tend to become a “negative interest stratum” that pursues its own enrichment, precisely the agent behavior Sng’s model predicts.

    Further evidence: Qin Hui’s observation about the land reform movement is particularly striking. During Communist land reform, “the overwhelming majority of peasants’ grievances were typically directed not at landlord exploitation through rents, but at the Nationalist government’s crushing taxes, conscription, and the abuses of local baojia personnel” (p. 39). This directly supports Sng’s claim that the primary source of peasant suffering in Chinese history was extraction by state agents and their proxies, not private landlord-tenant exploitation. It also supports Sng’s argument that peasant revolts were triggered by violations of the no-revolt constraint \(\tau+\varepsilon>r\) rather than by private rent levels. Across all Chinese dynasties, Qin Hui argues, peasant wars were caused by “the court’s abuse of corvée labor, excessive tax surcharges, severe economic controls that cut off the people’s livelihoods, conflicts between strict household registration controls and waves of displaced people, or corrupt local administration” (pp. 44–45), all about the state and its agents exceeding the population’s tolerance threshold.

    Sng的观点：地方代理人利用信息优势向纳税人勒索。均衡勒索率 \(\varepsilon^*=Y/(2mX)\) 随经济规模增加而上升，随监督能力增强而下降。统治者将官方税率 \(\tau\) 保持在低水平，因为总有效负担 \(\tau+\varepsilon\) 不能超过叛乱门槛 \(r\)。由此“高腐败与轻税负在因果上联系在一起”（第112页）。

    补充性观察：秦晖对村干部“国家经纪”角色的描述，为Sng的代表性代理人提供了近乎完美的经验例证，并将这一机制延伸到现代时期。改革后行政村处于尴尬位置：“给国家办事而国家不养，由村民养活而无益于村”（第29页）。用农民的话说，其职能就是替上头“要粮要钱要命”。当这些代理人“非官非民非族长，管地管人管皇粮”（第302页）时，他们往往成为追求自身利益的“消极利益阶层”，正是Sng模型所预测的代理人行为。

    进一步证据：秦晖关于土改运动的观察特别引人注目。在共产党土改中，“农民绝大部分的控诉通常并非冲着地租剥削，而是冲着国民党的苛捐杂税、拉丁派款和乡村保甲人员的为非作歹”（第39页）。这直接支持了Sng的论点：中国历史上农民苦难的主要来源是国家代理人及其代理的榨取，而非私人地主-佃农剥削。它还支持Sng的论点：农民叛乱由无反叛约束的违反（\(\tau+\varepsilon>r\)）而非私人地租水平所触发。秦晖论证，在所有中国朝代中，农民战争都是由“朝廷滥用民力、横征加派、严厉的经济统制绝民生路、官府严厉的户口控制与流民潮冲突、或由于吏治腐败”所引起的（第44至45页），全都涉及国家及其代理人超越了民众忍耐阈值。

  3. Point 3: Clerks Were Not Simply “Tyrants at the Bottom”: They Were a Complex, Double-Edged Institution (CHALLENGE)

    Sng’s argument: In Sng’s model, local agents are treated as pure rent-seekers who exploit information asymmetry to extort the population. The agent’s only strategic choice is how much to extract (the extortion rate ε), and the only constraint on this extraction is the probability of being caught and punished by the ruler. The agent has no social ties, no local identity, no interests other than personal enrichment. This one-dimensional characterization drives the entire model’s predictions about corruption, low tax rates, and fiscal decline.

    Challenging observation: Historical evidence from the White Lotus movement reveals that clerks and runners were far more complex figures than Sng’s model allows. They were not purely extractive agents of the state; they were embedded social actors whose local ties could make them conduits for anti-state mobilization, the exact opposite of their assigned role. During the Jia-Qing era White Lotus uprising, clerks in the Dangyang (当阳) religious network served as core organizers. They rallied co-religionists, coordinated with forces outside the city walls, and helped the rebels hold Dangyang city for five months. The very personnel who were supposed to be the state’s frontline instruments for suppressing “heterodox sects” had become those sects’ most effective organizers.

    Further evidence: the structural logic behind clerks’ dual role:

    This was not accidental but structurally determined by several institutional features that Sng’s model does not capture.

    First, clerks occupied the position of “pivots between officials and the populace” (官民交接之枢纽) and bridges between city and countryside. Their daily work, collecting taxes, serving legal papers, conducting inspections, managing local registries, brought them into constant contact with all social strata. This gave them exactly the kind of dense, cross-cutting social network that the “big community standard” (大共同体本位) had destroyed among ordinary peasants. In Sng’s model, agents are isolated utility-maximizers facing only the ruler above and taxpayers below. In reality, clerks were the most socially connected people in local society. Their occupational ties (业缘关系), kinship ties (血缘关系), and territorial ties (地缘关系) made them natural nodes for the spread of religious networks. The White Lotus teaching did not diffuse randomly; it spread along the pre-existing social channels that clerks’ work had created, including, critically, the inter-yamen communication networks that connected clerks in subordinate offices with those in superior offices, allowing the movement to break through geographic boundaries and achieve long-distance diffusion.

    Second, the Qing deliberately rotated provincial-level officials frequently to prevent them from building local power bases. This “rule of avoidance” (回避制度) and frequent rotation meant that appointed officials almost never had deep knowledge of their jurisdictions’ specific conditions. They therefore delegated most substantive administrative work to the permanently stationed clerks. This created a severe information asymmetry, but one that ran in the opposite direction from what Sng emphasizes. In Sng’s model, the information problem is between the ruler and his agents: the ruler cannot observe the agent’s extortion. In reality, there was an equally severe information problem between the rotating official and the permanent clerk: the official could not observe the clerk’s actual activities, loyalties, or social connections. The clerk’s stability and locality gave him an information monopoly that could be used for extortion (as Sng predicts), for protecting co-religionists, sheltering dissidents, filing false reports, and actively subverting the state’s control functions. Whether a clerk actually wielded de facto power depended almost entirely on the personal quality of his superior, an institutional fragility far more specific than Sng’s abstract “monitoring parameter” m.

    Third, the clerks’ low status and miserable compensation, the very factor that Sng correctly identifies as driving extortion, also pushed them toward solidarity with the local population against the state. Deep-rooted local sentiment (乡土观念) made the state’s formal mechanisms for suppressing heterodox sects, mutual responsibility systems (连坐) and informant incentives (举报), virtually unenforceable, except in cases involving personal grudges. Clerks tasked with investigating and apprehending “heterodox sect members” had enormous discretionary power in defining who counted as a sect member and who did not. This operational ambiguity explains why the White Lotus faith could endure across several dynasties of official suppression and even grow stronger under persecution: the very agents charged with eradicating it were also its protectors and propagators.

    Fourth, and most importantly, some scholars have argued that the structural separation between “officials” and “clerks” (官吏分途), the very institutional feature that created this dual role, was itself a rational response to the transaction costs of centralized governance. Keeping clerks as permanent, local, low-status functionaries did create the information asymmetry and corruption that Sng describes. But it also ensured continuity of local knowledge, maintained functioning administrative routines despite frequent official rotations, and provided a flexible (if exploitative) mechanism for adapting central directives to local conditions. This system was not purely dysfunctional; it was a second-best institutional solution to the genuine problem of governing a vast territory through a thin layer of rotating generalist officials.

    Implication for Sng’s model: Sng treats the agent as a one-dimensional extractor. The White Lotus evidence shows that the agent was simultaneously an extractor, a local social broker, a potential rebel organizer, and an indispensable administrator. The “principal-agent problem” in late imperial China was not simply that agents stole too much; it was that the state had created agents who were deeply embedded in local society in ways that made them both necessary and uncontrollable. This is a fundamentally different kind of governance failure from the one Sng models, and it cannot be reduced to a distance-based monitoring problem. It is an institutional design problem, the specific way in which the Qing chose to structure the relationship between rotating officials and permanent clerks, that would exist regardless of the empire’s geographic size.

    Sng的观点：在Sng的模型中，地方代理人被视为纯粹的寻租者，利用信息不对称来勒索民众。代理人唯一的策略选择是榨取多少（勒索率ε），唯一的约束是被统治者发现和惩罚的概率。代理人没有社会联系、没有地方认同、没有个人牟利之外的利益。这种一维刻画驱动了模型关于腐败、低税率和财政衰退的全部预测。

    挑战性观察：白莲教运动的历史证据揭示了胥吏远比Sng模型所允许的更为复杂。他们不是纯粹的国家榨取性代理人；他们是嵌入地方社会的行动者，其地方关系可以使他们成为反国家动员的渠道，这恰恰是其被赋予角色的反面。乾嘉之际白莲教起义中，当阳教团中的胥吏作为骨干，号召同教起事、接应城外同教，帮助叛军占据当阳城达五个月。本应作为国家镇压“邪教”最前线工具的人员，反而成了这些教派最有效的组织者。

    进一步证据：胥吏双重角色背后的结构逻辑：

    这并非偶然，而是由Sng模型未能捕捉的几个制度特征在结构上所决定的。

    第一，胥吏处于“官民交接之枢纽”和城乡桥梁的位置。他们的日常工作，即征税、送达法律文书、下乡巡查、管理地方簿册，使他们与各社会阶层保持持续接触。这赋予了他们恰恰是“大共同体本位”在普通农民中已经摧毁的那种密集的、交叉联系的社会网络。在Sng的模型中，代理人是孤立的效用最大化者，上面只面对统治者，下面只面对纳税人。在现实中，胥吏是地方社会中社会联系最广泛的人。他们的业缘关系、血缘关系和地缘关系使他们成为宗教网络传播的天然节点。白莲教的传播不是随机扩散的；它沿着胥吏工作所创造的既有社会渠道传播，关键的是，包括连接下级衙门胥吏与上级衙门胥吏的公务交往网络，使运动得以突破地域界限实现长距离扩散。

    第二，清朝刻意频繁调动省级官员以防止他们建立地方势力。这种“回避制度”和频繁调动意味着任命的官员几乎不可能对其辖区的具体情况有深入了解。他们因此将大多数实质性行政工作委托给长期驻留的胥吏。这创造了一种严重的信息不对称，但方向与Sng所强调的相反。在Sng的模型中，信息问题在于统治者与其代理人之间：统治者无法观察代理人的勒索。在现实中，流动的官员与常驻的胥吏之间存在同样严重的信息问题：官员无法观察胥吏的实际活动、忠诚或社会联系。胥吏的稳定性和地方性赋予他们信息垄断，这不仅可用于勒索（如Sng所预测），还可用于保护同教信徒、庇护异见者、呈报虚假信息、以及积极颠覆国家的控制职能。胥吏是否实际掌握权力几乎完全取决于其上司的个人素质，这是一种远比Sng抽象的“监督参数”m更具体的制度脆弱性。

    第三，胥吏低下的地位和微薄的报酬，正是Sng正确地将其识别为驱动勒索的因素，同时也推动他们与当地民众形成对抗国家的团结。深厚的乡土观念使国家镇压“邪教”的正式机制，即连坐和举报，几乎无法有效执行，除非涉及个人恩怨。被指派缉查和追捕“邪教”“教匪”的胥吏在界定谁算教徒、谁不算方面拥有巨大的自由裁量空间。这种操作上的模糊性解释了为什么白莲教信仰能绵延几个朝代的官方镇压而不断绝，反而在镇压下日益强盛：被赋予根除它的使命的代理人，同时也是它的保护者和传播者。

    第四，也是最重要的，一些学者认为“官吏分途”，正是创造了这种双重角色的制度特征，本身是对中央集权治理交易成本的理性应对。将胥吏保持为永久的、地方的、低地位的职能人员确实创造了Sng所描述的信息不对称和腐败。但它也确保了地方知识的连续性，在官员频繁轮换的情况下维持了行政运作的常规性，并提供了一种灵活的（虽然是剥削性的）机制，使中央指令能适应地方条件。这一制度并非纯粹功能失调；它是通过一薄层流动的通才官员治理广大领土这一真实问题的次优制度解决方案。

    对Sng模型的意涵：Sng将代理人视为一维的榨取者。白莲教的证据表明，代理人同时是榨取者、地方社会中介人、潜在的叛乱组织者和不可或缺的行政人员。晚期帝国中国的“委托-代理问题”不仅仅是代理人偷得太多；而是国家创造了深度嵌入地方社会的代理人，使他们既不可或缺又不可控制。这是一种与Sng所建模的根本不同的治理失败，它不能被简化为基于距离的监督问题。这是一个制度设计问题，即清朝选择如何构建流动官员与常驻胥吏之间关系的具体方式，无论帝国的地理规模如何，这一问题都会存在。

  4. Point 4: The Rebellion Threshold r May Have Been Endogenously High (CHALLENGE)

    Sng’s argument: The ruler sets the tax rate to ensure \(\tau+\varepsilon\le r\), where \(r\) is the rebellion threshold. This constraint is presented as relatively tight: the ruler must constantly worry about pushing the population past the breaking point (pp. 111–112).

    Challenging observation: Qin Hui’s comparative analysis of collectivization in China and Russia suggests that r was endogenously much higher than Sng’s model implies, precisely because the “big community standard” had destroyed the organizational capacity for collective resistance. In Russia, where autonomous village communes (mir) survived, peasants mounted fierce organized resistance to collectivization. In China, where atomized smallholders lacked organizational resources, collectivization “encountered no strong resistance and was accomplished faster than in Russia” (pp. 259–266), even though it represented a far more radical transformation. Qin Hui also documents that “heavy burdens, corrupt administration, and various disasters made peasants abandon their land and flee, even when they had land” (p. 51). The phrase “受田者与田为仇” (“those who received land became enemies of their land”) captures a situation where the rational response to state extraction was not revolt but flight, suggesting a much higher tolerance threshold for collective action than Sng assumes.

    Further evidence and implication: If \(r\) was very high, then the reason for low taxes was not primarily fear of revolt (as Sng argues), but something else: institutional incapacity to collect more, or the political choice to maintain a low-cost governance model that preserved imperial power, or sheer administrative inertia rooted in “quota-ism” (原额主义). This does not invalidate Sng’s model, but it suggests that the no-revolt constraint may have been less important than institutional constraints on the state’s ability to expand its extractive capacity. Sng’s model may be solving for the wrong binding constraint.

    Sng的观点：统治者设定税率以确保 \(\tau+\varepsilon\le r\)，其中 \(r\) 是叛乱门槛。这一约束被呈现为相对紧的：统治者必须不断担心把民众逼过承受极限（第111至112页）。

    挑战性观察：秦晖对中俄集体化的比较分析表明，r内生地远高于Sng模型所暗示的，正因为“大共同体本位”摧毁了集体抵抗的组织能力。在自治村社（米尔）存续的俄国，农民对集体化进行了激烈的有组织抵制。在原子化小农缺乏组织资源的中国，集体化“虽非农民所愿，却也未遇到强烈反抗便以比俄国更快的速度实现了”（第259至266页），尽管这是一个远更激进的转型。秦晖还记载，“沉重的负担、恶劣的吏治以及种种天灾人祸使农民有地不种、弃地而逃”（第51页）。“受田者与田为仇”捕捉了这样一种状况：面对国家榨取，理性反应不是反叛而是逃亡，暗示集体行动的忍耐门槛远高于Sng所假设的。

    进一步证据及其意涵：如果 \(r\) 非常高，那么低税收的原因主要不是对叛乱的恐惧（如Sng所论证），而是其他东西：收取更多税收的制度性无能，或者维持低成本治理模式以保持皇权的政治选择，或者根植于“原额主义”的纯粹行政惯性。这并不否定Sng的模型，但暗示无反叛约束可能不如国家扩大汲取能力的制度性约束重要。Sng的模型可能在求解错误的约束条件。

  5. Point 5: The “Paradox of Development”: SUPPLEMENTARY, with a CHALLENGE on Its Cause

    Sng’s argument: Proposition 3 predicts that when agency costs are severe, economic expansion will initially increase but eventually depress tax revenue. The Qing’s real tax revenue peaked in the early eighteenth century and then declined steadily despite continued economic growth. Revenue that could support 9.6% of the population in 1685 could support only 2.3% by 1848 (pp. 113, 119–120, Fig. 3).

    Supplementary observation: This matches well-known fiscal data: from the Qing’s entry through the Pass (1644) to the eve of the Opium War, revenue increased by only about 169%, while population increased by roughly 400%. The hump-shaped pattern with peak under the Qianlong emperor followed by slow decline is a widely documented fact. Brook’s The Confusions of Pleasure documents a strikingly similar process in the Ming dynasty: the central government “was retreating from tax administration, leaving it to local officials to figure out how to increase revenue” (p. 102), and officials were “forced to find ways to collect money on their own, which could not be formally recorded in the county’s account books.” The same dynamic of the formal fiscal system failing to keep pace with economic change is visible across multiple dynasties.

    Challenge on the cause: However, Sng attributes this “paradox” to the interaction between size and agency costs, treating the economy’s growth as purely extensive (\(Y=AN\), with constant productivity \(A\)). Brook’s evidence shows that the economy’s growth was more than extensive. It involved qualitative structural changes: specialization of production stages in Jiangnan textile manufacturing, where “each stage of production became an independent productive activity” (p. 220); the shift from in-kind to silver-based taxation (the Single Whip Reform); and the development of long-distance commercial networks. These structural changes altered the nature of the monitoring problem. Silver-based transactions are inherently more auditable than in-kind transactions: a shift to commercial taxation could have improved monitoring capacity \(m\) without any change in geographic distance. The fact that the Ming and Qing states failed to exploit this opportunity suggests that the “paradox” was driven not by size making reform impossible, but by institutional rigidity making reform unattempted. The economy offered new possibilities for state revenue; the state’s institutional apparatus was unable or unwilling to seize them.

    Sng的观点：命题3预测当代理成本足够严重时，经济扩张最初将增加但最终会压低税收。清朝实际税收在十八世纪初达到峰值，此后尽管经济持续增长仍稳步下降。1685年税收能养活9.6%的人口，到1848年只能养活2.3%（第113、119至120页，图3）。

    补充性观察：这与广为人知的财政数据吻合：从清朝入关（1644年）到鸦片战争前夕，岁入仅增加约169%，而人口增加了约400%。乾隆朝达到最高峰后缓慢下降的驼峰形模式是广泛记载的事实。卜正民的《纵乐的困惑》记录了明朝一个惊人相似的过程：中央政府“正退出税收的行政管理，交由地方官员去处理如何增加税收的问题”（第102页），官员们“被迫要自己去想方法征集金钱，而这又不能正式记录于县里的账簿里”。正式财政体系跟不上经济变迁的同一动态，在多个朝代都清晰可见。

    对原因的挑战：然而，Sng将这一“悖论”归因于规模与代理成本的交互作用，将经济增长视为纯粹外延型的（\(Y=AN\)，生产率 \(A\) 不变）。卜正民的证据表明经济增长不仅仅是外延型的。它涉及质的结构变化：江南纺织业生产阶段的专业化分工，“生产的每个环节都成为独立的生产活动”（第220页）；从实物税到白银税的转变（一条鞭法）；以及长距离商业网络的发展。这些结构性变化改变了监督问题的性质。白银交易本质上比实物交易更容易审计：向商业税的转变本可在不改变地理距离的情况下改善监督能力 \(m\)。明清国家未能利用这一机会表明，“悖论”不是因为规模使改革不可能，而是因为制度僵化使改革未被尝试。经济为国家收入提供了新的可能性；国家的制度机器无力或无意去抓住它们。

  6. Point 6: Why the Fiscal System Did Not Budget for Actual Governance Needs: SUPPLEMENTARY, Deepening the Explanation

    Sng’s argument: A severe principal-agent problem leads to a small bureaucracy (Proposition 2). The equilibrium tax rate is too low to justify expanding the county system. In 1800, roughly 20,000 ranked officials governed over 300 million people (p. 113).

    Supplementary observation, deepening the explanation: Sub-bureaucratic personnel (clerks, runners, private secretaries) who actually ran local government were deliberately kept in an informal, “off-budget” status. As Qu Tongzu’s research documents, each county needed hundreds of these functionaries, but the formal budget provided for almost none of them. Runners received about 6 taels per year, far below subsistence. Their real income came from customary fees and extortion, which is the ε in Sng’s model. This arrangement was not accidental. It served three functions simultaneously: (1) it saved central fiscal expenditure by shifting governance costs to local society; (2) it transferred the political risk of extraction, peasants blamed the clerks, not the emperor; (3) it kept agents in a state of permanent legal jeopardy (their income was technically illegal), giving the emperor perpetual grounds for punishment and thus maintaining asymmetric power. This was, from the emperor’s perspective, not a “bad” institution but a “usable” one, until accumulated dysfunction made it unsustainable.

    Further evidence: Zelin’s The Magistrate’s Tael shows that Yongzheng’s “nourish honesty silver” (养廉银) reforms raised stipends roughly 30-fold but did not eliminate corruption, because as the economy grew, rent-seeking temptations grew proportionally. Sng cites Ni and Van’s (2006) estimate that eliminating corruption in 1750 would have required a 250-fold wage increase (p. 123). But beyond this “efficiency wage” problem, Qin Hui identifies a deeper political-structural obstacle: without taxpayer rights, what he calls an “identity-based tribute” (身份性贡赋) system rather than a rights-and-obligations-based tax system, there was no institutional mechanism for a “tax-for-services” bargain (p. 25). In European parliamentary traditions, taxpayers consented to higher taxes in exchange for specific public services, creating a positive feedback loop between taxation and governance quality. In China, taxation was a unilateral obligation with no corresponding entitlement to services, so no constituency demanded that the state professionalize or expand its bureaucracy, even when doing so would have benefited the population.

    Sng的观点：严重的委托-代理问题导致小官僚机构（命题2）。均衡税率太低，无法证明扩展县制的合理性。1800年约20,000名有品级官员治理超过3亿人口（第113页）。

    补充性观察，深化解释：实际运转地方政府的编外人员（胥吏、衙役、幕友）被刻意保持在非正式的“编外”状态。如瞿同祖的研究所记载，每个县需要数百名这类人员才能运转，但正式预算几乎没有为他们提供经费。衙役年薪约6两，远低于维生所需。他们的实际收入来自惯例收费和勒索，这就是Sng模型中的ε。这种安排并非偶然。它同时服务于三重功能：（1）将治理成本从中央财库转嫁给地方社会，节省中央财政支出；（2）转移了榨取的政治风险，农民怨恨胥吏而非皇帝；（3）使代理人处于永久的法律不安全状态（其收入在技术上是非法的），给予皇帝永久的惩罚理由，从而维持不对称的权力关系。从皇帝的视角看，这不是“坏”制度而是“可用”的制度，直到积累的功能失调使其不可持续。

    进一步证据：曾小萍（Zelin）的《州县官的银两》表明雍正的“养廉银”改革将俸禄提高了约30倍，但未能消除腐败，因为随着经济增长，寻租诱惑也等比增长。Sng引用倪志伟和Van（2006）的估算，认为在1750年消除腐败需要250倍的工资增长（第123页）。但超越这一“效率工资”问题，秦晖识别了更深层的政治-结构性障碍：在没有纳税人权利的情况下，即他称之为“身份性贡赋”体制而非“权利-义务统一”的税制，不存在“以税换服务”的制度性机制（第25页）。在欧洲议会传统中，纳税人同意更高税收以换取特定公共服务，在税收与治理质量之间创造了正反馈循环。在中国，纳税是单方面义务，没有相应的获得服务的权利，因此没有利益群体要求国家专业化或扩大其官僚机构，即使这样做会让民众受益。

  7. Point 7: Why Commercial Development Did Not Lead to Political Reform or Fiscal Expansion: CHALLENGE

    Sng’s argument: Sng does not directly address this question. His model treats the economy as an exogenous variable and focuses on how its growth affects fiscal capacity through the principal-agent channel. He notes that China’s peaceful external environment reduced the pressure to build fiscal capacity (p. 119), but does not explore why the thriving commercial economy did not generate pressure for institutional change from within.

    Challenging observation: This is a major gap in Sng’s analysis. The supplementary literature reveals several interconnected reasons why commercial growth failed to translate into political reform or fiscal modernization, none of which are captured by a “size” variable.

    Further evidence: four mechanisms:

    (1) Merchants were absorbed into the gentry system rather than forming an independent political class. Brook demonstrates that the social distance between merchants and gentry “was constantly being narrowed, and this narrowing strengthened rather than weakened the gentry social group” (p. 291). Merchants sought gentry status through land purchase, cultural investment, and marriage alliances. They did not seek to build alternative political institutions. Brook explicitly invokes Braudel: China developed “an extensive market economy... but this was not capitalism in the European sense” (p. 227). The key difference was the social structure within which commerce operated.

    (2) State monopoly over key revenue sources created patron-client relations, not political bargaining. Merchants in the most profitable sectors (salt, tea, mining) operated under state licenses and depended on state permission for their profits. As Brook documents regarding Huizhou salt merchants, their strategy was to “find opportunities to enter officials’ cultural circles” (pp. 144–145) to reduce official predation and gain competitive advantages, the opposite of the European pattern where independent merchant wealth enabled demands for political concessions.

    (3) No organizational infrastructure for collective merchant political action. Qin Hui’s “big community standard” framework explains this directly: independent social organizations were systematically suppressed. Merchants had no equivalent of European urban communes, chartered guilds, or representative assemblies. As Liang Shuming observed (quoted in Qin Hui, p. 271): “For a Chinese person, what is close at hand is the family; what is distant is all-underheaven... the middle ground suitable for cultivating group life” was missing. Without intermediate organizations, merchants could only interact with the state as individuals, never as a collective political force.

    (4) Policy alternatives existed but were not adopted, indicating political, not geographic, obstacles. Brook documents that Qiu Jun’s (丘濬) Supplement to the Great Learning (1487) argued against state monopolies, for free maritime trade, and for letting merchants manage production and distribution (pp. 116–117). The Ming intellectual tradition knew what more market-friendly policies would look like. The fact that such ideas were never implemented points to the political logic of a system that would not empower social groups outside the existing hierarchy.

    Sng的观点： Sng没有直接讨论这个问题。他的模型将经济视为外生变量，聚焦于经济增长如何通过委托-代理渠道影响财政能力。他指出中国和平的外部环境降低了建设财政能力的压力（第119页），但没有探讨为什么繁荣的商业经济没有从内部产生制度变革的压力。

    挑战性观察： 这是Sng分析的一个重大缺口。补充文献揭示了商业增长未能转化为政治改革或财政现代化的几个相互关联的原因，没有一个能被“规模”变量所捕捉。

    进一步证据：四重机制：

    （1）商人被吸纳进绅士体系而非形成独立政治阶层。 卜正民证明商人与绅士之间的社会距离“不断被拉近，而这种弥合的方式加强而不是削弱了士绅社会集团的力量”（第291页）。商人通过购地、文化投资和联姻追求绅士身份，而不寻求建立替代性政治制度。卜正民明确引用布罗代尔：中国发展了“一个广泛的市场经济……但这不是欧洲意义上的资本主义”（第227页）。关键差异在于商业运作于其中的社会结构。

    （2）国家对关键税源的垄断创造了庇护-依附关系而非政治谈判。 最有利可图行业（盐、茶、矿业）的商人在国家许可下经营，利润依赖国家准许。正如卜正民记述的徽州盐商，他们的策略是“努力寻找机会进入官员的文化圈子”（第144至145页）以减少官府掠夺和获取竞争优势，这与欧洲模式正相反，在欧洲，独立的商人财富使他们能够要求政治让步。

    （3）商人缺乏集体政治行动的组织基础设施。 秦晖的“大共同体本位”框架直接解释了这一点：独立的社会组织被系统性地压制。商人没有欧洲城市公社、拥有特许权的行会或代议机构的对等物。如梁漱溟所观察（转引自秦晖，第271页）：“中国人切己的便是身家，远大的便是天下了……得乎其中，有一适当范围，正好培养团体生活”的中间地带是缺失的。没有中间组织，商人只能以个人身份与国家互动，而永远无法作为集体政治力量。

    （4）政策替代方案存在但未被采纳，表明障碍是政治性的而非地理性的。 卜正民记载丘濬的《大学衍义补》（1487年）反对国家垄断，支持自由海上贸易，主张让商人管理生产和分配（第116至117页）。明代知识传统知道更友好的市场政策长什么样子。这些思想从未被实施，指向的是一个不愿赋权于现有等级体系之外社会群体的制度的政治逻辑。

  8. Point 8: “Without Foreign Invasion, the Qing Might Have Fallen Sooner”: CHALLENGE

    Sng’s argument: Sng treats the Opium War and subsequent foreign invasions primarily as external shocks that exposed the Qing state’s accumulated weakness, especially its underinvestment in military capacity due to fiscal constraints (pp. 119–123).

    Challenging observation: The causal relationship may have been more complex, indeed, partially reversed. Without the new revenue sources created by forced opening (customs duties administered by the Imperial Maritime Customs Service under Robert Hart, and the likin 厘金 tax on internal commerce introduced during the Taiping Rebellion), the Qing likely could not have survived the mid-nineteenth-century internal crises. Before the Opium War, prior dynasties almost all fell to large-scale peasant revolts. The Qing was already facing the same fiscal exhaustion that had destroyed previous dynasties: per capita revenue had been declining for over a century. The foreign-managed customs service and the new commercial taxes broke through a fiscal ceiling that the traditional land-tax system could never have breached.

    Further evidence: Late Qing fiscal data dramatically illustrates this point. Revenue exceeded 100 million taels in 1899, crossed 200 million in 1903, and reached nearly 300 million by 1911, an explosion of fiscal capacity unimaginable in a purely agricultural tax regime. This was concrete evidence that Chinese society was already undergoing a transformation toward a commercial-industrial economy. The critical point is: this revenue explosion occurred on the same territory with essentially the same population. Size did not change. What changed was the institutional channel of extraction: customs duties, commercial transit taxes, and eventually modern-style taxes on trade and manufacturing. If size were the binding constraint (as Sng argues), then new institutional channels should not have made such a dramatic difference.

    Moreover, the Maritime Customs Service itself refutes the size thesis. It operated across the entire Chinese coastline, a vast geographic span, yet achieved levels of fiscal efficiency that the traditional bureaucracy never approached. This was not because the coastline was shorter than the interior, but because the institutional design was fundamentally different: professional staff, standardized procedures, independent auditing, and vertical management insulated from the traditional bureaucratic hierarchy.

    Sng的观点： Sng将鸦片战争及此后的外国入侵主要视为暴露清朝积累的弱点的外部冲击，尤其是因财政约束导致的军事投入不足（第119至123页）。

    挑战性观察： 因果关系可能更复杂，实际上部分是反过来的。如果没有被迫开放所创造的新税源（赫德主持的海关总税务司所征收的关税，以及太平天国时期引入的厘金），清朝很可能熬不过十九世纪中期的内部危机。鸦片战争之前，此前各朝几乎都灭亡于大规模农民起义。清朝已经面临着摧毁此前各朝的同样的财政枯竭：人均税收已经持续下降了一个多世纪。外国人管理的海关和新的商业税收突破了传统土地税制永远无法突破的财政天花板。

    进一步证据： 晚清财政数据戏剧性地说明了这一点。1899年岁入超过1亿两，1903年超过2亿两，1911年将近3亿两，这是纯农业税制下不可想象的财政能力爆发。这是中国社会正在转型为工商业社会的具体证据。关键在于：这一收入爆发发生在同一片领土上，人口本质上相同。规模没有改变。改变的是汲取的制度渠道：关税、商业过境税，以及最终的现代型贸易和制造业税收。如果规模是约束性瓶颈（如Sng所论证），那么新的制度渠道不应该造成如此戏剧性的差异。

    更有甚者，海关总税务司本身就反驳了规模论。它运作于整个中国海岸线，一个巨大的地理跨度，却达到了传统官僚体系从未接近的财政效率。这不是因为海岸线比内陆短，而是因为制度设计根本不同：专业人员、标准化程序、独立审计和不受传统官僚层级干扰的垂直管理。

  9. Point 9: The KMT’s Fiscal Structure: Extending the Institutional Argument Beyond the Qing

    Sng’s argument: Sng’s analysis ends at 1850 and does not discuss subsequent fiscal developments.

    Extending the analysis: The Nationalist (KMT) government’s fiscal structure provides a further test case that confirms the primacy of institutional design over geographic size. The KMT derived the majority of its revenue from customs duties and coastal salt taxes, taxes that did not require deep engagement with rural society. At its peak in 1929, customs revenue constituted 51% of total government revenue. The KMT was essentially a regime that “taxed foreigners” through import duties. Coastal salt taxes added another 10% or more. This meant the state had virtually no institutional linkage with the vast interior agricultural population: it neither taxed them significantly nor invested in them. The fiscal base depended on the volume of imports and coastal trade, not on the productivity or welfare of the rural majority.

    This fiscal structure had two consequences directly relevant to Sng’s framework. First, it made the KMT structurally incapable of deep reform: customs and salt revenues were largely pledged to repay foreign debts and cover military expenditures, leaving little for productive investment or rural administration. Second, it put the KMT’s interests in direct conflict with domestic capitalists: the regime depended on imports, while domestic industry would have benefited from protection. The result was a government fiscally disconnected from the society it nominally governed, unable to mobilize resources for rural development, land reform, or grassroots administrative capacity. This was not a consequence of China’s size. It was a consequence of the state’s institutional choices about how and from whom to extract revenue.

    The Communist Party, governing the same territory, chose a fundamentally different approach, penetrating rural society through party organization and extracting surplus through collective institutions, and achieved dramatically different results, confirming that the binding constraint was institutional, not geographic.

    Sng的观点： Sng的分析止于1850年，未讨论此后的财政发展。

    延伸分析： 国民党政府的财政结构提供了一个进一步的检验案例，确认了制度设计优先于地理规模。国民党的大部分收入来自关税和沿海盐税：这些税种不需要与农村社会深度接触。1929年高峰期，关税收入占政府总收入的51%。国民党本质上是一个通过进口关税“向外国人收税”的政权。沿海盐税又占10%以上。这意味着国家与广大内陆农业人口之间几乎没有制度性联系：既不大量向他们征税，也不向他们投资。财政基础依赖进口量和沿海贸易，而非农村多数人口的生产力或福祉。

    这种财政结构对Sng的框架有两个直接相关的后果。第一，它使国民党在结构上无力进行深层改革：关税和盐税收入大部分被用于偿还外债和军费开支，几乎没有余力用于生产性投资或农村行政。第二，它使国民党的利益与国内资本家直接冲突：政权依赖进口，而国内工业本将受益于保护政策。结果是一个在财政上与其名义治理的社会脱节的政府，无力动员资源用于农村发展、土地改革或基层行政能力建设。这不是中国规模的后果。这是国家关于如何以及向谁汲取收入的制度选择的后果。

    共产党治理着同一片领土，选择了根本不同的方式：通过党组织渗透农村社会、通过集体制度汲取剩余，并取得了戏剧性不同的结果，确认了约束性瓶颈是制度性的，而非地理性的。

  10. Point 10: The Fundamental Problem Was Institutional Obsolescence, Not Size: MAJOR CHALLENGE

    Sng’s argument: Size is the key independent variable. Geographic size determines the monitoring parameter m, which in turn determines corruption, tax rates, bureaucratic size, and the dynamic trajectory of fiscal capacity. The parameter m did not improve significantly during the late imperial period due to the absence of improvements in information technology (p. 122).

    Major challenge: The strongest counterargument to Sng’s size thesis comes from the modern Chinese experience. The People’s Republic governs essentially the same territory with dramatically greater state capacity. This was achieved not by shrinking the territory but by fundamentally redesigning the institutional “operating system.” The Communist Party raised m through organizational innovation, not through geographic change: (1) Party cells penetrated to the village level, creating a parallel monitoring hierarchy the imperial system never had. (2) Land reform destroyed the local gentry class, Sng’s “representative agent,” and replaced it with cadres whose careers depended on the party hierarchy. (3) Collectivization and the unified purchase and sale system (统购统销) allowed the state to directly appropriate agricultural surplus without relying on local intermediaries. (4) The 1994 tax-sharing reform (分税制) restructured central-local fiscal relations, and modern information systems dramatically improved monitoring capacity. These were institutional innovations, not geographic changes.

    This suggests that Sng’s model confounds two distinct variables: geographic size (which is genuinely exogenous and constant) and institutional design (which is endogenous and changeable). The late imperial state was weak not because China was large, but because its institutional “software” had become obsolete relative to the society it was governing. The Chinese bureaucratic system, though it appeared advanced and matured early, had long since ceased to be adequate to the economy’s actual form. Its officials’ skills, specialization, and knowledge were designed for a simple agrarian society, not for the complex commercial economy that had developed by the Ming-Qing period. It could persist as long as there were no external military threats; the Opium War exposed the mismatch.

    Further evidence: five scholarly traditions support this institutional-obsolescence diagnosis over the size thesis:

    (1) Ray Huang’s “management by numbers” (数目字管理) thesis. The traditional state could not precisely measure, record, or audit economic activity, not because of distance, but because the entire administrative system was not designed for precision management. Tax quotas were set by ancestral precedent, not by actual economic output. Financial records were so chaotic that even the Board of Revenue could not truly verify provincial reports.

    (2) Iwai Shigeki’s “quota-ism” (原額主義) concept. The Ming-Qing fiscal system’s defining feature was fixing revenues and expenditures at permanent quotas, rarely adjusted once set. This guaranteed stability but eliminated all flexibility. When the economy grew, population shifted, or new governance demands appeared, the quota remained unchanged, and the state’s real fiscal capacity shrank in relative terms. This was an institutional design problem, not a distance problem.

    (3) Wang Yeh-chien’s (王业键) fiscal history of the Qing. Wang documents that the Qing tax system had no income tax concept, no property assessment system, and no periodic land surveys or tax-base updates. “Yongbu jiashui” (“永不加赋,” “taxes will never be increased”) was more than a political promise; it reflected an institutional reality: the state simply did not possess the administrative capacity to assess how much to increase taxes or from whom. This is a capacity deficit, not a size deficit.

    (4) Philip Kuhn’s study of the 1768 “soulstealers” scare (叫魂案). Kuhn (cited by Sng, p. 116) shows that even the most energetic emperor (Qianlong), confronting a nationwide incident, could not distinguish real information from bureaucratic obfuscation. This is indeed a monitoring problem, but Kuhn’s analysis reveals it was institutional rather than only geographic: the Qing had no information-gathering apparatus independent of the bureaucracy itself. No independent statistical office, no independent auditing body: the emperor could only set bureaucrats to watch bureaucrats, and the watchers could be co-opted.

    (5) The civil examination system as an elite-absorption mechanism. The examination system channeled elite ambition into the existing bureaucratic structure. Wealthy merchants’ rational strategy was to invest in examination preparation and cultural capital to gain gentry status (Brook, pp. 242, 291), not to build autonomous political organizations. This prevented the emergence of an independent bourgeoisie that might have pressured the state to modernize its fiscal and administrative systems, the trajectory that occurred in parts of Western Europe.

    Sng的观点： 规模是关键自变量。地理规模决定监督参数m，m进而决定腐败、税率、官僚规模和财政能力的动态轨迹。由于信息技术没有显著改善，参数m在晚期帝国时期没有明显提高（第122页）。

    重大挑战： 对Sng规模论的最强反驳来自现代中国的经验。中华人民共和国治理着本质上相同的领土，但国家能力戏剧性增强。这不是通过缩小领土实现的，而是通过根本性地重新设计制度“操作系统”。共产党通过组织创新而非地理变化提高了m：（1）党支部渗透到村一级，创建了帝国制度从未有过的平行监督层级。（2）土改摧毁了地方绅士阶层（Sng的“代表性代理人”），代之以职业生涯依赖党的层级的干部。（3）集体化和统购统销制度使国家能直接占有农业剩余，不依赖地方中间人。（4）1994年分税制改革重构了中央-地方财政关系，现代信息系统戏剧性地改善了监督能力。这些是制度创新，而非地理变化。

    这表明Sng的模型混淆了两个不同的变量：地理规模（真正外生且恒定）和制度设计（内生且可变）。晚期帝国国家的虚弱不是因为中国大，而是因为其制度“软件”相对于它治理的社会已经过时。中国的官僚制度虽然看起来很先进且成熟很早，但其官员的技能、专业化和知识早已不适应经济发展的实际形态。它是为简单的农业社会而设计的，而不是为明清时期已经发展起来的复杂商业经济而设计的。它之所以能一直维持，是因为没有外部军事威胁；鸦片战争暴露了这种错配。

    进一步证据：五个学术传统支持制度过时的诊断而非规模论：

    （1）黄仁宇的“数目字管理”说。 传统国家无法精确地测量、记录和审计经济活动，不是因为距离远，而是因为整个行政体系的设计理念就不以精确管理为目标。税额按祖制设定，而非按实际经济产出动态调整。财务记录混乱到连户部都无法真正核实各省上报的数字。

    （2）岩井茂树的“原额主义”概念。 明清财政制度的根本特征是将各项收支固定为“定额”，一旦确定就极少调整。这保证了稳定性但消除了所有灵活性。当经济增长、人口转移或新治理需求出现时，定额不变，国家的实际财政能力在相对意义上萎缩。这是制度设计问题，不是距离问题。

    （3）王业键的清代财政史研究。 王业键的研究表明，清代税制没有所得税概念，没有财产评估制度，没有定期的土地普查和税基更新。“永不加赋”不只是政治承诺，它反映了制度现实：国家根本不具备评估应该向谁加多少税的行政能力。这是能力缺陷，不是规模缺陷。

    （4）孔飞力对1768年“叫魂”案的研究。 孔飞力（Sng引用，第116页）展示了即使是最精力充沛的皇帝（乾隆），面对一个全国性事件，也无法区分真实信息和官僚的敷衍塞责。这确实是监督问题，但孔飞力的分析揭示它是制度性的，而非仅仅地理性的：清朝没有独立于官僚体系本身的信息收集机构。没有独立的统计机构，没有独立的审计机构：皇帝只能设置官僚监督官僚，而监督者也可能被收买。

    （5）科举制度作为精英吸纳机制。 科举制度将精英抱负引导进现有官僚结构。富裕商人的理性策略是投资于科举准备和文化资本以获取绅士身份（卜正民，第242、291页），而非建立自主的政治组织。这阻止了独立资产阶级的出现（独立资产阶级本可能向国家施压，要求其财政和行政体系现代化），这正是西欧部分地区曾出现的轨迹。

  11. Point 11: The Broader Pattern: States Outgrow Their Institutional Capacity: SUPPLEMENTARY, Reframed

    Sng’s argument: The “paradox of development” (economic growth weakening state fiscal capacity when agency costs are severe) is specific to the principal-agent dynamics of large, centralized, weakly monitored states (pp. 108, 113, 119).

    Reframing: Japanese material from Kang Hao’s study suggests that a similar pattern (the state’s responsibilities outgrowing its institutional capacity) can occur even in small polities. The Kamakura Bakufu, governing a far smaller territory, experienced a version of the same trap after the Mongol invasions: it acquired unprecedented responsibilities but “many of these matters were originally outside the Bakufu’s jurisdiction; after the Mongol invasions, the Bakufu became the outlet for all contradictions” (Kang Hao, p. 83). The result was institutional overstretch and collapse, not because Japan was large, but because institutional capacity could not keep pace with demands.

    Further evidence and general principle: This suggests a reframing of Sng’s insight. The “paradox of development” is not specifically about size but about the gap between institutional capacity and governance demands. Premodern states faced rigid constraints: narrow tax base definitions (mainly land), high fixed costs of administrative expansion (adding a county meant an entire apparatus), and no credit instruments (no bonds, no viable paper money; the Ming tried and failed). Modern states can respond elastically through debt issuance, monetary policy, and adjustable tax types. The critical question is not “how big is the territory?” but “can the institutional system upgrade itself in response to new demands?” Size amplifies the gap (larger territories generate more diverse demands), but the rigidity of institutional design determines whether the gap becomes fatal. China’s size made the problem more acute; it did not create it.

    Sng的观点： “发展悖论”（当代理成本严重时经济增长削弱国家财政能力）是大型、中央集权、监督薄弱的国家特有的委托-代理动态（第108、113、119页）。

    重新框定： 康昊研究中的日本材料表明，类似的模式（国家职责超越其制度能力）即使在小政体中也可能发生。镰仓幕府治理着远更小的领土，在蒙古入侵后经历了同一陷阱的一个版本：它获得了前所未有的职责，但“很多事情原本并不在幕府的职权范围之内，蒙古袭来以后，幕府变成了一切矛盾的出口”（康昊，第83页）。结果是制度过度扩张和崩溃，不是因为日本大，而是因为制度能力跟不上需求。

    进一步证据和一般性原理： 这建议对Sng的洞见进行重新框定。“发展悖论”并非特别关于规模，而是关于制度能力与治理需求之间的差距。前现代国家面临刚性约束：狭窄的税基定义（主要是土地）、行政扩张的高固定成本（增设一县就意味着整套机构）、以及缺乏信用工具（没有债券，没有可行的纸币，明朝尝试过但失败了）。现代国家可以通过发行债务、货币政策和可调节的税种进行弹性回应。关键问题不是“领土有多大？”而是“制度体系能否自我升级以回应新的需求？”规模放大了差距（更大领土产生更多样的需求），但制度设计的僵化决定了差距是否变得致命。中国的规模使问题更加尖锐；但并非创造了它。

  12. Point 12: A Fair Assessment of Sng’s Contribution

    Sng’s paper remains a significant contribution. His model captures a genuine mechanism: the principal-agent problem in hierarchical bureaucracies is real, and geographic distance does exacerbate it. His empirical contribution, the spatial gradient of taxation and administration measured with original data, the reconstructed fiscal time series, is valuable. The “paradox of development” is a genuinely novel theoretical proposition. What this discussion suggests is not that Sng is wrong, but that his explanation is incomplete.

    More precisely, Sng confounds two distinct variables: geographic size (genuinely exogenous and constant) and institutional design (endogenous and changeable). The late imperial Chinese state’s fiscal weakness was the joint product of a challenging geography and an institutional apparatus that had failed to evolve for centuries. The same territory can be governed with vastly different levels of effectiveness depending on the institutional “software” deployed, as the dramatic contrasts between the Qing, the KMT, and the PRC demonstrate. The traditional Chinese bureaucratic system matured early and appeared advanced, but its officials’ skills, knowledge, and organizational methods had long since ceased to match the economy’s actual complexity. It could persist without external military pressure; the Opium War exposed the mismatch. The root of dynastic decline was not that China was too large to govern, but that its governing institutions had stopped developing while its economy and society had not.

    Sng的论文仍然是一项重要贡献。他的模型捕捉了一个真实的机制：层级官僚体系中的委托-代理问题是真实的，地理距离确实加剧了它。他的经验贡献：用原创数据测量的税收和行政的空间梯度、重建的财政时间序列，是有价值的。“发展悖论”是一个真正新颖的理论命题。本讨论所表明的不是Sng是错误的，而是他的解释是不完整的。

    更准确地说，Sng混淆了两个不同的变量：地理规模（真正外生且恒定）和制度设计（内生且可变）。晚期中华帝国国家的财政虚弱是具有挑战性的地理与几个世纪未能进化的制度机器的共同产物。同一片领土可以根据部署的制度“软件”以截然不同的效力水平来治理，正如清朝、国民党和中华人民共和国之间的戏剧性对比所展示的。中国传统的官僚制度虽然成熟很早、看起来很先进，但其官员的技能、知识和组织方式早已不再匹配经济的实际复杂性。在没有外部军事压力的情况下它可以持续存在；鸦片战争暴露了这种错配。王朝衰落的根源不是中国太大而无法治理，而是其治理制度停止了发展，而其经济和社会却没有。

Shared week context: the blackboard principal-agent model & cross-reading synthesis

Lecture framing: the Feb. 26 class used a principal-agent toy model to organize the week around legibility, monitoring, and centralization. The ruler chooses an institutional form, indirect rule/tax farming (\(I\)) or direct rule/state administration (\(D\)); the agent chooses effort \(e\in\{0,1\}\); and effort affects the probability of high tax revenue \(R\in\{L,H\}\).

\[ \Pr(R=H\mid e=1)=p\in(0,1),\qquad \Pr(R=H\mid e=0)=0. \]

The key monitoring parameter is:

\[ q=\Pr(e=0\mid R=L),\qquad q\in\left[\frac{1}{2},1\right]. \]

Indirect rule/tax farming works through a share contract. The agent's incentive compatibility constraint and the ruler's optimal share are:

\[ pSH\ge k,\qquad S_I^*=\frac{k}{pH}. \]

The ruler's profit under indirect rule is independent of monitoring quality:

\[ \pi_I=p(1-S_I^*)H=pH-k. \]

Direct rule/state administration adds the threat of dismissal. If the agent is retained, the position has continuation value \(V>0\). The incentive constraint, optimal share, and profit are:

\[ pSH+qV\ge k,\qquad S_D^*=\max\left\{0,\frac{k-qV}{pH}\right\}. \]
\[ \pi_D=\begin{cases} pH, & qV>k,\\ pH-k+qV, & qV\le k. \end{cases} \]

The institutional switch from \(I\) to \(D\) requires paying a transition cost \(C\). Direct rule is chosen when:

\[ \pi_D-C\ge\pi_I\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad qV\ge C\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad q^*=\frac{C}{V}. \]

The core takeaway is that tax farming is the fallback institution when monitoring is weak, while direct administration becomes attractive once monitoring and dismissal can discipline agents enough to justify the transition cost.

\[ \pi_D=\begin{cases} pH, & qV>k,\\ pH-k+qV, & qV\le k. \end{cases} \]

从 \(I\) 转向 \(D\) 需要支付制度转换成本 \(C\)。直接统治被选择当且仅当：

\[ \pi_D-C\ge\pi_I\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad qV\ge C\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad q^*=\frac{C}{V}. \]

核心结论：监控弱时，包税制是兜底制度；当监控与解雇威胁足以约束代理人并覆盖转换成本时，科层行政才变得有吸引力。

Kiser (1994)合约选择：包税制 vs. 国家行政征收。税收征集的制度形式。基本外生；税种、国家规模、统治者自主性、控制技术影响监控。
