Dependent variable (DV)

The main dependent variable is state capacity, operationalized as fiscal capacity. The authors measure fiscal capacity through “tax pressure,” defined as per capita tax revenue expressed as a multiple of the country-specific daily urban wage. This measure controls for differences in wealth across countries, allowing for meaningful cross-national and temporal comparisons of a state’s ability to extract resources from its population.

主要因变量是国家能力，具体操作化为财政能力。作者通过“税收压力”来测量财政能力，即人均税收收入相对于该国特定的城市日工资的倍数。这一测量方法控制了不同国家间的财富差异，从而使国家间和跨时期的资源汲取能力比较具有意义（第1412页、第1430-1431页）。

Independent variable (IV)

The main independent variable is the importance of money for military success, denoted by the parameter λ in the theoretical model. This parameter captures how much fiscal resources influence the probability of winning a war. A higher λ means that wealthier states have a significantly greater chance of military victory. The authors conceptualize the “Military Revolution” (a sequence of technological innovations in warfare after 1500) as an increase in λ.

The model also includes domestic divisions (denoted by B), which measure the cost of centralizing taxation when local power holders resist. The interaction between λ and B explains why European powers followed different state-building paths.

主要自变量是金钱对于军事成功的重要性，在理论模型中用参数λ（lambda）表示。该参数反映财政资源在多大程度上影响战争获胜的概率。λ越高，意味着更富裕的国家在战争中获胜的可能性显著更大。作者将“军事革命” (1500年后一系列战争技术创新) 概念化为λ的增加。

此外，模型还纳入了国内分裂程度（用B表示），用以衡量通过克服地方权力持有者的反对来实现税收集中化的成本。λ与B之间的交互作用对于理解欧洲各国国家建设的分化模式至关重要（第1410页、第1418-1419页）。

Stateness

The authors conceptualize state-building primarily as the centralization of the tax system and the process of sidelining domestic power holders such as local nobles, regional assemblies, and other elites who previously controlled or obstructed revenue collection. In their model, a ruler increases state capacity by centralizing districts, bringing them under uniform central taxation rather than allowing local power holders to set taxes and retain revenues for themselves. Centralization increases the ruler’s control over revenue collection, reduces tax distortions (such as overtaxation of market activities by uncoordinated local authorities), and thereby boosts both fiscal revenues and overall economic output.

Empirically, the authors measure state capacity through fiscal capacity indicators. The primary measure is tax pressure, calculated as per capita fiscal revenue divided by the country-specific daily urban wage. This normalization accounts for differences in economic development across countries. They also examine total tax revenue (in tons of silver per annum), army and navy sizes, and administrative reforms such as the transition from tax farming to centralized collection of excise and customs taxes. Historical evidence shows that England increased its tax pressure tenfold between 1500 and 1780, while the Ottoman /ˈɒtəmən/ Empire actually experienced a decline.

The theoretical model treats centralization as a continuous variable \(\kappa \in [0,1]\), representing the fraction of districts brought under central control. Full centralization (\(\kappa = 1\)) means the ruler sets uniform taxes across all districts and collects all fiscal revenues; full decentralization (\(\kappa = 0\)) means local power holders control taxation and keep revenues for themselves. The cost of centralization increases with the parameter \(B\), which captures domestic conflict intensity, how difficult it is for the ruler to buy off or suppress opposition from local elites. Countries with greater ethnic, religious, or regional divisions have higher \(B\) values.

作者主要将国家建设概念化为税收制度的集中化，以及边缘化此前控制或阻碍税收征集的国内权力持有者（如地方贵族、地区议会和其他精英）的过程。在其模型中，统治者通过集中化各地区来提升国家能力，即将各地区纳入统一的中央税收体系，而非允许地方权力持有者自行设定税率并保留税收收入。集中化增强了统治者对税收征集的控制，减少了税收扭曲（如地方当局因缺乏协调而对市场活动过度征税），从而提高了财政收入和整体经济产出（第1410页、第1415-1416页）。

在测量方面，作者通过财政能力指标来测量国家能力。主要测量指标是税收压力，计算方法为人均财政收入除以该国特定的城市日工资。这种标准化处理考虑了不同国家经济发展水平的差异。作者还考察了总税收收入（以每年吨银计算）、陆军和海军规模，以及行政改革（如从包税制向集中征收消费税和关税的转变）。历史证据显示，英格兰在1500年至1780年间税收压力增长了十倍，而奥斯曼帝国实际上出现了下降（第1412页）。

理论模型将集中化处理为一个连续变量 \(\kappa\in[0,1]\)，表示被纳入中央控制的地区比例。完全集中化（\(\kappa=1\)）意味着统治者在所有地区设定统一税率并征收全部财政收入；完全分权（\(\kappa=0\)）意味着地方权力持有者控制税收并保留税收收入。集中化的成本随参数 \(B\) 的增加而增加，\(B\) 反映了国内冲突的强度,即统治者收买或压制地方精英反对力量的难度。民族、宗教或地区分裂程度较高的国家，其 \(B\) 值也较高（第1416-1417页）。

Core argument

The core argument of this article challenges the conventional wisdom articulated by Charles Tilly /ˈtɪli/ that “states made war, and war made states.” The authors argue that war’s impact on state-building is conditional on the importance of money for military success (λ) and the degree of domestic divisions (B). The relationship between war and state-building is not uniformly positive; instead, it depends critically on military technology and initial political fragmentation.

When the importance of money for military success is low (λ ≤ 1), the threat of external conflict actually stifles state-building rather than promoting it. In this scenario, both contending rulers have similar chances of winning a war regardless of their fiscal revenues. Consequently, the richer ruler risks losing his accumulated fiscal resources to a poorer adversary. This creates perverse incentives: warfare is frequent, but rulers have little motivation to invest in centralizing taxation because the returns from doing so are uncertain. Indeed, having greater fiscal resources makes a ruler a more attractive target for predation. The model predicts that under low λ, a higher probability of war (θ) actually reduces state-building efforts across all countries, namely a “race to the bottom”.

When the importance of money for military success becomes high (large λ), the possibility of armed conflict causes strong divergence in state-building. Now the odds of winning a war are stacked heavily in favor of the fiscally stronger state. Cohesive states with low domestic divisions (low B) find it profitable to invest heavily in centralizing taxation because they can accumulate resources and then prey upon weaker neighbors. Conversely, divided states with high costs of centralization (high B) rationally “drop out” of the competition. Their chances of success in military competition are too low to justify the painful domestic reforms needed to centralize taxation. As a result, cohesive states engage in aggressive state-building while divided ones stagnate or even decline. This creates a widening gap between strong and weak states. Eventually, a “race to the top” emerges among competing powers, with all survivors building greater state capacity as they compete more intensely in fiscal and military terms.

The model identifies three distinct phases of state-building as λ increases (Figure 4, p. 1424). First, when λ is very low (λ ≤ 3cH/4, where cH is the marginal cost of centralization for the more cohesive country H), external war causes a “race to the bottom” that discourages state-building in all countries. The state system remains fragmented, the balance of power is unstable, and no strong power emerges. Second, at intermediate levels of λ (3cH/4 < λ < 3cF/4), strong divergence occurs: cohesive countries disproportionately centralize while divided countries restrain or reduce their state-building efforts. The international system now consists of politically strong, economically developed, centralized countries alongside weaker, poorer, less centralized countries that are increasingly likely to fall prey to the strong ones. Third, when λ becomes very high (λ ≥ 3cF/4), all rulers boost their state-building efforts, converging toward full centralization with minimal tax distortions and maximal production (p. 1423-1424).

本文挑战了查尔斯·蒂利提出的“国家制造战争，战争制造国家”这一传统观点。作者认为，战争对国家建设的影响取决于金钱对于军事成功的重要性（λ）以及国内分裂程度（B）。战争与国家建设之间的关系并非一致为正，而是在很大程度上取决于军事技术和初始政治分裂程度。

论文要回答一个经典难题：为什么战争在欧洲长期频繁，但现代国家（尤其财政汲取能力的跃升）却主要在 1500 之后出现？作者给出的答案是：“战争促建国”不是常数关系，而是被军事技术所改变的战争融资结构（即 λ）所调节。军事技术变革（“Military Revolution”）使战争更昂贵、更依赖持续融资，从而提高 λ；在高 λ 的环境里，内部凝聚国家会加税建制以应对威胁，而内部分裂国家在竞争中处于结构劣势，会“give up / drop out”，甚至出现财政能力下降。

这也意味着：同样的外部冲突压力，在低 λ 时更像“税”（tax-like burden）压垮各国的财政建设；在高 λ 时才会在“凝聚 vs 分裂”的国家之间制造分化。

当金钱对于军事成功的重要性较低（λ≤1）时，外部冲突的威胁实际上抑制国家建设而非促进它。在这种情况下，无论财政收入如何，交战双方统治者获胜的机会相近。因此，更富裕的统治者面临将其积累的财政资源输给更贫穷对手的风险。这产生了扭曲的激励：战争频繁，但统治者几乎没有动力投资于税收集中化，因为这样做的回报是不确定的。事实上，拥有更多财政资源反而使统治者成为更具吸引力的掠夺目标。模型预测，在低λ条件下，更高的战争概率（θ）实际上会降低所有国家的国家建设努力，即“竞相逐底”（第1410页、第1420-1421页）。

当金钱对于军事成功的重要性变得很高（λ值大）时，武装冲突的可能性会导致国家建设的强烈分化。此时，战争获胜的概率严重偏向财政实力更强的国家。凝聚力强的国家，即国内分裂程度低（B值低）的国家，发现大力投资税收集中化是有利可图的，因为它们可以积累资源，然后掠夺较弱的邻国。相反，分裂的国家，即集中化成本高（B值高）的国家，理性地选择“退出”竞争。它们在军事竞争中获胜的机会太低，不足以证明实现税收集中化所需的痛苦国内改革是值得的。结果是，凝聚力强的国家进行积极的国家建设，而分裂的国家则停滞甚至衰退。这造成了强国与弱国之间差距的扩大。最终，在相互竞争的大国之间出现了“竞相争顶”的局面，所有幸存者都在财政和军事方面更加激烈的竞争中建设更强的国家能力（第1410页、第1423-1424页）。

模型识别出随着λ增加而出现的三个不同的国家建设阶段（见图4，第1424页）。第一，当λ非常低（λ≤3cH/4，其中cH是凝聚力更强的国家H的集中化边际成本）时，外部战争导致“竞相逐底”，抑制所有国家的国家建设。国家体系保持分裂，权力平衡不稳定，没有强国出现。第二，在中等水平的λ（3cH/4<λ<3cF/4）条件下，出现强烈分化：凝聚力强的国家过度集中化，而分裂的国家则限制或减少其国家建设努力。此时的国际体系由政治上强大、经济发达、集中化的国家与较弱、较贫穷、集中化程度较低的国家并存，后者越来越可能沦为前者的猎物。第三，当λ变得非常高（λ≥3cF/4）时，所有统治者都加强其国家建设努力，趋向于完全集中化，税收扭曲最小化，产出最大化（第1423-1424页）。

Mechanisms

(1) The Military Revolution and Rising λ

The “Military Revolution” fundamentally transformed the relationship between fiscal resources and military success. Three key technological and organizational changes drove this transformation. First, the spread of cannon after 1400 made medieval walls obsolete, but Italian military engineers devised the trace italienne /trɑːs itɑːliˈen/, new fortifications with earthen bulwarks covered by bricks that could withstand cannon fire. These fortifications were immensely costly to build (Louis XIV allegedly asked if the fortress of Besançon /bəˈzɑ̃sɔ̃/ was made of gold when informed of its cost). The existence of numerous strongpoints meant that wars dragged on for long periods, requiring sustained expenditure (p. 1413).

Second, the introduction of standing armies added to rising costs. Due to the need for firearms training, states began to organize, equip, and drill soldiers continuously, investing in their human capital. Starting with William of Nassau’s /ˈnæsɔː/ reforms during the Dutch rebellion, soldiers were garrisoned and trained year-round rather than being raised only for campaigns. Third, states began to organize permanent navies with large numbers of warships kept in readiness. Even smaller ships in the 18th-century English navy cost more than the largest industrial companies had in capital. In combination, these changes made war vastly more expensive and increasingly dependent on sustained fiscal resources rather than feudal dues or temporary levies (pp. 1413–1414).

(2) The Contest Success Function and War Incentives

The theoretical mechanism operates through a contest success function that determines the probability of winning a war. The probability that ruler \(H\) wins is given by:

\[ p(R_H,R_F)= \frac{L_H^{\alpha}R_H^{\lambda}} {L_H^{\alpha}R_H^{\lambda}+L_F^{\alpha}R_F^{\lambda}}. \]

Here \(L_J\) represents army size and \(R_J\) represents fiscal revenues for country \(J\). The parameter \(\lambda\) measures the “money sensitivity of military strength.” When \(\lambda\) is high, fiscal revenues matter for victory; when \(\lambda\) is low, other factors (such as luck, terrain, or individual commander ability) dominate (pp. 1418–1419).

This contest function generates three direct effects of war on the incentive to centralize (see equations 4.5 and 4.6, p. 1420). First, war creates an offensive incentive: higher fiscal revenues enhance the probability of winning and allow the ruler to predate on his competitor and seize their resources. Second, war creates a defensive risk: by increasing fiscal revenue, a ruler becomes a more attractive prey, which stunts the incentive to centralize. Third, war imposes a resource cost: fiscal revenues must be spent on military operations during wartime, reducing the benefits of centralization. The net effect of war on state-building depends on which of these forces dominates, which in turn depends on λ (p. 1420).

(3) Domestic Opposition and the Cost of Centralization

State-building requires overcoming domestic opposition from local power holders who benefit from the decentralized status quo. In the decentralized regime, local power holders (nobles, regional assemblies, urban elites) control taxation in their districts and retain the revenues for themselves. They have no incentive to coordinate on efficient taxation; instead, each tries to steal revenue from others by overtaxing market activities that cross district boundaries. This creates economic inefficiency but benefits local elites (pp. 1415–1416).

Centralizing taxation requires the ruler to buy off or suppress these power holders, at a cost that increases with the degree of domestic conflict \(B\). The authors assume that the cost of centralizing district \(i\) is:

\[ \beta_i\,2(A_l-A_h), \]

where \(\beta_i\) measures the ability and willingness of the local power holder to resist, and \((A_l-A_h)\) represents the tax rent the power holder loses under centralization. The distribution of \(\beta_i\) across districts, captured by the c.d.f. \(H(\beta)\), is lower in a first-order stochastic dominance sense for countries with greater ethnic, religious, or regional divisions. The ruler centralizes districts in order of resistance, starting with low-\(\beta\) districts and moving to more hostile ones. The marginal cost of centralization is therefore increasing, creating a convex cost function:

\[ C(R)=cR^2,\qquad c\equiv \frac{B(A_l-A_h)}{(A_m-A_h)^2}. \]

(4) Strategic Interaction and Divergence

Strategic interaction between competing rulers drives divergence. When λ is low, the reaction functions of the two rulers have opposite slopes: the cohesive ruler’s reaction function is negatively sloped, while the divided ruler’s is positively sloped. This means that if the divided ruler centralizes more, the cohesive ruler actually centralizes less in response. Because when λ is low, even a poor opponent has a reasonable chance of winning, making the cohesive ruler’s accumulated wealth a liability. This dampens divergence under low λ (p. 1421, Figure 3, p. 1422).

When λ is high, this strategic logic reverses (Corollary 1, p. 1425). Now, stronger centralization by the divided ruler increases the cohesive ruler’s incentive to centralize further, because high money sensitivity makes a richer opponent more attractive to attack. If the divided country is also weaker militarily (γ < 1 in the linearized model), divergence becomes extremely pronounced. As money sensitivity increases, the cohesive country centralizes aggressively while the divided country may actually reduce its state-building efforts. Unable to withstand the competition, the divided country gives up.

(5) Spoils of War and Resource Redistribution

Wars in early modern Europe did more than destroy; they systematically redistributed resources from losers to winners. This redistribution took three main forms: territorial conquests within Europe, transfers of overseas possessions, and war indemnities. In the 42 main peace settlements between 1500 and 1780, some 20.6 million square kilometers were redistributed, equivalent to 14% of the earth’s land surface. Conquered territories provided substantial tax revenues to their new rulers; for example, Prussia /ˈprʌʃə/ doubled the tax revenue from Silesia /saɪˈliːʒə/ after conquering it from Austria /ˈɒstriə/ in 1740. Frederick /ˈfredərɪk/ the Great extracted 55-82 million Thaler from occupied Saxony /ˈsæksəni/, Mecklenburg /ˈmeklənbɜːrɡ/, and Pomerania /ˌpɒməˈreɪniə/ during the Seven Years War, enough to cover one-third to one-half of the war’s cost (p. 1414).

This mechanism of resource redistribution amplifies the effects of λ on state-building. When λ is high and the cohesive state is likely to win, war transfers fiscal resources and territories from weaker, divided states to stronger, cohesive ones, accelerating the latter’s state-building while devastating the former. When λ is low and outcomes are more random, resource redistribution is less systematically directed, but the risk of loss still discourages investment in state capacity by the relatively wealthy (pp. 1427–1428).

(6) Endogenous War and Selection Effects

In an extension of the model (Section 5.2), the authors endogenize the decision to go to war. Given the option not to fight, war occurs when one ruler expects to benefit from initiating conflict. The identity of the initiating party depends on λ. When λ is low, the poorer, divided country may initiate war because, even though its probability of winning is below 50%, the payoff from conquering a wealthy opponent is attractive relative to its low stakes. When λ is high, the richer, cohesive country initiates war because it is disproportionately likely to win and can prey upon the weaker opponent. This creates additional forces toward either convergence (when the poor attack the rich under low λ) or divergence (when the rich attack the poor under high λ) (Proposition 3, p. 1427-1428).

（1）军事革命与λ的上升

“军事革命”从根本上改变了财政资源与军事成功之间的关系。三个关键的技术和组织变革推动了这一转变。首先，1400年后大炮的普及使中世纪城墙过时，但意大利军事工程师设计了“意大利式棱堡”，即由砖块覆盖的土质堡垒组成的新型防御工事，能够抵御炮火。这些防御工事造价极其昂贵（据说路易十四得知贝桑松堡垒的造价后，质问它是否由黄金建造）。众多据点的存在意味着战争会持续很长时间，需要持续的支出（第1413页）。

第二，常备军的建立增加了不断上升的成本。由于需要进行火器训练，国家开始持续组织、装备和训练士兵，对其人力资本进行投资。从荷兰叛乱期间拿骚的威廉改革开始，士兵全年驻扎和训练，而不是仅在战役期间才被征召。第三，国家开始组建常设海军，大量战舰随时待命。即使是18世纪英国海军中较小的舰船，其造价也超过了最大工业公司的资本总额。综合来看，这些变化使战争变得更加昂贵，越来越依赖持续的财政资源，而非封建贡赋或临时征税（第1413-1414页）。

（2）竞争成功函数与战争激励

理论机制通过一个决定战争获胜概率的竞争成功函数运作。统治者 \(H\) 获胜的概率为：

\[ p(R_H,R_F)= \frac{L_H^{\alpha}R_H^{\lambda}} {L_H^{\alpha}R_H^{\lambda}+L_F^{\alpha}R_F^{\lambda}}. \]

其中 \(L_J\) 表示国家 \(J\) 的军队规模，\(R_J\) 表示国家 \(J\) 的财政收入。参数 \(\lambda\) 衡量“军事力量的金钱敏感度”。当 \(\lambda\) 较高时，财政收入对胜利至关重要；当 \(\lambda\) 较低时，其他因素（如运气、地形或个别指挥官的能力）占主导地位（第1418-1419页）。

这一竞争函数产生了战争对集中化激励的三个直接效应（见公式4.5和4.6，第1420页）。第一，战争产生进攻性激励：更高的财政收入提高获胜概率，使统治者能够掠夺竞争对手并夺取其资源。第二，战争产生防御性风险：通过增加财政收入，统治者成为更具吸引力的猎物，这抑制了集中化的激励。第三，战争施加资源成本：财政收入必须在战时用于军事行动，减少了集中化带来的消费收益。战争对国家建设的净效应取决于这些力量中哪个占主导，而这又取决于λ（第1420页）。

（3）国内反对与集中化成本

国家建设需要克服从分权现状中受益的地方权力持有者的国内反对。在分权体制下，地方权力持有者（贵族、地区议会、城市精英）控制其辖区内的税收并保留税收收入。他们没有动力协调实施有效的税收；相反，每个人都试图通过对跨越地区边界的市场活动过度征税来窃取他人的收入。这造成了经济效率低下，但有利于地方精英（第1415-1416页）。

实现税收集中化要求统治者收买或压制这些权力持有者，其成本随国内冲突程度 \(B\) 的增加而增加。作者假设集中化地区 \(i\) 的成本为：

\[ \beta_i\,2(A_l-A_h), \]

其中 \(\beta_i\) 衡量地方权力持有者抵抗的能力和意愿，\((A_l-A_h)\) 代表权力持有者在集中化下损失的税收租金。\(\beta_i\) 在各地区的分布由累积分布函数 \(H(\beta)\) 描述，对于民族、宗教或地区分裂程度较高的国家，该分布在一阶随机占优意义上较低。统治者按抵抗程度的顺序集中化各地区，从低 \(\beta\) 地区开始，逐步向更具敌意的地区推进。因此，集中化的边际成本是递增的，形成凸成本函数：

\[ C(R)=cR^2,\qquad c\equiv \frac{B(A_l-A_h)}{(A_m-A_h)^2}. \]

（4）战略互动与分化

导致分化的一个关键机制是竞争统治者之间的战略互动。当λ较低时，两位统治者的反应函数斜率相反：凝聚力强的统治者的反应函数斜率为负，而分裂统治者的反应函数斜率为正。这意味着如果分裂的统治者加强集中化，凝聚力强的统治者实际上会相应减少集中化，因为当λ较低时，即使是贫穷的对手也有合理的获胜机会，使得凝聚力强的统治者积累的财富成为负担。这在低λ条件下抑制了分化（第1421页，图3，第1422页）。

当λ较高时，这种战略逻辑发生逆转（推论1，第1425页）。此时，分裂统治者的更强集中化实际上增加了凝聚力强的统治者进一步集中化的激励，因为当金钱敏感度较高时，凝聚力强的统治者渴望掠夺更富裕的对手。如果分裂的国家在军事上也较弱（在线性化模型中γ<1），分化就会变得极为明显。随着金钱敏感度的增加，凝聚力强的国家积极集中化，而分裂的国家可能实际上减少其国家建设努力。无法承受竞争，分裂的国家选择放弃。

（5）战争果实与资源再分配

近代早期欧洲的战争不仅仅是破坏性的；它们系统性地将资源从失败者再分配给胜利者。这种再分配主要采取三种形式：欧洲内部的领土征服、海外属地的转让以及战争赔款。在1500年至1780年间的42项主要和平协定中，约2060万平方公里的领土被重新分配，相当于地球陆地面积的14%。被征服的领土为新统治者提供了大量税收收入；例如，普鲁士在1740年从奥地利征服西里西亚后，使该地税收收入翻倍。腓特烈大帝在七年战争期间从占领的萨克森、梅克伦堡和波美拉尼亚提取了5500万至8200万塔勒，足以支付战争成本的三分之一至二分之一（第1414页）。

这种资源再分配机制放大了λ对国家建设的影响。当λ较高且凝聚力强的国家可能获胜时，战争将财政资源和领土从较弱的、分裂的国家转移给较强的、凝聚力强的国家，加速后者的国家建设，同时摧毁前者。当λ较低且结果更加随机时，资源再分配的方向性不那么系统，但损失风险仍然抑制了相对富裕者对国家能力的投资（第1427-1428页）。

（6）内生战争与选择效应

在模型的扩展部分（第5.2节），作者将战争决策内生化。在有选择不战斗的情况下，当一方统治者预期从发动冲突中获益时，战争就会发生。发动战争一方的身份取决于λ。当λ较低时，更贫穷、分裂的国家可能发动战争，因为尽管其获胜概率低于50%，但相对于其低赌注，征服富裕对手的回报是有吸引力的。当λ较高时，更富裕、凝聚力强的国家发动战争，因为它获胜的可能性过高，可以掠夺较弱的对手。这产生了趋向收敛（当低λ时穷国攻击富国）或分化（当高λ时富国攻击穷国）的额外力量（命题3，第1427-1428页）。

Data & methods

Data

The primary data for measuring the money sensitivity of military success (λ) comes from 374 battles fought in Europe between 1500 and 1800. Battle information is drawn from Jaques (2007), Dictionary of Battles and Sieges, while outcomes are coded from Landers (2003), The Field and the Forge. The authors exclude sieges, civil conflicts, and peasant revolts. Of the 374 battles, 80 were naval engagements. For each battle, the outcome is coded as a binary variable: success (1) or defeat (0) for the fiscally stronger power (p. 1428-1429, Appendix E, p. 1446).

Fiscal data comes from the European State Finance Database (ESFD), as compiled by Bonney (1989) and augmented and summarized by Karaman and Pamuk (2010). The key measure is tax pressure, defined as per capita fiscal revenue divided by the country-specific daily urban wage. This normalization allows meaningful comparison across countries with different levels of economic development. Total tax revenue is also measured in tons of silver per annum. The data cover 17 European states over the period 1500-1780, organized into 50-year periods (p. 1429, 1431).

Population data comes from McEvedy and Jones (1978), Atlas of World Population History. For each combatant state, population is recorded at the beginning of the period to which the fiscal data refers, measured in millions (Appendix E, p. 1446).

The authors use three proxy measures for the cost of centralization arising from domestic opposition. First, the number of predecessor states in 1300 on the territory of countries existing in 1500 (using 1500 borders). This variable proxies for the extent to which local power holders can resist centripetal forces; for example, Spain’s difficulty in raising revenue outside Castile /kæˈstiːl/ reflects the problems of territorial expansion. All figures are based on historical maps from euratlas.net. Second, total surface area is an indicator of potential opposition, since physical distance constrained an early modern ruler’s ability to project power, and larger states are more likely to be culturally and linguistically diverse. Third, ethnic heterogeneity is taken from Alesina et al. (2003) as a proxy for internal fragmentation. The authors construct a composite measure “Sum” by standardizing all three variables to have zero mean and unit standard deviation, then summing them for each country (p. 1430-1431, Appendix E, p. 1446).

Additional control variables include: Allies, a dummy variable for battles fought with allies (source: Jaques 2007); Naval, a dummy variable for engagements at sea (source: Landers 2003); ConsExec, constraints on the executive from the Polity database as used by Acemoglu et al. (2005); Area, total surface area calculated in Q-GIS from euratlas.net historical maps; Slope, average slope of terrain as a measure of ruggedness; Pop200, the share of population within 200 km of the capital; and WarFreq, the frequency of warfare in each 50-year period. The instrument for war frequency is WarNeighbour, the frequency of war amongst immediately adjacent states in the same period, excluding wars of the country in question (Appendix E, p. 1446; Table 6, p. 1436).

The geographic coverage includes major European powers: England, France, the Dutch Republic, Spain, Austria, Prussia, Russia, Poland, the Ottoman Empire, and Venice /ˈvenɪs/, among others (Figure 1, p. 1412; Table 2, p. 1413). The time period spans 1500-1800, with the main analysis focusing on 1500-1780. Data are typically organized into 50-year periods (e.g., 1500-1549, 1550-1599, etc.) for panel analysis.

The unit of analysis varies across different empirical tests. For the battle outcome analysis, the unit is the individual battle (374 observations). For the fiscal capacity analysis, the unit is the country-period (e.g., England 1650-1699), yielding 53 observations in most specifications. For the IV analysis, the unit is similarly country-period with 45-54 observations depending on data availability (Tables 3-6).

用于测量军事成功的金钱敏感度（λ）的主要数据来自1500年至1800年间在欧洲进行的374场战役。战役信息取自雅克（2007年）的《战役与围城词典》，而结果编码则来自兰德斯（2003年）的《战场与锻造》。作者排除了围城战、内战和农民起义。在374场战役中，80场是海战。对于每场战役，结果被编码为二元变量：财政实力较强的国家获胜（1）或失败（0）（第1428-1429页，附录E，第1446页）。

财政数据来自欧洲国家财政数据库（ESFD），由邦尼（1989年）编纂，并由卡拉曼和帕穆克（2010年）补充和汇总。关键测量指标是税收压力，定义为人均财政收入除以该国特定的城市日工资。这种标准化处理使得不同经济发展水平国家之间的比较具有意义。总税收收入也以每年吨银计量。数据涵盖1500-1780年期间的17个欧洲国家，按50年期划分（第1429、1431页）。

人口数据来自麦克维迪和琼斯（1978年）的《世界人口历史地图集》。对于每个交战国，人口记录在财政数据所属时期的开始时点，以百万人计（附录E，第1446页）。

作者使用三个代理指标来测量源于国内反对的集中化成本。第一，1500年存在的国家领土上1300年的前身国家数量（使用1500年边界）。这一变量代表地方权力持有者抵抗向心力的程度；例如，西班牙在卡斯蒂利亚以外地区征税困难反映了领土扩张的问题。所有数据基于euratlas.net的历史地图。第二，总面积作为潜在反对的指标，因为物理距离限制了近代早期统治者投射权力的能力，且较大的国家更可能在文化和语言上多样化。第三，民族异质性取自阿莱西纳等（2003年），作为内部分裂的代理。作者通过将三个变量标准化为零均值和单位标准差，然后将每个国家的值相加，构建了一个综合指标“Sum”（第1430-1431页，附录E，第1446页）。

其他控制变量包括：Allies，有盟友参战的战役虚拟变量（来源：雅克2007年）；Naval，海战虚拟变量（来源：兰德斯2003年）；ConsExec，来自Polity数据库的行政约束，如阿西莫格鲁等（2005年）所使用；Area，使用Q-GIS从euratlas.net历史地图计算的总面积；Slope，作为地形崎岖度测量的平均坡度；Pop200，距首都200公里范围内的人口比例；以及WarFreq，每50年期间的战争频率。战争频率的工具变量是WarNeighbour，同一时期紧邻国家之间的战争频率，不包括该国本身的战争（附录E，第1446页；表6，第1436页）。

地理覆盖范围包括主要欧洲列强：英格兰、法国、荷兰共和国、西班牙、奥地利、普鲁士、俄罗斯、波兰、奥斯曼帝国和威尼斯等（图1，第1412页；表2，第1413页）。时间段跨越1500-1800年，主要分析集中于1500-1780年。数据通常按50年期（如1500-1549、1550-1599等）组织，用于面板分析。

分析单位在不同的经验检验中有所不同。对于战役结果分析，分析单位是单场战役（374个观察值）。对于财政能力分析，分析单位是国家-时期（如英格兰1650-1699年），在大多数设定中产生53个观察值。对于工具变量分析，分析单位同样是国家-时期，根据数据可用性有45-54个观察值（表3-6）。

Methods

To demonstrate that fiscal resources became increasingly important for military success, the authors estimate probit models of battlefield outcomes. The dependent variable is a dummy equal to 1 if the fiscally stronger power wins a battle. The key independent variable is Diffrev, the log difference in total fiscal revenue between the richer and poorer powers: Diffrev = ln(TH,t) - ln(TL,t). They also control for Diffpop (log difference in population), Allies (dummy for allied battles), and Naval (dummy for naval engagements). Standard errors are clustered at the opponent-pair-period level to account for non-independence of battles between the same adversaries in the same period.

To capture the time-varying nature of λ, the authors split the sample at 1650 and estimate separate regressions for the pre-1650 and post-1650 periods. They also generate a time-varying λt by regressing battle outcomes on the difference in log revenues period-by-period. This approach allows them to track how the importance of money for battlefield success changed over time as the Military Revolution unfolded.

The core prediction of the model is that the effect of war threat on state-building depends on the interaction between money sensitivity \((\lambda)\) and domestic divisions \((B)\). The authors test this by estimating:

\[ R_{i,t}=C+\beta B_{i,t}+\delta\lambda_t+\rho(B_{i,t}\lambda_t)+\varepsilon_{i,t}. \]

where \(R_{i,t}\) is tax revenue (as a multiple of wages) in country \(i\) at time \(t\), \(B_{i,t}\) is the measure of fragmentation, \(\lambda_t\) is the time-varying estimate of money sensitivity, and \(B_{i,t}\lambda_t\) is the interaction term. The coefficient \(\rho\) on the interaction term captures whether fragmented countries respond differently to increases in \(\lambda\). The model predicts \(\rho<0\): as \(\lambda\) increases, more fragmented powers should invest less (or even disinvest) in fiscal capacity relative to cohesive powers. Standard errors are clustered at the period level to account for the fact that \(\lambda_t\) varies only by period (p. 1435, Table 5, p. 1434).

To illustrate cross-sectional patterns, the authors estimate separate regressions of fiscal capacity on λ for each country individually. This yields a country-specific responsiveness coefficient that captures how strongly each country’s revenue-raising responded to overall changes in the military importance of money. They then plot these coefficients alongside the composite fragmentation measure to demonstrate the inverse relationship: countries with low fragmentation (like England and Prussia) show high responsiveness to λ, while highly fragmented countries (like Poland and the Ottoman Empire) show almost no responsiveness.

To address the endogeneity of war frequency (since war is a choice that depends on fiscal strength), the authors use war frequency in neighboring states (excluding the country itself) as an instrument for war frequency in the country in question. The logic is that war in Europe came in waves, and frequent war among neighbors heightened a ruler’s expectation that he too might be affected. The exclusion restriction requires that war frequency in neighboring country j affects fiscal capacity in country i only through the risk of war affecting country i directly, not through other channels. The first stage shows that war in neighboring states is a strong predictor of war in each individual country. The IV estimates show that the instrumented variation (fiscal capacity increases driven by the threat of war from neighbors) is even more strongly associated with revenue-raising than simple war frequency .

For the probit estimates to identify the causal effect of fiscal resources on military outcomes, the key assumption is that there are no omitted variables correlated with both Diffrev and battle outcomes. The authors control for population differences, allied participation, and naval versus land battles, and use time fixed effects in some specifications. However, unobserved factors such as commander quality, tactical innovations, or defensive terrain advantages could bias the estimates.

For the interaction regressions to be valid, the measure of domestic divisions (B) must be pre-determined relative to the state-building outcomes. The authors argue that the number of predecessor states in 1300 is their preferred measure precisely because it predates the period of analysis and does not depend on endogenous responses to state-building or conquest. However, ethnic heterogeneity and surface area may be partly endogenous if successful states expanded their territories or homogenized their populations.

For the IV estimates, the exclusion restriction requires that neighboring countries’ war frequency affects domestic fiscal capacity only through the channel of increased war risk, instead of through trade disruptions, refugee flows, or other mechanisms. This assumption may be questionable if wars among neighbors directly affected economic conditions in country i.

为了证明财政资源对军事成功变得越来越重要，作者估计了战役结果的probit模型。因变量是一个虚拟变量，如果财政实力较强的国家赢得战役则为1。关键自变量是Diffrev，即较富裕和较贫穷国家之间总财政收入的对数差：Diffrev = ln(TH,t) - ln(TL,t)。他们还控制了Diffpop（人口对数差）、Allies（盟友战役虚拟变量）和Naval（海战虚拟变量）。标准误在对手对-时期层面聚类，以考虑同一时期同一对手之间战役的非独立性（第1429-1430页，表3，第1430页）。

为了捕捉λ的时变特性，作者在1650年将样本分割，分别对1650年前和1650年后的时期进行回归。他们还通过逐期对战役结果与收入对数差进行回归，生成时变的λt。这种方法使他们能够追踪随着军事革命的展开，金钱对战场成功重要性的变化（第1430、1433页）。

模型的核心预测是，战争威胁对国家建设的影响取决于金钱敏感度（λ）与国内分裂程度（B）之间的交互作用。作者通过估计以下方程来检验这一点：

\[ R_{i,t}=C+\beta B_{i,t}+\delta\lambda_t+\rho(B_{i,t}\lambda_t)+\varepsilon_{i,t}. \]

其中 \(R_{i,t}\) 是国家 \(i\) 在时间 \(t\) 的税收收入（作为工资的倍数），\(B_{i,t}\) 是分裂程度的测量，\(\lambda_t\) 是金钱敏感度的时变估计值，\(B_{i,t}\lambda_t\) 是交互项。交互项的系数 \(\rho\) 捕捉分裂的国家是否对 \(\lambda\) 的增加有不同的反应。模型预测 \(\rho<0\)：随着 \(\lambda\) 的增加，更分裂的国家相对于凝聚力强的国家应该减少（甚至撤回）对财政能力的投资。标准误在时期层面聚类，以考虑 \(\lambda_t\) 仅按时期变化的事实（第1435页，表5，第1434页）。

为了说明横截面模式，作者为每个国家单独估计财政能力对λ的回归。这产生了一个国家特定的响应系数，捕捉每个国家的税收收入对金钱军事重要性总体变化的反应强度。然后他们将这些系数与综合分裂指标一起绘制，以展示反向关系：低分裂程度的国家（如英格兰和普鲁士）对λ显示出高响应性，而高度分裂的国家（如波兰和奥斯曼帝国）几乎没有响应性（图7，第1436页）。

为了解决战争频率的内生性问题（因为战争是一个取决于财政实力的选择），作者使用邻国的战争频率（不包括该国本身）作为该国战争频率的工具变量。逻辑是欧洲的战争呈波浪式出现，邻国之间频繁的战争提高了统治者认为自己也可能受到影响的预期。排除性限制要求邻国j的战争频率仅通过直接影响国家i的战争风险来影响国家i的财政能力，而非通过其他渠道。第一阶段显示邻国的战争是每个国家战争的强预测因子。工具变量估计显示，被工具化的变异，由邻国战争威胁驱动的财政能力增加，与税收收入的关联甚至比简单的战争频率更强（表6，第1436页）。

为使probit估计能够识别财政资源对军事结果的因果效应，关键假设是不存在与Diffrev和战役结果同时相关的遗漏变量。作者控制了人口差异、盟友参与以及海战与陆战，并在某些设定中使用时间固定效应。然而，未观察到的因素如指挥官素质、战术创新或防御地形优势可能使估计产生偏差（第1430页）。

为使交互回归有效，国内分裂程度（B）的测量必须相对于国家建设结果是预先确定的。作者认为1300年的前身国家数量是他们偏好的测量指标，正是因为它早于分析时期，不依赖于国家建设或征服的内生反应。然而，民族异质性和面积可能部分内生，如果成功的国家扩大了领土或同质化了人口的话（第1430-1431页）。

对于工具变量估计，排除性限制要求邻国的战争频率仅通过增加战争风险的渠道影响国内财政能力，而非通过贸易中断、难民流动或其他机制。如果邻国之间的战争直接影响了国家i的经济条件，这一假设可能值得质疑（第1435-1436页）。
