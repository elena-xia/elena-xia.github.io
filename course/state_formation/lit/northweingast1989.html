Week 9 · Organization–Representation lane · Early modern (S3)

Douglass C. North & Barry R. Weingast (1989), “Constitutions and Commitment,” J. Econ. Hist.诺思、温加斯特（1989）《宪法与承诺》。

Analytic narrativeDeductive theoryCredible commitment

Dependent variable (DV)

The main dependent variable is the credibility of the government’s commitment to secure property rights and to honor its financial agreements, as reflected in the development of public and private capital markets in England. The authors operationalize this through several empirical indicators: the volume and terms of government borrowing (the magnitude of government debt and the interest rates at which the government could borrow), the growth of private capital markets (including stock market volume, bank notes in circulation, and Bank of England drawing accounts), and the general security of private property rights (pp. 803–804, 819, 824–828).

主要因变量是政府对保障产权和履行其财务协议的承诺的可信度，其体现在英格兰公共和私人资本市场的发展之中。作者通过若干经验指标将其操作化：政府借款的规模与条件（政府债务的量级以及政府能够借款的利率）、私人资本市场的增长（包括股票市场交易量、流通中的银行券，以及英格兰银行的往来账户），以及私人产权的总体安全程度（pp. 803–804, 819, 824–828）。

Independent variable (IV)

The main independent variable is the set of institutional changes that followed the Glorious Revolution of 1688, including the establishment of parliamentary supremacy, the curtailment of the royal prerogative, the creation of an independent judiciary, parliamentary control over taxation and expenditure, and the fiscal innovations that accompanied these constitutional reforms. The authors treat these institutional changes as a package of reforms that fundamentally restructured the relationship between the Crown and Parliament (pp. 804, 815–817).

主要自变量是 1688 年光荣革命之后随之而来的一整套制度变革，包括确立议会至上、限制王室特权、创设独立的司法机构、议会对征税和支出的控制，以及伴随这些宪制改革的财政创新。作者将这些制度变革视为一揽子改革，它们从根本上重构了王室与议会之间的关系（pp. 804, 815–817）。

Stateness

North and Weingast conceptualize the state primarily in terms of the sovereign’s coercive power and its capacity to extract resources from society. The state is understood as an entity with a comparative advantage in coercion, and the central analytical problem is what prevents the state from using this coercive power to expropriate all surplus from society (p. 806). In this framework, the “state” under the Stuart /ˈstjuːərt/ monarchs is characterized by the concentration of executive, legislative, and judicial powers in the Crown, which enabled the sovereign to alter property rights unilaterally. The key institutional features of this pre-1688 state include the royal prerogative (quasi-legislative power to issue proclamations without Parliament), the Star Chamber (combining legislative, executive, and judicial powers to enforce Crown policies), and Crown control over the judiciary (judges served at the king’s pleasure and could be dismissed for rulings against the Crown) (pp. 813–814).

North 和 Weingast 主要从主权者的强制权力及其从社会汲取资源的能力来构想国家。国家被理解为一个在强制方面具有比较优势的实体，而核心的分析问题是：什么阻止国家利用这种强制权力来攫取社会的全部剩余（p. 806）。在这一框架中，Stuart 王朝君主治下的“国家”的特征是行政、立法和司法权力集中于王室，这使主权者能够单方面改变产权。这一 1688 年之前国家的关键制度特征包括：王室特权（无需议会即可发布公告的准立法权）、星室法庭（Star Chamber，集立法、行政和司法权力于一体以执行王室政策），以及王室对司法机构的控制（法官任职取决于国王的意志，若作出不利于王室的裁决即可能被免职）（pp. 813–814）。

Core argument

The core argument is that sustained economic growth requires the sovereign or government to establish property rights and make a credible commitment to uphold them (p. 803). Rules that the sovereign can readily revise at will differ fundamentally from the same rules when they are not subject to unilateral revision. The more likely it is that the sovereign will alter property rights for its own benefit, the lower the expected returns from investment and hence the lower the incentive to invest (p. 803).

The authors argue that the institutional changes following the Glorious Revolution of 1688 solved this commitment problem. Before 1688, the Stuart monarchs repeatedly reneged on financial agreements, expropriated wealth through monopoly grants, forced loans, and manipulation of the legal system, and concentrated executive, legislative, and judicial powers in the Crown, all of which made property rights deeply insecure (pp. 808–814). The post-1688 institutional settlement solved this by requiring parliamentary consent for changes in policy, taxation, and the terms of loans; by establishing an independent judiciary; and by creating a balance of power between Crown and Parliament that prevented either from acting arbitrarily (pp. 815–819). These arrangements were self-enforcing: the Crown accepted the constraints in exchange for a sound fiscal foundation, while Parliament limited its own potential for abuse through the division of powers and the independence of common law courts (pp. 817–819).

核心论点是：为了获得持续的经济增长，主权者或政府不仅必须确立产权，而且必须对维护产权作出可信的承诺（p. 803）。主权者可以随意轻易修改的规则，与不受单方面修改的同样规则，有着根本的不同。主权者为其自身利益而改变产权的可能性越大，投资的预期回报就越低，因而投资的激励也就越低（p. 803）。

作者认为，1688 年光荣革命之后的制度变革解决了这一承诺问题。1688 年之前，Stuart 王朝君主一再违背财务协议，通过垄断特许、强制借贷和操纵法律体系来攫取财富，并将行政、立法和司法权力集中于王室，所有这些都使产权极不安全（pp. 808–814）。1688 年之后的制度安排通过以下方式解决了这一问题：要求政策、征税和贷款条款的变更须经议会同意；确立独立的司法机构；并在王室与议会之间建立一种权力制衡，防止任何一方任意行事（pp. 815–819）。这些安排是自我执行的：王室接受这些约束以换取稳健的财政基础，而议会则通过权力划分和普通法法院的独立性来限制自身滥权的可能（pp. 817–819）。

Mechanisms

1. Wealth holders got veto power through parliamentary consent. By requiring Parliament’s assent to major changes in policies, taxation, and the terms of loans, the representatives of wealth holders could veto any move that was not also in their interest. This eliminated the Crown’s unilateral ability to alter agreements (p. 817). Since Parliament represented the interests of those who would be expropriated, giving Parliament veto power meant that expropriation could only occur if the victims themselves consented to it.

2. Alternative revenue sources and escape routes had been eliminated. Channels the Crown had previously used to break commitments were closed off: the royal prerogative (allowing unilateral legislation) was curtailed and subordinated to common law; the prerogative courts that enforced Crown proclamations were abolished; and the Crown’s independent sources of revenue were limited, making it dependent on Parliament for funding (pp. 816–817). Without independent revenue, the Crown could not bypass Parliament; without the prerogative courts, it could not enforce unilateral changes in rights.

3. Independent judiciary functioned as a bonding. The creation of a politically independent judiciary enabled judges not to serve the king, which greatly expanded the government’s ability to credibly promise to honor its agreements. The courts could enforce contracts and property rights against the government itself, providing a credible bond (pp. 816, 819). This mechanism is particularly important because it meant that even if the political branches wished to renege, an independent court system would stand as an obstacle.

4. Credible threat of removal. The successful dethroning of Charles /tʃɑːrlz/ I and later James /dʒeɪmz/ II established a credible threat to any future monarch who engaged in irresponsible behavior.

5. Fiscal bargain functioned as mutual benefit. In exchange for greater parliamentary control, parliamentary interests agreed to provide sufficient tax revenue. This removed the fiscal crises that had been the primary motive for the Crown’s predatory behavior, and it gave William /ˈwɪljəm/ III the resources to wage war against France. The arrangement was thus mutually beneficial and self-enforcing.

1. 财富持有者通过议会同意获得了否决权。通过要求议会对政策、征税和贷款条款的重大变更表示同意，财富持有者的代表能够否决任何不符合他们利益的举动。这消除了王室单方面改变协议的能力（p. 817）。由于议会代表了那些可能被攫取者的利益，赋予议会否决权意味着只有在受害者本人同意的情况下攫取才可能发生。

2. 替代性收入来源和逃避途径被消除了。王室先前用来违背承诺的渠道被封堵：王室特权（允许单方面立法）被削减并从属于普通法；执行王室公告的特权法院被废除；王室的独立收入来源受到限制，使其在资金上依赖于议会（pp. 816–817）。没有独立收入，王室就无法绕过议会；没有特权法院，它就无法执行对权利的单方面变更。

3. 独立的司法机构充当了一种担保。政治上独立的司法机构的创设使法官不再服务于国王，这极大地扩展了政府可信地承诺履行其协议的能力。法院能够针对政府本身执行契约和产权，提供了一种可信的担保（pp. 816, 819）。这一机制尤其重要，因为它意味着即便政治部门想要违约，独立的法院体系也会成为障碍。

4. 可信的罢黜威胁。成功废黜 Charles I 以及后来的 James II，为任何未来从事不负责任行为的君主确立了一种可信的威胁。

5. 财政交易充当了共同利益。为换取更大的议会控制权，议会各方利益同意提供足够的税收收入。这消除了曾是王室掠夺行为主要动机的财政危机，并为 William III 提供了对法国作战的资源。因此这一安排是互利的且自我执行的。

作者大量借助二手历史来源来记录 Stuart 王朝君主具体的攫取行为，包括出售垄断专利权、1640 年查抄伦敦塔中的金银块、出售并滥发爵位、使用采买征用权（purveyance）、操纵监护法庭（court of wards），以及罢免法官（pp. 809–814）。他们还利用 Maitland、Keir、Hayek 等人的标准宪制史著作来记录革命安排（Revolution Settlement）的具体制度变革。

Methods

1. Pre-post comparison. The core research body is a comparison of institutional arrangements, government fiscal behavior, and capital market outcomes before and after the Glorious Revolution of 1688. The authors document the Crown’s inability to borrow, its repeated defaults, and the insecurity of property rights under the Stuarts (1603–1688), and then contrast this with the dramatic expansion of government borrowing, falling interest rates, and growth of private capital markets after 1688 (pp. 819–828). The logic is that if the institutional changes credibly secured property rights, we should observe a sharp improvement in capital market conditions at the time of the Revolution, which is exactly what the data show.

2. Process tracing. The authors trace the causal process from the Stuart monarchs’ fiscal pressures and institutional powers, through specific acts of expropriation, to the political opposition that led to the Civil War and eventually the Glorious Revolution, and then to the specific institutional innovations of the Revolution Settlement and their consequences for capital markets.

3. The historical narrative is structured by an explicit theoretical framework drawn from the new institutional economics, particularly Oliver Williamson’s work on credible commitments, transaction costs, and governance structures.

Assumptions that must hold for these methods to be valid:

(1) No other major changes occurred simultaneously with the Glorious Revolution that could independently explain the dramatic improvement in capital markets. The authors acknowledge but set aside the larger economic and religious context (p. 805).

(2) Capital market behavior is a reliable indicator of the perceived security of property rights, that lenders’ willingness to lend at lower rates reflects genuine belief in the government’s commitment, rather than other factors such as changes in the money supply, shifts in savings behavior, or exogenous economic growth.

1. 前后对比。核心研究主体是对 1688 年光荣革命前后制度安排、政府财政行为和资本市场结果的比较。作者记录了 Stuart 王朝治下（1603–1688）王室无力借款、其反复违约以及产权的不安全，然后将其与 1688 年之后政府借款的急剧扩张、利率的下降和私人资本市场的增长相对照（pp. 819–828）。其逻辑是：如果这些制度变革可信地保障了产权，我们就应当在革命之时观察到资本市场状况的急剧改善，而数据所显示的正是如此。

2. 过程追踪。作者追踪其因果过程：从 Stuart 王朝君主的财政压力和制度权力，经由具体的攫取行为，到导致内战并最终引发光荣革命的政治反对，再到革命安排的具体制度创新及其对资本市场的后果。

3. 这一历史叙事由一个明确的理论框架所结构化，该框架取自新制度经济学，尤其是 Oliver Williamson 关于可信承诺、交易成本和治理结构的著作。

为使这些方法有效，必须成立的假设：

（1）没有其他重大变化与光荣革命同时发生，以致能够独立地解释资本市场的急剧改善。作者承认但搁置了更大的经济和宗教背景（p. 805）。

（2）资本市场行为是产权被感知安全程度的可靠指标，即贷款人以较低利率放贷的意愿反映的是对政府承诺的真实信念，而非仅仅是诸如货币供给变化、储蓄行为转变或外生经济增长等其他因素。

Class Notes

Week 9 lecture: discussion of this reading

The Week 9 discussion reconstructed North and Weingast as a commitment problem. A citizen first decides whether to evade or comply. If the citizen complies, the ruler then chooses whether to exploit or provide public goods.

Citizen evades: payoff \((0,0)\).
Citizen complies: the ruler moves next.
Ruler action after complianceCitizen payoffRuler payoff
Exploit\(-T\)\(T\)
Provide public goods\(V-T\)\(T-G\)

With \(V>T\) and \(G>0\), the one-shot equilibrium is evasion: once the citizen has paid, the ruler prefers \(T\) to \(T-G\), so the citizen anticipates exploitation and evades.

The Glorious Revolution is modeled as adding an institutional penalty \(P\) to exploitation:

\[ \text{exploit payoff}=T-P,\qquad \text{provide payoff}=T-G. \]

If \(P>G\), the ruler prefers provision, and the fiscal contract equilibrium becomes \((\text{Comply},\text{Provide})=(V-T,T-G)\).

The instructor then mapped Levi's framework onto this game: relative bargaining power can raise \(P\); transaction costs shift the cost of provision \(G\) or other parameters; and the discount factor can sustain cooperation in a repeated game even without a formal penalty.

\[ \frac{T-G}{1-\beta}>T \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad T-G>T(1-\beta) \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \beta>\frac{G}{T}. \]

When \(\beta\) is high, cooperation can be sustained by future punishment. When fiscal pressure and war shorten the ruler's horizon, the institutional penalty \(P\) becomes necessary.

第9周课堂将 North and Weingast 重构为一个承诺问题。公民先选择逃税或纳税；如果公民纳税，统治者再选择掠夺或提供公共品。

公民逃税：收益为 \((0,0)\)。
公民纳税：统治者继续行动。
纳税后统治者行动公民收益统治者收益
掠夺\(-T\)\(T\)
提供公共品\(V-T\)\(T-G\)

在 \(V>T\)、\(G>0\) 时，单次博弈均衡是逃税：公民一旦纳税，统治者会偏好 \(T\) 而非 \(T-G\)，所以公民预期会被掠夺并选择逃税。

光荣革命后的制度变迁可以被模型化为对掠夺加入惩罚 \(P\)：

\[ \text{掠夺收益}=T-P,\qquad \text{提供收益}=T-G. \]

若 \(P>G\)，统治者会选择提供公共品，财政契约均衡变为 \((\text{纳税},\text{提供})=(V-T,T-G)\)。

老师随后将 Levi 的框架映射到这个博弈中：相对议价能力可以提高 \(P\)；交易成本会移动提供公共品的成本 \(G\) 或其他参数；贴现因子则可能在重复博弈中维持合作，即使没有正式制度惩罚。

\[ \frac{T-G}{1-\beta}>T \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad T-G>T(1-\beta) \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \beta>\frac{G}{T}. \]

当 \(\beta\) 高时，合作可由未来惩罚维持；当战争和财政压力缩短统治者时间视野时，制度性惩罚 \(P\) 就变得必要。

Shared week context: course framing & four-reading synthesis

This week compares four classic or synthetic accounts of limited government, fiscal contracts, and tax compliance. The shared premise is that a ruler wants revenue, but sustainable revenue requires solving constraints that pure coercion cannot cheaply solve.

\[ \max R=(\text{tax revenue})-(\text{collection costs}) \quad\text{s.t.}\quad \{\text{bargaining power},\text{ transaction costs},\text{ discount factor}\}. \]

Levi (1988)

Starts with the ruler as revenue maximizer, then adds quasi-voluntary compliance. Compliance is more than coercion; it combines perceived fairness, coordination among taxpayers, and the ruler's credibility.

North & Weingast (1989)

Turns the problem into a commitment game. Institutions impose a penalty \(P\) on exploitation, making provision credible when \(P>G\). Levi's bargaining power can be read as one source of \(P\).

Stasavage (2011)

Endogenizes the penalty parameter: \(P=f(A,M)\), where \(A\) is alignment between creditors and representatives and \(M\) is monitoring capacity. The effect is strongest in small, creditor-controlled polities.

Gehlbach (2008)

Flips the fiscal-contract logic. The government may support taxable sectors without voluntary compliance: it feeds sectors that are easy to tax. Taxability works like a transaction-cost/legibility condition.

The theoretical map is therefore not a simple sequence from coercion to legitimacy. It is a set of alternative ways to make revenue extraction sustainable: quasi-voluntary compliance, institutional penalties, endogenous representative constraints, or coercive support for legible taxable sectors.

\[ \frac{T-G}{1-\beta}>T\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \beta>\frac{G}{T}. \]

The repeated-game result shows where Levi and Olson connect to North and Weingast: patient rulers may sustain cooperation even without formal institutions, but when time horizons shorten, institutional penalties become more important.

本周四篇文献共同讨论有限政府、财政契约与税收合规。共同前提是：统治者想要收入，但可持续收入不能只靠昂贵的强制，还必须解决若干约束。

\[ \max R=(\text{税收收入})-(\text{征税成本}) \quad\text{s.t.}\quad \{\text{议价能力},\text{ 交易成本},\text{ 贴现因子}\}. \]

Levi (1988)

以“统治者最大化收入”为起点，随后加入准自愿合规。合规不只是强制，而是由公平感、纳税人之间的协调、以及统治者可信承诺共同支撑。

North & Weingast (1989)

将问题转写为承诺博弈。制度对掠夺施加惩罚 \(P\)，当 \(P>G\) 时，提供公共品才可信。Levi 的议价能力可以理解为 \(P\) 的来源之一。

Stasavage (2011)

将惩罚参数内生化：\(P=f(A,M)\)，其中 \(A\) 是债权人与代表者的利益一致性，\(M\) 是监控能力。小规模、债权人控制议会的政体中效果最强。

Gehlbach (2008)

翻转财政契约逻辑。政府可以在没有自愿合规的情况下扶持可税部门：它“喂养”容易征税的部门。可税性相当于一种交易成本/可读性条件。

因此，这张理论图谱不是从强制走向合法性的单线进步史，而是展示几种让汲取可持续的替代路径：准自愿合规、制度性惩罚、内生代表约束，或对可读可税部门的强制性扶持。

\[ \frac{T-G}{1-\beta}>T\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \beta>\frac{G}{T}. \]

重复博弈结果说明 Levi/Olson 与 North-Weingast 的连接点：有耐心的统治者即使没有正式制度，也可能维持合作；但当时间视野变短，制度性惩罚就变得更关键。
